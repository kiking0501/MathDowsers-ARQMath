Is there a geometric interpretation for the geometric mean of multiple numbers?

Given a list of n nonnegative real numbers a1,a2,…,an, the geometric mean of that list is defined to be a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢ann.

In the case of n=2, there are a few standard "geometric" interpretations of the geometric mean (generally involving power of a point, to visualize how the length a1⁢a2 depends on the lengths a1 and a2).

For the case of n≥3, are there are also nice geometric ways of understanding what the geometric mean measures? If so, what are they?

It's the side of an n-cube whose n-dimensional volume is equal to that of an n-box with sides a1,,…,an
