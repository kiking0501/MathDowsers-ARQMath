Let An:={x:|f⁢(x)|≥n}; we may write μ⁢(An)=∫XχAn⁢(x) where X is the entire measure space. Then n⁢An=∫Xn⁢χAn⁢(x). But now by definition of An we have that

n⁢χAn⁢(x)≤|f⁢(x)|

for every x∈X. Indeed, fix an n∈ℕ and an x∈X. If x∉An then n⁢χAn⁢(x)=0 which is certainly less than or equal to |f⁢(x)|. If x∈An, n⁢χAn⁢(x)=n≤|f⁢(x)| by definition of An. Thus we may apply the DCT (since f is integrable) to get that

limn→∞⁡n⁢μ⁢(An)=limn→∞⁡∫Xn⁢χAn⁢(x)=∫Xlimn→∞⁡n⁢χAn⁢(x)=0.
