Prove that ∫0+∞ln⁡(1+x2)1+x2⁢𝑑x=π⁢ln⁡(2) Tip: Use Leibniz's formula with F⁢(λ)=∫0+∞ln⁡(1+λ⁢x2)1+x2⁢𝑑x,λ≥0

Note that F⁢(0)=0 and that\begin{align}F'(\lambda)&=\int_0^{+\infty}\frac\partial{\partial\lambda}\frac{\ln(1+\lambda x^2)}{1+x^2}\,\mathrm dx\\&=\int_0^{+\infty}\frac1{\lambda-1}\frac1{x^2+1}-\frac1{\lambda-1}\frac1{\lambda x^2+1}\,\mathrm dx\\&=\frac\pi{2\sqrt{\lambda}\left(\sqrt\lambda+1\right)}\end{align}Therefore F⁢(λ)=π⁢log⁡(λ+1) and, in particular, F⁢(1)=π⁢log⁡2.
