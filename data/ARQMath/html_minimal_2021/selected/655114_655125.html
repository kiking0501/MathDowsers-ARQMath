Let D={|z|<1} be the open unit disk. We will prove:

Theorem: All biholomorphic maps f:D→D have the form f⁢(z)=α⁢z-a1-a¯⁢z for some α∈∂⁡D and a∈D.

Proof: The first observation is that

ga⁢(z)=z-a1-a¯⁢z where a∈D, maps D biholomorphically onto D. Indeed, ga is holomorphic in D and for |z|=1 we have

|ga⁢(z)|=|z-a||1-a¯⁢z|=|z¯-a¯||1-a¯⁢z|=|z⁢z¯⏞=1-a¯⁢z||1-a¯⁢z|=1

Therefore |ga⁢(z)|=1 for |z|=1. By the maximum principle we conclude |ga⁢(z)|<1 for |z|<1. Thus ga maps D on D. It is bijective since the inverse map is given by

ga-1⁢(z)=z+a1+a¯⁢z

The second observation is the following surprising lemma:

Lemma: Let f:D→D be biholomorphic with f⁢(0)=0. Then f⁢(z)=α⁢z for some α∈∂⁡D.

Proof: Setting g⁢(z)=f⁢(z)/z we obtain again a holomorphic function on D (it is holomorphic at 0, since f⁢(0)=0). For |z|=r<1 we have |g⁢(z)|=|f⁢(z)|≤1/r.

By the maximum principle (this is the key point!) it follows that |g⁢(z)|≤1/r for all |z|≤r (not just |z|=r). Letting r→1- we see that |g⁢(z)|≤1 for all z∈D. Therefore we have shown

|f⁢(z)|≤|z| for all z∈D.

Since f is biholomorphic, the same argument applies to f-1, showing that also

|f-1⁢(z)|≤|z|

for all z∈D. But that implies |z|=|f-1⁢(f⁢(z))|≤|f⁢(z)|. Combined with (1) this gives |f⁢(z)|=|z|, i.e. |g⁢(z)|=1. But that means that g attains its maximum in the open unit disk, so by the maximum principle it must be constant: g⁢(z)=α for some α with |α|=1. With other words

f⁢(z)=α⁢z

for z∈D. □

Now we can put both together to finish the proof. Let f:D→D be a biholomorphic map. Set a=f-1⁢(0). Then h=f∘ga-1 is also a biholomorphic map D→D and we have

h⁢(0)=f⁢(ga-1⁢(0))=f⁢(f-1⁢(0))=0

By the lemma, h⁢(z)=α⁢z for some α∈∂⁡D. But that means

f⁢(z)=h∘ga⁢(z)=α⁢z-a1-a¯⁢z

which is the claim. □
