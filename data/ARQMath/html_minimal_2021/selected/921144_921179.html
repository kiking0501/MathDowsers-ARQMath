Did you read the examples on the Wikipedia page for uniform continuity? There is an example there.
On ℝ2, take f⁢(x)=1/x on (0,∞).

Generally for continuity when we write δ,we mean that δ=δ⁢(ϵ,x0),x0∈D. Similarly for uniform continuity we mean δ=δ⁢(ϵ). This notation is consistent. It is taken for granted that we understand the situation in which δ is referred.

Now for the example :
f⁢(x)=x2 in ℝ is continuous but not uniformly continuous. But f⁢(x)=x is uniformly continuous in ℝ.

Note that : Uniform continuity ⟹ continuity, but converse is not true.

Another example is e⁢x⁢p⁢(x): it is everywhere continuous, but not uniformly continuous on an interval [a,∞].
