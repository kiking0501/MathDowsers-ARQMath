The question is to prove that every element of (Z/72⁢Z)× has order dividing 12, somehow using Euler's theorem to first reach the fact that every element has order dividing 24, and then proceeding from there. I am very confused; I can't see how Euler's theorem/phi function are to be used here.

since ϕ⁢(72)=24 you know that a24=1 for all the elements of (Z/72⁢Z)×. Then you can use the fact that the least number satisfying ak=1 is the order of a to conclude that the order divides 24. From here you just need to rule out the 24 case.

Hint: By the Chinese remainder theorem, (ℤ/72⁢ℤ)×≅(ℤ/8)××(ℤ/9)×≅C4×C6 and l⁢c⁢m⁢(4,6)=12.
