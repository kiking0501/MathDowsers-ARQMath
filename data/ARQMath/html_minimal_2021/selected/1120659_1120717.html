Here it is a nice geometric proof. Let A⁢B⁢C⁢D⁢E a regular pentagon, and F a point on A⁢C such that A⁢F=A⁢B. By angle chasing, we have that C⁢F⁢B is similar to A⁢B⁢C, hence: A⁢CA⁢B=1+C⁢FA⁢B=1+C⁢FC⁢B=1+A⁢BA⁢C, giving A⁢CA⁢B=ϕ. By applying the sine theorem to the triangle A⁢B⁢C and the sine duplication formula we easily prove our claim.
