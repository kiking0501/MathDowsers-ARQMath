I'm working through the following question:

Question Reference: Oxford Part I Paper B2 2003

Find the monic polynomial f∈ℤ⁢[X] whose roots are the complex primitive 12th roots of unity. Let p be a prime and 𝔽p be the field with p elements. Let fp be the image of f under the (unique) ring homomorphism ℤ→𝔽p.

Claim 1: fp is reducible

Claim 2: f is irreducible in ℚ⁢[X]

Progress:

Am I missing something here? Are their any results that govern the properties of a polynomial under this map?

One can first consider the primes p=2,3 that divide 12 separately. For p=2 the reduction is f¯=X4+X2+1=(X2+X+1)2, and for p=3 it is f¯=X4+2⁢X2+1=(X2+1)2, so both are reducible. For a primep that does not divide12, the polynomial x12-1 is separable over 𝔽p (its roots in some extension field are all distinct), and so is therefore f¯ which divides it. If α is a root off¯, then other roots in 𝔽p⁢(α) can be obtained from it by iterating the Frobenius automorphism x↦xp of that field, and taking the product of factors X-β where β runs through the orbit of α, one obtains a polynomial μ∈\Fp⁢[X] (the minimal polynomial ofα) that dividesf¯. The fact that this is a proper divisor come from the circumstance that in fact αp2=α, so that the size of the orbit and the degree ofμ are2. This is because α12=1 and every invertible elementx of ℤ/12⁢ℤ satisfies x2=1 as is easily checked; the class ofp being such an element one therefore has p2≡1(mod12) and αp2=α1.

This gives your first claim. The second is a special case of the known fact that all cyclotomic polynomials are irreducible overℚ, as is proved for instance here; this in fact uses reduction modulop for many primesp, and arguments similar but not identical to the ones above.
