The point (8,0) is rotated about the origin counterclockwise by 38 degrees. Determine the coordinates of the image.

geometry trigonometry analytic-geometry rotations
  • The new point is at a distance r=8 away from the origin.
  • The new point is at an angle θ=38∘ counterclockwise from the positive x-axis.

In general, if you know these two values (r and θ) of a point, the coordinates of that point are x=r⁢cos⁡θ, and y=r⁢sin⁡θ. You can see this by drawing a triangle with hypotenuse going from (0,0) to the given point.
