@Micah: Your comment pointed in the right direction; see my answer.

Micah already pointed the way in a comment: The identity

tan⁡a⁢tan⁡b=tan⁡a-tan⁡btan⁡(a-b)-1

causes the inner sum to telescope. To make full use of this, let's extend the inner sum to k=2⁢n:

∑l=1n∑k=12⁢ntan⁡l⁢k⁢π2⁢n+1⁢tan⁡l⁢(k+1)⁢π2⁢n+1amp;=∑l=1n∑k=12⁢n(tan⁡l⁢(k+1)⁢π2⁢n+1-tan⁡l⁢k⁢π2⁢n+1tan⁡l⁢π2⁢n+1-1)amp;=∑l=1n(tan⁡l⁢(2⁢n+1)⁢π2⁢n+1-tan⁡l⁢π2⁢n+1tan⁡l⁢π2⁢n+1-2n)amp;=∑l=1n(0-1-2n)amp;=-n(2n+1).

This sum contains each of the terms we want to sum twice, with mirror symmetry, and it contains one additional term in the middle for k=n. Thus, for our sum to vanish, we need

∑l=1ntan⁡l⁢n⁢π2⁢n+1⁢tan⁡l⁢(n+1)⁢π2⁢n+1=-n⁢(2⁢n+1).

The arguments of the two factors add to l⁢π, so they're negatives of each other, so we're looking for

-∑l=1ntan2⁡l⁢n⁢π2⁢n+1.

We can again extend the sum to 2⁢n to double it, since the arguments form pairs that add up to n⁢π; then, since n and 2⁢n+1 are coprime, we can replace l⁢n by l while traversing the same arguments; and then we can set the upper limit back to n, since the arguments still add up to π in pairs. Thus, what we need is

∑l=1ntan2⁡l⁢π2⁢n+1=n⁢(2⁢n+1).

How to find this sum is shown at Prove that ∑k=1n-1tan2⁡k⁢π2⁢n=(n-1)⁢(2⁢n-1)3. Adapting the argument in the accepted answer there for our odd denominator, we obtain

(cos⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1+i⁢sin⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1)2⁢n+1=(-1)k,

taking the imaginary part,

∑r=0n(2⁢n+12⁢r+1)⁢(cos⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1)2⁢n-2⁢r⁢(i⁢sin⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1)2⁢r+1=0,

dividing by (cos⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1)2⁢n+1,

∑r=0n(2⁢n+12⁢r+1)⁢(i⁢tan⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1)2⁢r+1=0,

and dividing by tan⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1 and letting x=tan2⁡k⁢π2⁢n+1,

∑r=0n(2⁢n+12⁢r+1)⁢(-x)r=0.

Then Vieta's formula shows that the sum of the roots of this equation is

(2⁢n+12)(2⁢n+10)=n⁢(2⁢n+1),

as required.

[Update:]

This answer suggests an alternative, more elementary way to calculate the sum of the squares of the tangents: On the grid l⁢π2⁢n+1, the tangent decomposes into n mutually orthogonal sines, each of whose dot product with itself is 2⁢n+1, so the dot product of the tangent with itself is n⁢(2⁢n+1).
