Since your sum starts with i=1 the first step of induction is to prove it is true for n=1 and not 0
Although the sum starts with i=1, it must be proved for all n \in \mathbb{N}. We include zero in the natural numbers here..

Try the following \begin{align} 1-\frac{1}{2^n}+\frac{1}{2^{n+1}}=1-(\frac{1}{2^n}-\frac{1}{2^{n+1}}) =1-(\frac{2}{2^{n+1}}-\frac{1}{2^{n+1}})=1-\frac{2-1}{2^{n+1}}=1-\frac{1}{2^{n+1}} \end{align}
