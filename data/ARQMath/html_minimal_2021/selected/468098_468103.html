You could use 19! instead of 20!

For every natural number n, if k≤n is a natural number, then k∣n!+k (simply since k∣n!). In particular, if 1<k≤n, then n!+k is not prime. It follows that all of n!+2,n!+3,⋯,n!+n are composite. Thus, for every n you can find a list of n-1 consecutive composite numbers.

This should explain how the result you quote was arrived at (you just need to notice that 20!+21 is also composite.
