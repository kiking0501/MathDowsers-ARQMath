Let n be the largest integer that divides p4-1 for all prime p≥7.

We have 114-1=14640 and 74-1=2400. The g⁢c⁢d of 14640 and 2400 is 240. So n≤240. If p is odd then modulo 16 we have p4∈{(±1)4,(±3)4,(±5)4,(±7)4}={12,92,252,492}={12,92,92,12}= ={1,81,81,1}={1}.

If p is not divisible by 3 then modulo 3 we have p4∈{(±1)4}={1}.

If p is not divisible by 5 then modulo 5 we have p4∈{(±1)4,(±2)4}={1,16}={1}.

So for any integer p that is not divisible by 2,3, or 5 we have p4≡1(mod16) and p4≡1(mod3) and p4≡1(mod5); and since 16,3, and 5 are pair-wise co-prime, therefore p4≡1(mod16⋅3⋅5)=240, so n≥240.
