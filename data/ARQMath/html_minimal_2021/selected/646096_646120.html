Hint. First note that sin2⁡x=12⁢Re⁢(1-e2⁢x⁢i), and hence your integral equals 14⁢∫-∞∞1-e2⁢x⁢ix2⁢𝑑x Then define the curve γε,R to be the union of:

  1. γR⁢(t)=R⁢ei⁢t,t∈[0,π].

  2. γ-⁢(t)=t,t∈[-R,ε],

  3. γε⁢(π-t),t∈[0,π],

  4. γ+⁢(t)=t,t∈[ε,R].

Then use Residue Theorem (here the function DOES NOT have poles in the interior of γε,R), and let ε→0, R→∞.
