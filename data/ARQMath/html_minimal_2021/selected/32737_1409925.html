Let d=(a+b,a-b) (Recall that d>0 by definition.

From lemma 3, we can write

d=(2⁢a,a-b) d=(2⁢b,a-b)

Since d is the greatest common divisor of 2⁢a, a-b, and 2⁢b, it follows that d also divides each of them. In particular, d∣2⁢a and d∣2⁢b. Hence, from lemma 2, we know that d∣(2⁢a⁢m+2⁢b⁢n) for arbitrary integers m and n.

By lemma 1, we can translate d∣(2⁢a⁢m+2⁢b⁢n) into the inequality

d≤2⁢(a⁢m+b⁢n)

Since equation (2) is true for every integer m and n, we can allow m and n to represent particular integers by universal instantiation*

In our case, let m=x and n=y. Then a⁢m+b⁢n=a⁢x+b⁢y, which allows us to conclude from (1) that a⁢m+b⁢n=1.

Therefore, we substitute a⁢m+b⁢n=1 into (2) to conclude that

d≤2

which implies that d=1 or d=2.
