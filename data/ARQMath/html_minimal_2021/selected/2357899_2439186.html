I want to prove that the cartesian product of a finite amount of countable sets is countable. I can use that the union of countable sets is countable.

My attempt:

Let A1,A2,…,An be countable sets.

We prove the statement by induction on n

For n=1, the statement clearly holds, as A1 is countable. Now, suppose that B:=A1×A2×…⁢An-1 is countable.

We have: B×An={(b,a)|b∈B,a∈An} =⋃a∈An{(b,a)|b∈B}

and {(b,a)|b∈B} is countable for a fixed a∈An, since the function fa:B→B×{a}:b→(b,a) is a bijection, and B is countable by induction hypothesis. Because the union of countable sets remains countable, we have proven that (A1×…⁢An-1)×An is countable, and because f:(A1×…⁢An-1)×B→A1×…⁢An-1×An:((a1,…,an-1),an)↦(a1,…,an-1,an) is a bijection, the result follows.
