Basically lim supn→∞=-∞ means that there is a subsequence {xn}n≥k with supremum smaller than any M∈ℤ. Therefore every xn must become arbitrarily small.

By definition, lim supn→∞⁡xn=limn→∞⁡sup⁡{xn,xn+1,…}, and xn≤sup⁡{xn,xn+1,…}.

If xn≤sup⁡{xn,xn+1,…} then lim⁡xn≤lim⁡sup⁡{xn,xn+1,…}?
