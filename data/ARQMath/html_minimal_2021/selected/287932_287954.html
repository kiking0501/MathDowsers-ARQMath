Is the reverse implication false?
@michel Yes, it is not true in general.

By definition of limit, for each ε>0 there exists N s.t. n>N⟹||an+1an|-L|<ε. So |an|=|an||an-1|⁢⋯⁢|aN+1||aN|⁢|aN|<(L+ε)n-N⁢|aN| Take the nth root of both sides of the inequality. Then we get |an|n<(L+ε)1-N/n⁢|aN|n. Taking n→∞ then limn→∞⁡|an|n≤L+ε. Since ε is arbitrary, we get limn→∞⁡|an|n≤L. Likewise we can get limn→∞⁡|an|n≥L.
