linear-algebra eigenvalues-eigenvectors

The geometric multiplicity of an eigenvalue λ for the n×n matrix A is, by definition, the dimension of the subspace EA⁢(λ)={v∈Kn:A⁢v=λ⁢v} where K is the base field, in your case probably ℝ or ℂ, and the elements of Kn are column vectors.

This subspace is just the null space (also known as kernel) of the matrix A-λ⁢In, because A⁢v=λ⁢v if and only if A⁢v=λ⁢In⁢v if and only if (A-λ⁢In)⁢v=0

The dimension of this subspace is computed easily: dim⁡EA⁢(λ)=dim⁡N⁢(A-λ⁢In)=n-rank⁡(A-λ⁢In) by the rank-nullity theorem.

The case of λ=0 is no different: dim⁡EA⁢(0)=dim⁡N⁢(A-0⁢In)=dim⁡N⁢(A)=n-rank⁡(A) and in your case n=5 and rank⁡(A)=1.
