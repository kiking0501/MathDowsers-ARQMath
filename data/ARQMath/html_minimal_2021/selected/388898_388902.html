Since sn→+∞ when n→∞, for each n⁢\geqslant⁢1 there exists some finite m⁢\gt⁢n such that sm⁢\geqslant⁢2⁢sn. In particular, ∑k=n+1maksk⁢\geqslant⁢∑k=n+1maksm=1-snsm⁢\geqslant⁢1/2. Thus, the rests of the series ∑kaksk do not converge to zero, QED.
