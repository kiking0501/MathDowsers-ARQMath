Basic Epsilon-N proof clarification

It's been a couple years since I've done analysis, so I was hoping someone could point out any possible flaws I have in the following proof.

Prove limn→∞⁡2nn!=0.

For n>2, we have: limn→∞⁡2nn!=limn→∞⁡222!×23×24×25×⋯×2n.

Need to show that 222!×23×24×25×⋯→0. In other words, need to show that limn→∞⁡2n=0:

∀ϵ∈ℕ+,∃N∈ℕ such that ∀n>N we have 2n<ϵ. Take N=2ϵ. Since n>N we have n>2ϵ. Thus 2n<ϵ. Therefore limn→∞⁡2n=0.

And so limn→∞⁡222!×23×24×25×…×2n=0.

Then limn→∞⁡2nn!=0.

A couple of things: first of all, writing limn→∞2n=0 ⇒limn→∞222!×23×24×25×…×2n=0 isn't completely rigorous, unless you observe that all the factors before 2n are &⁢l⁢t;1 (except for the first, which is 2), which implies that their product is too. Thus you can write: 0<222!×23×24×25×…×2n<2⋅1⋅2n→0 because, as you have proved, 2/n→0. Whithout this observation the statement isn't justified, since all the accumulating factors could have resulted in an unbounded quantity.
Also another minor consideration: in your proof that limn→∞⁡2n=0 your N should be a natural number, such as N=[2ϵ]+1.
