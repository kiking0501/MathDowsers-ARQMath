When does the curve y=xpcosπx(0<x\leqslant1) have finite length?

When does the curve y=xp⁢cos⁡πx⁢(0<x⁢\leqslant⁢1) have finite length? I cannot figure out how to solve this problem. Thanks for your help.

calculus
See [arc length](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arc_length).

You need to integrate the arc-length d⁢s through the curve, i.e., L=∫01𝑑s. The arc-length is found by the formula

d⁢s=1+(d⁢yd⁢x)2⁢d⁢x. Summarizing, you have to consider y=f⁢(x), and then compute the integral ∫011+f′⁢(x)2⁢𝑑x, and check when is it convergent in terms of p.
