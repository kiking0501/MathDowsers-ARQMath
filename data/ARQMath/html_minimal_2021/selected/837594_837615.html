The binomial expansion is given by \begin{align} (1+x)^{n} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} x^{k}. \end{align} Differentiating both sides with respect to x yields \begin{align} n (1+x)^{n-1} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} k \ \binom{n}{k} x^{k-1}. \end{align} Multiplying this last expression by 3n⁢4-1 leads to \begin{align} \frac{3^{n} n}{4} \ x(1+x)^{n-1} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} k \ \binom{n}{k} 4^{-1} 3^{n} x^{k}. \end{align} Now let x=4/3 to obtain the desired expression \begin{align} n \cdot 7^{n-1} = \sum_{k=0}^{n} k \ \binom{n}{k} 4^{k-1} 3^{n-k}. \end{align}
