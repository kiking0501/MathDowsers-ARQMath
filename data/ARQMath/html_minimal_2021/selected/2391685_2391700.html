One can speak about the continuity or discontinuity of a function only when the domain and the codomain of the function have a topology. When one of the set is ℝ and nothing is said about the topology, it is always the usual topology.

We denote d⁢(x,A):=inf⁡{d⁢(x,a)∣a∈A}. It will be useful to first show that any x,y∈X satisfy d⁢(x,A)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,A). To see this, note that by the triangle inequality we have d⁢(x,A)≤d⁢(x,a)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,a),a∈A. Now taking the infimum over a∈A yields the desired inequality.

Now let ε>0 and choose δ:=ε. Then, if d⁢(x,y)<δ, we have by our inequality that |f⁢(x)-f⁢(y)|=|d⁢(x,A)-d⁢(y,A)|≤d⁢(x,y)<ε. This completes the proof.
