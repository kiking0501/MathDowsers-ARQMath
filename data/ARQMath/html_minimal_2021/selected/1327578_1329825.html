Your sandwich is tastily correct.

Let n>3 so that log⁡n>1 and we then have the inequality 1<log⁡n<n for all positive integers n>3. Thus we get 1<(log⁡n)1/n<n1/n and we know that n1/n→1 as n→∞ hence by Squeeze theorem (log⁡n)1/n→1 as n→∞.

There are many ways to show that n1/n→1. For example we can set n1/n=1+h so that (via Binomial Theorem) n=(1+h)n>n⁢(n-1)2⁢h2 or 0<h2<2n-1 and hence h2→0 as n→∞. Since h>0 this means that h→0 and hence n1/n=1+h→1 as n→∞.
