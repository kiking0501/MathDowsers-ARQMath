The equation 2n-7=x2 is called the Ramanujan–Nagell equation. It has been conjectured by Ramanujan and proven by Nagell, and later others, that the only solutions are n=3,4,5,7 and 15. Here are two proofs:

  • one originally due to Hasse
  • another by Wells Johnson

I believe all proofs make use of unique factorization in the ring of integers of ℚ⁢(-7).

Find all the solutions of x2+7=2n.
