Notice that Sn and P2 are (topological) manifolds. Since they are homeomorphic to an euclidean space locally, they are locally path-connected. Of course P2 (ℝ⁢P2 and ℂ⁢P2) is path-connected. The most difficult part, in my opinion, is that π1⁢(X) is finite, but you already knew it.

First, 𝕊n is locally path connected, for any neighborhood of a point of 𝕊n contains an ε-ball of the point, which is homeomorphic to ℝn.

Second, P2 is path connected, for it is the image of 𝕊2 under a continuous (quotient) map.

Third, P2 is locally path connected. This is because any neighborhood of a point contains a neighborhood on which the preimage of the aforementioned quotient map is homeomorphic to two copies of ℝ2, hence is homeomorphic to ℝ2.
