analysis continuity epsilon-delta

For the second function, recalling that f⁢(0)=0 and that f⁢(x)≥0 since 1+x2≥1, you pick ε>0 and start with the inequality |f⁢(x)-f⁢(0)|<ε, namely log⁡(1+x2)<ε. Since log is a strictly increasing function, your last inequality is equivalent to 1+x2<eε. Hence -eε-1<x<eε-1, and you can define δ=eε-1>0.
