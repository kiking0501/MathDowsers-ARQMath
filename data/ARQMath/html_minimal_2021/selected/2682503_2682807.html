Suppose you want

1n⁢∥A∥∞=∥A∥2=m⁢∥A∥∞

Then we have ∥A∥∞=m⁢n⁢∥A∥∞ and since ∥A∥∞>0 if A≠0, we have m⁢n=1, that is m=n=1.

When m=n=1, the equality holds.

Also, when m≠n, then the equality doesn't hold if A≠0.
