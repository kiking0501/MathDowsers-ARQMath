The main thing that "integral domain" gives you is that If x⁢y=0 then x=0 or y=0. To make use of this it would seem helpful to have "=0" statements so to change the question to

Prove that in every integral domain If a2-b2=0 then a-b=0 or a+b=0. It seems the best chance is to use the defining property with x=a-b and y=a+b. Since you already have x and y you don't have to factor, you just have to multiply.

Had the question been merely "When is a2=b2 in an Integral Domain?" One would have to first get to a2-b2=0 and then factor (although knowing the solution a=b does suggest the factor a-b.)

It is true that, for this line of reasoning, you have to be thinking of Integral Domain in this way... As far as the definitions, cancellation is the first property below and the rest are equivalent

  • x⁢A=x⁢B and x≠0 implies A=B.
  • x⁢(A-B)=0 and x≠0 implies A-B=0.
  • x⁢y=0 and x≠0 implies y=0.
  • x⁢y=0 implies x=0 or y=0.

(To a constructive mathematician the last is not equivalent... but that is another story.)

Sometimes the first form is more useful and sometimes, as in this case, the last form is more useful.
