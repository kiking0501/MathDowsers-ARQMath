Following the hint by Hagen von Eitzen, we can show that, if there is a z0 such that f′⁢(z0)=0, then f is not injective. Consider a small circular path γ around z0. By Rouche's theorem, the winding number of f⁢(γ) around f⁢(z0) is the order of the zero z0 of f⁢(z)-f⁢(z0) which, by inspecting its Taylor series at z=z0, is greater than 1. So f⁢(γ) must cross itself somewhere, making f not injective. By contraposition, f′ is never 0. Now we have that f-1⁢(z) is holomorphic because it's differentiable and \begin{eqnarray} \frac{df^{-1}(z)}{dz}=\frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(z))}\end{eqnarray}
