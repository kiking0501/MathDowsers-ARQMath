Perhaps I should say that the rules of the game suggest strongly that you explain how much you already know in Galois Theory/Field Theory, what you’ve tried, and how far you’ve gotten so far.

(i) Suppose F⊂E2⊂L and suppose α∈L. Can you show that [E2(α):E2]≤[F(α):F]?

[Hint: [F⁢(α):F] is the degree of the minimal polynomial of α over F, and [E2⁢(α):E2] is the degree of the minimal polynomial of α over E2. How are the degrees of these minimal polynomials related?]

(ii) Let E1,E2 be finite extensions of F, all contained inside L. Convince yourself that E1=F⁢(α1,…,αn) for certain α1,…,αn∈L. Convince yourself that E1⁢E2=E2⁢(α1,…,αn). Can you use the result of (i) inductively to show that [E1E2:E2]≤[E1:F]?

[Of course, a similar argument gives [E1E2:E1]≤[E2:F].]

(iii) Apply the tower law to the inclusions F⊂E1⊂E1⁢E2 and F⊂E2⊂E1⁢E2. Use the fact that [E1:F] and [E2:F] are relatively prime, together with the result from (ii), to show that [E1E2:E2]=[E1:F] and [E1E2:F]=[E1:F][E2:F].
