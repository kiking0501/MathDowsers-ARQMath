∑k=3n(k2-3)=∑k=3nk2-∑k=3n3 =n⁢(n+1)⁢(2⁢n+1)6-3⁢n

However, I know the last term is incorrect, as the answer is given as

n⁢(n+1)⁢(2⁢n+1)6-3⁢n+1

Knowing the identity of ∑i=1n3=3⁢n, how do I arrive at the last term?

Using the identity ∑i=1ni2=n⁢(n+1)⁢(2⁢n+1)6, gives 1+4+∑i=3ni2=n⁢(n+1)⁢(2⁢n+1)6. and hence ∑i=3ni2=n⁢(n+1)⁢(2⁢n+1)6-5. We also have ∑i=3n3=3⁢n-6. Substracting the one of these from the other gives the desired answer.
