How to show the determinant of a matrix is zero for which the column elements sum to zero

It is given that the sum of the elements of each column of a N×N square matrix A is zero. How can I show that the determinant is zero without specializing to N=2 or N=3?

You can observe that last row would be linear combination of other rows, and hence det must be zero.
A similar question had been asked sometime back where the sum of elements of each row was a constant. Then thanks to a well known theorem, that constant must be an eigenvalue corresponding to the eigenvector of all ones.
