How to solve equations in the form f⁢(x)=x!?

Preliminary Google's grant nothing, so I'll pose it here:

How may I go about solving equations for the independent when in the form f⁢(x)=x! ?

x! being the factorial of x, i.e.: 4!=4×3×2×1

I'm aware of factorial approximation methods such as Stirling's approximation; x!≈2⁢π⁢x⋅(xe)x; but am aware of no such method to solve for x itself in the natural form.

Thoughts?

see [here](http://mathoverflow.net/questions/12828/inverse-gamma-function)

The factorial extends continuously onto the real numbers via a function called the gamma function, which remarkably has the same values at the integers as the factorial does with a small shift : Γ⁢(n)=(n-1)!. This permits us to not only look for integer, but also real number solutions for this problem. I suggest you look up this remarkable function on Wikipedia.
