It's not quite right to say that the square and the square root cancel each other (in fact, this example demonstrates that nicely!)

For this problem, we have (-3)2=9=3. But why are these the right two steps? Because we evaluate functions from the inside out. Thus, the first step is to square the -3. The second step is a matter of convention: the square root of a positive number is taken to be the positive root (one could choose the negative root, but that would be a different function...)
