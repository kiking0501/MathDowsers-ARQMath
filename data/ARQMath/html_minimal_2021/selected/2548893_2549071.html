Clearly, if V and W are two (possibly infinite-dimensiional) vector spaces and f:V→W is multiplicative but not additive, we must have dim⁡V≥2 and dim⁡W≥1.

Suppose this dimension constraint is indeed satisfied. Pick any two nonzero vectors v∈V and w∈W and define f by f⁢(c⁢v)=c⁢w for any scalar c and f⁢(u)=0 for any u∉span⁡(v). Then f is multiplicative by construction, but it is not additive. In fact, if u∉span⁡(v), we have f⁢((v-u)+u)=f⁢(v)=w≠0+0=f⁢(v-u)+f⁢(u).
