In general, if a number has k divisors, what's the maximum number of prime divisors that it could have?

Every integer can be written as the product of different primes raised to some power greater than or equal to 1, for example x=p1n1⁢p2n2⁢…⁢pmnm, with all nj≥1. The number of divisors is k=(n1+1)⁢(n2+1)⁢…⁢(nm+1). The number of prime divisors is m.

m is largest if the nj are as small as possible: Take k, factor it into prime factors, and count the prime factors according to their multiplicity. For example, if a number x has k=20=2⋅2⋅5 divisors, it can have at most 3 prime factors since 2⋅2⋅5 is the product of three primes. Numbers with 20 divisors and 3 prime divisors are the numbers of the form p⁢q⁢r4 where p, q and r are distinct primes.
