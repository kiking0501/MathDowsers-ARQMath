I suppose this is more of a brute force method. By FlT, you know p4≡1(mod5), so 5 is a factor. Also, factoring, you get

p4-1=(p-1)⁢(p+1)⁢(p2+1),

and since p is odd, each factor is divisible by 2. Moreover, one of the factors p-1 or p+1 is divisible by 4, so 4⋅2⋅2=16 is another factor. Finally, of the three consecutive integers, p-1, p, and p+1, 3 is a factor of one. Since p is prime and larger than 5, 3⁢\nmid⁢p, so 3|p4-1. Hence 16⋅3⋅5=240 is a factor of p4-1 for any prime p⁢\gt⁢5.

It still remains to see that 240 is the greatest such divisor. This can be confirmed by looking at 74-1=2400 and 114-1=14640.

A possibly simpler way is that: p16-1=0mod48, as φ⁢(48)=16. We also have x4+1=2mod4 for odd x. Thus p4-1=0mod48. Since p4-1=0mod5 too, it follows that p4-1=0mod240.
