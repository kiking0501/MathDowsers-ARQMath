Strong Markov property with two stopping times

I have a diffusion X=(Xt)t≥0 and a stopping time τ. From the strong Markov property I know that for any time t≥0 (or a random time independent of X) I get that ℙX0[Xτ+t∈⋅|ℱτ]=ℙXτ[Xt∈⋅]. Now, let us in addition to τ have another stopping time σ which we define as σ:=inf{s>τ;Xs∈A} for some subset A of the state space. Now, intuitively ℙX0[Xσ∈⋅|ℱτ]=ℙXτ[Xσ′∈⋅], where σ′:=inf{s>0;Xs∈A}. However, I somehow fail to show this rigorously. It is clear that σ is "sort of like" τ+σ′ but the problem is that unlike the case when I have τ+t,i.e. a stopping time and a constant, the σ′ depends on X. I feel like I am missing something fairly easy here. Thanks for any hints.

EDIT: Is it simply that if I denote by θτ the shift operator at τ then σ∘θτ=σ′ and Xσ∘θτ=Xσ′? I am asking because I am not sure how to actually apply the shift operator in this case.

1. (Correction) Your first formula should read ℙX0[Xτ+t∈⋅|ℱτ]=ℙXτ[Xt∈⋅].

2. It is true that σ⁢(ω)=τ⁢(ω)+σ′⁢(θτ⁢(ω)⁢ω). Consequently ℙX0[Xσ∈⋅|ℱτ]=ℙXτ[Xσ′∈⋅]
