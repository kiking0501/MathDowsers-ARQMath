Full solution: Let f⁢(x) be the indicator function for the interval [-π,π]. According to the definition of Fourier transform you supplied (and sticking to u instead of ω is the frequency variable), the transform of f(x) is F⁢(u)=2⁢∫0πcos⁡(u⁢x)⁢𝑑x=2⁢sin⁡(π⁢u)u. Now, the inverse transform of F⁢(u) is, according to what you supplied, f⁢(x)=1π⁢∫0∞F⁢(u)⁢cos⁡(x⁢u)⁢𝑑u=2π⁢∫0∞sin⁡(π⁢u)⁢cos⁡(x⁢u)u⁢𝑑u, where I in the last step just substituted F⁢(u) for it's expression. But now you know that this last step is the inverse transform of the transform of f⁢(x), which is f⁢(x), by definition.
