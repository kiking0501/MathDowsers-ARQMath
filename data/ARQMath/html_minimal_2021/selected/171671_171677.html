You can get a cleaner proof when you avoid sequences altogether:

Suppose that every neighborhood of x contains a point of A. Obviously, d⁢(x,A)≥0. Now let ϵ>0. Then there exists by assumption a∈A with d⁢(x,a)<ϵ. Since ϵ was arbitrary, d⁢(x,A)=0.

Suppose that d⁢(x,A)=0. Let ϵ>0. By assumption, there is a∈A with d⁢(x,a)<ϵ. Hence, every open ϵ-ball around x contain an element of A. Since every neighborhood of x is a superset of such a ball, we are done.

Afficionados might note that one avoids having to make arbitrary choices in the sequence-free proof.
