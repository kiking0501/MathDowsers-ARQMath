Both are fine.

  • Basically the induction hypothesis is a tool that is given to you.

  • The (k+1)-th case is what you want to show. Your goal.

As long as you can show the (k+1)-th case, it is up to you how do you want to use your tool. You can either use it at the beginning as the video shown, or you can use it as an intermediate tool.

In fact, for this particular case, it is almost identical.

\begin{align} \text{LHS of what you want to show} &= 3^{k+1} \\ &= 3 (3^k) \\ & \geq 3(1+2k) \\ &= 3+6k\\ &= 2k + 3+ 4k\\ &\geq 2k+3\\ &=\text{RHS of what you want to show} \end{align}
