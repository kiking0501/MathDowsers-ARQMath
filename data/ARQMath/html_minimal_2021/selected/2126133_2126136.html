Is there more than one harmonic number that is natural?

n-th harmonic number is:

Hn=∑k=1n1k

is there some n≠1 for which Hn is a natural number?

Or can it be proven that there is no such number?

harmonic-numbers
There is no such number. You can show it by checking that the denominator of Hn is even but the numerator of Hn is odd, for n≥2.

There are no examples other than n=1. This can be seen as a result of Bertrand's Postulate. Moreover, for all n≥2 the numerator of Hn is an odd number while the denominator of Hn is an even number.
