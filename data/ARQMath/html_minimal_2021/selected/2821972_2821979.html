Comparing cohomologies of 𝒪X and 𝒪Xred

Let X be a noetherian scheme. If Xred is affine, then Hi⁢(X,𝒪X)→Hi⁢(X,𝒪Xred) is an isomorphism for all i>0. Is this true even if Xred is not affine?

This is in general false. For a simple example, take Y, the projective line and consider R=𝒪Y⊕L for any line bundle L on Y. Then R can be given an 𝒪Y-algebra structure by putting L⋅L=0. Let X be the spec of R. Then, you have 𝒪X=R and Xr⁢e⁢d=Y. By choosing L=𝒪Y⁢(-n) for n≥2, you see that H1⁢(𝒪Y)=0, but H1⁢(𝒪X)≠0.
