As Qiaochu points out d⁢(x,y) is continuous for fixed x. You may like to see this as well, as this is a familiar result in Topology:

If A is a non empty subset of a metric space (X,d) then the function f on X given by f⁢(x)=d⁢(x,A):=infy∈A⁡d⁢(x,y) is continuous. Indeed, |f⁢(x)-f⁢(y)|=|d⁢(x,A)-d⁢(y,A)|≤d⁢(x,y), and thus f is uniformly continuous (use δ=ϵ in any point).

To show this, let x and y be points in X, and p any point in A.

Then d⁢(x,p)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,p)   (triangle inequality) and so d⁢(x,A)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,p) as d⁢(x,A) is the infimum. But then d⁢(y,p)≥d⁢(x,A)-d⁢(x,y) (for all p, obtained by subtracting from the previous inequality), so that d⁢(y,A)≥d⁢(x,A)-d⁢(x,y) (as d⁢(y,A) is the infimum). So : d⁢(x,A)-d⁢(y,A)≤d⁢(x,y).

Now reverse the roles of x and y to get d⁢(y,A)-d⁢(x,A)≤d⁢(x,y).
