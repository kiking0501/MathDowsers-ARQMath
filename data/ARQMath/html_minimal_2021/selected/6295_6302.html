It's helpful to know the multiplicative structure of (ℤ/n⁢ℤ)*. It is always a product of cyclic subgroups.

For powers of 2, we have (ℤ/2k⁢ℤ)*≡C2×C2k-2.

For odd primes p, we have (ℤ/pk⁢ℤ)*≡C(p-1)⁢pk-1.

Knowing the structure for the prime powers is enough; we can use the Chinese Remainder Theorem to see that

(ℤ/p1i1⁢⋯⁢prir⁢ℤ)*=(ℤ/p1i1⁢ℤ)*×⋯×(ℤ/prir⁢ℤ)*.

Then (ℤ/p⁢q⁢ℤ)*=Cp-1×Cq-1 has four solutions to x2=72, and Bill's answer shows very nicely how to find them.
