The second case falls immediately under the category [characteristic polynomial of a rank 1 matrix](http://math.stackexchange.com/q/904926/18880), which would make it a duplicate question. The first one is somewhat different though.

Let

\begin{align} T_n &= \begin{vmatrix} a_1+x & x & x & \cdots & x \\ x & a_2+x & x & \cdots & x \\ x & x & a_3+x & \cdots & x \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x & x & x & \cdots & a_n+x \end{vmatrix} \\ &= \begin{vmatrix} a_1+x & x & x & \cdots & x \\ -a_1 & a_2 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ -a_1 & 0 & a_3 & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ -a_1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & a_n \end{vmatrix} \end{align}

Expand Tn with the last column, and we can get

Tn=x⁢a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢an-1+an⁢Tn-1

It's obvious that

T1=a1+x

By induction, we can get

Tn=x⁢∑j=1n∏i=1i≠jnai+∏i=1nai
