In fact, there are two solutions, namely 23 and 46. Also, there is a more general result, namely: If p is a prime number with p>3 and 2⁢p+1 is prime, then there are exactly two numbers n such that ϕ⁢(n)=2⁢p, namely 2⁢p+1 and 4⁢p+2. Also, there is the Carmichael conjecture (still open, as far as I know) which says that there is no number in the image of phi having a unique antecedent. In my article "Some remarks on Euler's totient function", I discuss these questions and others.
