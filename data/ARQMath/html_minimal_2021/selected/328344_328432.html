More generally (for n odd), it's unknown if there are any nontrivial solutions to xn+yn=zn+wn, for n≥5.

I didn't find any for 5th or higher odd powers (looked for -100≤x,y,z≤100). [Note I don't count a∈U1∖U2 or permutations; looking for three distinct absolute values.]

But for cubes, with x,y,z∈[-200,200] there are three cases of x3+y3+z3=2: (-161,-54,163),(-47,-24,49),(-6,-5,7). There are also several cases of x3+y3+z3=1, but since 1 is a cube this is a special case of four cubes adding to zero, which there is literature on. The six cases found, again with x,y,z∈[-200,200]: (-150,73,144),(-138,-135,172),(-138,-71,144), (-103,64,94),(-12,9,10),(-8,-6,9). My guess is that the equation with 2 on the right might not have much literature on it. And there may be known methods to rule out 1 on the right for odd powers greater than 3.
