Prove that a1n converges to 1 as n goes to ∞ for a>0

I am having trouble proving that the sequence a1n converges to 1 as n→∞ for a>0.

My attempt at a proof:

I want to show that for every ϵ>0 there exists N∈ℕ:n≥N implies |a1n-1|<ϵ. So let ϵ>0 be given and by the Archimedean Property, we can choose a n such that 1n<ϵ. Then |a1n-1|<|aϵ-1|<ϵ since ϵ can be made small. I think am getting lost in this problem since I am not sure how to deal with a1n. It is obvious that for large n, a1n will approach 1 and so |a1n-1|=0, which is less than ϵ. Any help will be appreciated.

Use the definition a1n=exp⁡(1n⁢log⁡a) and the fact that the exponential function is continuous.

If a=1 then a1/n=1. If the result holds for all a>1, then it holds for 0<a<1 because of the inequalities 0<1-a1/n<(1/a)1/n-1 for 0<a<1. So it suffices to consider a>1. Let δn=a1/n-1 and use the binomial theorem to show that a>n⁢(n-1)2⁢δn2. Then f⁢(x)=0⁢ ⁢∀x∈[0,δ). Now argue that δn→0.
