The differences are in the order of operations. Parentheses are used to override the default order of operations.

Now, -3 is most definitely NOT equal to -3. If n is a positive integer, then -n=i⁢n, where i=-1 so that i2=-1. Then, since 3≈1.732, then -3≈1.732⁢i. On the real number line, center your compass on 0 and put the pencil on 1.732. Then, keeping the compass centered on 0, move the pencil 90 degrees.

And then:

  • (-3)2=3. Because of the parentheses, the first thing we do is multiply -3 by itself, giving us (-3)×(-3)=9, and 9=3.
  • -32=3⁢i. Without the parentheses, we multiply 3 by itself first (exponentiation has higher precedence than negation), giving us 9, and then we multiply it by -1, so at this point we have -9, which is 3⁢i.
  • (-3)2=-3. Here the parentheses mean that we multiply -3 by itself, and that gives us -3.

Lastly, if I were you, I would put that "rule" about an out of my mind, before it leads me astray any further.

More generally, for real numbers a and b the equality a⁢b=a⁢b fails precisely when both are negative.
