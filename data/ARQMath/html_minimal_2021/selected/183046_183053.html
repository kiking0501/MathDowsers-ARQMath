Showing that lim⁡χBm⁢(x)=χA⁢(x) shouldn't be too hard. First suppose x∈A and show that χBm⁢(x)→1, using the definition of limit. Then handle the case x∉A. This doesn't actually have anything to do with σ-algebras, just elementary set theory.

The second part is sometimes called the Borel-Cantelli lemma. Hints for that:

  1. μ⁢(⋃Cn)≤∑μ⁢(Cn), whether or not the Cn are disjoint;

  2. What does it mean for an infinite sum to be finite?
