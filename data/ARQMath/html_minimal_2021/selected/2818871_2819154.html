The equality case follows from the proof of the Schur's inequality.
@MichaelRozenberg How does it follow from the proof?

You seem to want to show the implication that if xt⁢(x-y)⁢(x-z)+yt⁢(y-z)⁢(y-x)+zt⁢(z-x)⁢(z-y)=0 for x,y,z⩾0 and t>0 then at least two among x,y,z are the same and the other zero, or all are equal.

First suppose all are distinct, i.e. WLOG x>y>z⩾0 using symmetry. Then we may divide throughout by the positive number (x-y)⁢(y-z)⁢(x-z) to get xty-z+ztx-y=ytx-z Note that all the three fractions are necessarily non-negative. Further, xt>yt and y-z<x-z, so the first term on LHS is strictly higher than the RHS. Hence this equality is not possible, i.e. x,y,z cannot be distinct.

Now we consider that perhaps two among the three are equal, i.e. y=z, say. In this case the equality becomes xt⁢(x-y)2=0, which is satisfied iff x=0 or x=y.

Hence we have proved the implication required.

PS: the same argument works with a little tweak for x,y,z real when t Is even and positive or if xt is replaced by some increasing function f⁢(t) etc.
