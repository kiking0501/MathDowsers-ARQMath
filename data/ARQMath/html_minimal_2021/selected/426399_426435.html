I agree with the comment about right angled triangles. For example, by definition, arccos⁡(x) is the angle θ in a right triangle with hypotenuse 1 occurring adjacent to a leg of length x, and by the Pythagorean theorem and the definition of sine, the sine of such an angle is 1-x2/1 (draw the picture). The others can be shown similarly.

I think this is perfectly rigorous (and can be extended by symmetry or differentiation arguments to the whole domain of the arccosine function) -- it's just a matter of what you take as the starting definition of the trig functions.
