Here's an outline for a proof of your claim assuming X is regular.

Let x∈X be any point and let U be some open set containing x. We know that X∖U is closed so by regularity there exist disjoint open sets V,W with x∈V and X∖U⊆W. Let y be a limit point of V. The point y is not in W, because otherwise we would find an open set U′ such that y∈U′⊆W, but this contradicts that V∩W=∅ since U′ must contain points of V. Hence y∉W which implies y∈U and it follows V¯⊆U.
