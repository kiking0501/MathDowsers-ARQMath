Proving (n!1n)n∈ℕ*→∞

By definition, for any a∈ℝ, there exists k∈ℕ* such that, if n∈ℕ+k, then n!1n>a. Therefore, by induction, I must:

  • find some k that satisfies k!>ak;
  • show that, for each n≥k, if n!>an, then (n+1)!>an+1.

For the 2nd part, it's enough that k≥a-1. I noticed the smallest adequate k is 4 for a=2 and 7 for a=3, and I'm convinced that one can always find a k, but I would like a function of a to make an actual argument.

Definition aside, methods other than this one are welcome. I tried picking a sequence dominated by (n!1n) that went to infinity as well, but couldn't find any.
