Only hints for 1x⁢∫0xd⁢t2+cos⁡(t)=g⁢(x)/x. Let f⁢(x)=12+cos⁡(t). Note that f is periodic, with period 2⁢π. Let x large, and define n by 2⁢π⁢n≤x<(2⁢n+2)⁢π. Then g⁢(x) is the sum of ∫2⁢k⁢π(2⁢k+2)⁢πf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t, for k=0,⋯⁢n-1 and of ∫2⁢n⁢πxf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t. The change of variable t=2⁢k⁢π+u gives that ∫2⁢k⁢π(2⁢k+2)⁢πf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t=∫02⁢πf⁢(u)⁢𝑑u=L. You get that g⁢(x)=n⁢L+∫2⁢n⁢πxf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t Now it is easy to bound ∫2⁢n⁢πxf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t by 2⁢π, and to show that if we divide by x, it has limit 0, and to show that n/x has a limit, and we are done.
