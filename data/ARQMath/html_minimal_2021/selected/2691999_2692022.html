Since s⁢i⁢n⁢(x)x→1 as x→0, we can define f:ℝ→ℝ by

f⁢(x):=s⁢i⁢n⁢(x)x if x≠0 and f⁢(0):=1. Then f is continuous and ∫0π/2s⁢i⁢n⁢(x)x⁢𝑑x=∫0π/2f⁢(x)⁢𝑑x.

Consequence: ∫0π/2s⁢i⁢n⁢(x)x⁢𝑑x is not improper.
