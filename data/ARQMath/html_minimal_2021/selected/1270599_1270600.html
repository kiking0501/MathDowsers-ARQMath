As a reference: this exercise was proposed in the admission test of SNS (*Scuola Normale Superiore*, Italy), 2015 (If I recall it correctly).

For all positive n we have \require⁢\cancel⁢(n2+n2)2=\cancel⁢n4+n3+n24<\cancel⁢n4+n3+n2+n+1⁢n24<n2+n+1 and on the other hand \require⁢\cancel⁢(n2+n+22)2=n4+n3+2⁢n2+n2+4⁢n+44>n4+n3+n2+n+1⁢\cancel⁢n4+n3+94⁢n2⁢\cancel+n+1>\cancel⁢n4+n3+n2⁢\cancel+n+1⁢94⁢n2>n2. As a consequence, n cannot be even, and for some odd n we must have (n2+n+12)2=\cancel⁢n4+n3+n2+n2+2⁢n+14=\cancel⁢n4+n3+n2+n+1⁢n2+2⁢n+1=4⁢n+4⁢n2-2⁢n-3=(n-3)⁢(n+1)=0, whence n=3. In particular, 34+33+32+3+1=112.
