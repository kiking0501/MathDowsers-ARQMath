3x +3(-y) is congruent to 1; 3(x-y) is congruent to 1. x-y=-2=5. letting z=-y we get x+z=5. Thus the solutions are (5,0) (4,-1) (3,-2), (2,-3) (1,-4) (0,-5), (-1,-6)

Hint: for any x,y we always have 3⁢x+4⁢y=3⁢(x+4)+4⁢(y-3).

So by induction:

3⁢x+4⁢y≡1mod7⟹3⁢(x±4⁢k)+4⁢(y∓3⁢k)≡1mod7

So... if you know one solution, you know infinitely many.

The question is are there any not generated from your first solution? (I.e. can we make that ⟹ into ⇔?)
