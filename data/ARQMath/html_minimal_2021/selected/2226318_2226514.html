Answer: c⁢o⁢s⁢θ = -56.

Solution: Square s⁢i⁢n⁢θ to get 1136. Using the fundamental trigonometric identity sin2⁡θ+cos2⁡θ=1, substitute 1136 into this equation to get 1136+cos2⁡θ=1, and subtract from 1, giving us cos2⁡θ=2536. Taking the square root gives us cos⁡θ=±56.

To determine which sign we should use, we use the unit circle and see that sine is positive in two quadrants, the first and the second. Since all angles in the first quadrant are positive, that means we are working with the second quadrant (all angles between π2 and π), meaning c⁢o⁢s⁢θ is negative in the second quadrant. Hence, we choose the minus sign for the equation, ∴cos⁡θ=-56.
