That's a deep theorem: Invariance of domain. It says even more: every injective continuous mapping f:U→ℝn defined an open subset U⊂ℝn is an open map. The proof is very difficult and belongs one way or another to Algebraic Topology. One consequence of this theorem is Invariance of dimension: if ℝn is homeomorphic to ℝm then n=m. As for the especific question, the answer is yes.
