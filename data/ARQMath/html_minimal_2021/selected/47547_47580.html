Suppose f⁢(x) were such a function. Note that each An=f⁢([-n,n]) is a closed (actually compact) set, with ∪An=ℝn. By the Baire category theorem, there is one such An that contains a closed ball B. Since [-n,n] is compact, the image of any relatively closed subset of [-n,n] is compact and thus closed. Hence f-1 is continuous when restricted to An, and thus when restricted to B. So in particular f-1⁢(B) is a connected subset of ℝ. Since all connected subsets of ℝ are intervals, f-1⁢(B) is a closed interval I.

Let x be any point in the interior of B such that f-1⁢(x) is not an endpoint of I. Then B-{x} is still connected, but f-1⁢(B-{x}) is the union of two disjoint intervals, which is not connected. Since f-1 when restricted to B-{x} is continuous, you have a contradiction.
