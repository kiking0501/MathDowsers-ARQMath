See also [Proof of the Hockey-Stick Identity: ∑t=0n(tk)=(n+1k+1)](http://math.stackexchange.com/q/1490794) and other posts [linked there](http://math.stackexchange.com/questions/linked/1490794).

The coefficient of xm in (1+x)n+1-(1+x)mx is obviously equal to the coefficient of xm+1 in (1+x)n+1-(1+x)m which is nothing but (n+1m+1), as n≥m and the second term has degree m<m+1 and so gives no contribution.
