Let f:X→Y be a morphism of S-schemes. The graph morphism γf:X→X×SY is the pull-back of the diagonal morphism δ:Y→Y×SY along f×i⁢dY:X×SY→Y×SY. This implies that if δ is a closed embedding (i.e. Y is separated over S) so is γf. So γf induces an isomorphism between X and its image Γf⊂X×Y.

Why is it an isomorphism on it's image? A closed immersion only yields an epimorphism on the structure sheaves.
You are confusing the target (or codomain) X×Y and the schematic image Γf of γf. By definition Γf is the closed subscheme defined by the sheaf of ideal ℐ=ker(𝒪X×Y→(γf)*𝒪X). Since the morphism is surjective we get 𝒪X×Y/ℐ=𝒪Γf=(γf)*⁢𝒪X.
