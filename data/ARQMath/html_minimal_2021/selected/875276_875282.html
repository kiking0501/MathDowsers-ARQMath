For vector spaces, the definition of dimension is the number of vectors in a basis for the vector space.

A basis for a vector space V over a field ùîΩ is a set of vectors such that every vector in V is a unique linear combination of the basis vectors. That is, {v1,‚Ä¶,vk} is a basis when every vector v‚ààV can be written uniquely as v=a1‚Å¢v1+‚ãØ+ak‚Å¢vk for scalars a1, \dots, ak‚ààùîΩ.
