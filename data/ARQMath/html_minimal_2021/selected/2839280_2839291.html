"by definition" is not a circular explanation, it is the only possible explanation. What might be circular is an answer to why this is the definition that people use.
Actually, the simplest way to clear it out in my mind is to agree that -1 is never an "ok" notation, because it leads to meaningless confusions. Once we are given the usual abstract symbol i, there is no reason to consider it anymore.

A better question is: why is a⁢b=a⁢b for positive a, b?
It seems to be a common thing among students to assume that operations are "distributive". For example x2+y2=x+y, log⁡(x+y)=log⁡(x)+log⁡(y), etc.
You are assuming that the rule (a⁢b)r=ar⁢br extends to the complex numbers. It does not, that's it.

Since (a⁢b)2=a⁢b, a⁢b=±a⁢b. The only way to get rid of the ± sign for a more specific conclusion is by comparing the two sides of the equation. For a,b≥0, square roots are non-negative do we're fine. With negative numbers, the two i factors on the LHS ruin it. If you want a fancier explanation than that, no inverse of z2 on ℂ is continuous, as can be seen by considering its phase.
