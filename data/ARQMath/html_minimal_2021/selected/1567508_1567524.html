The second case falls immediately under the category [characteristic polynomial of a rank 1 matrix](http://math.stackexchange.com/q/904926/18880), which would make it a duplicate question. The first one is somewhat different though.

For 2) Let A=[a1a2..ana1a2..ana1a2..an…………a1a2..an]

Then A has rank 1 and hence λ=0 is an eigenvalue with geometric multiplicity n-1, and hence has algebraic multiplicity at least n-1.

As the trace is the sum of eigenvalues, the last eigenvalue is a1+a2+..+an.

Thus det⁡(λ⁢I-A)=λn-(a1+…+an)⁢λn-1

Now replace λ by -x.

For 1) Subtract the last row from each of the previous n-1. You get |a1+xxx…xxa2+xx…xxxa3+x…x⋮⋮⋱⋮xxx…an+x|=|a100…-an0a20…-an00a3…-an⋮⋮⋱⋮xxx…an+x|

This determinant is a linear function in x. Therefore |a1+xxx…xxa2+xx…xxxa3+x…x⋮⋮⋱⋮xxx…an+x|=a⁢x+b

Now when x=0 we get b=a1⁢…⁢an

All you have to do next is do row expansion by the last row in |a100…-an0a20…-an00a3…-an⋮⋮⋱⋮xxx…an+x| in order to find the coefficient of x.

For 2), without eigenvalues: Transpose the matrix, and then add all rows to the first. We get:

|a1+xa2a3…ana1a2+xa3…ana1a2a3+x…an⋮⋮⋱⋮a1a2a3…an+x|=|a1+xa1a1…a1a2a2+xa2…a2a3a3a3+x…a3⋮⋮⋱⋮ananan…an+x|=|a1+a2+..+an+xa1+a2+..+an+xa1+a2+..+an+x…a1+a2+..+an+xa2a2+xa2…a2a3a3a3+x…a3⋮⋮⋱⋮ananan…an+x|=(a1+a2+..+an+x)|111…1a2a2+xa2…a2a3a3a3+x…a3⋮⋮⋱⋮ananan…an+x|

Now if you do each row minus ai row one you get =(a1+a2+..+an+x)|111…10x0…000x…0⋮⋮⋱⋮000…x|
