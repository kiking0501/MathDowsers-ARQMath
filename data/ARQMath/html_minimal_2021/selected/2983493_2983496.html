There's many way to view it but basically x⁢(1-x)≤14⇔-x2+x-14≤0. -x2+x-14 is a parabola that "opens downward" and has a maximum.

With a bit more (calculus) machinery, let f⁢(x)=x⁢(1-x). The extreme value theorem implies that f has a maximum on [0,1]. Either the maximum occurs at one of the endpoints f⁢(0)=f⁢(1)=0 or in the interior in which case the derivative f′⁢(x)=1-2⁢x must vanish. We note that f′⁢(1/2)=0 and f⁢(1/2)=1/4>f⁢(0)=f⁢(1)=0. Thus for all x∈[0,1], f⁢(x)=x⁢(1-x)≤1/4 with equality iff x=1/2.
