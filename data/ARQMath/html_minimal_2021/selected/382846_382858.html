The ei⁢θ factor may be throwing you off. The functions ψa⁢(z)=a-z1-a¯⁢z are very important functions in complex analysis called Blaschke factors.

They have many nice properties, here are three that you should check:

Provided |a|<1,

  1. ψa interchanges a and 0, so ψa⁢(0)=a and ψa⁢(a)=0.

  2. As a map defined on the closed unit disk D, ψa is its own inverse.

  3. ψa sends the unit circle to itself.

Given 2 and 3, the rest of your problem should be very easy. 1 is just for fun, though if you study conformal mappings 1 turns out to be a crucial property as well.
