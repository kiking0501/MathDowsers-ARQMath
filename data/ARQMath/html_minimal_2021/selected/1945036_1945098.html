Convergence of the exponential for a densely-defined linear operator on a Hilbert space

Let

  • H be a ℝ-Hilbert space
  • D⁢(A) be a dense subspace of H and A be a linear operator from D⁢(A) to H
  • (en)n∈ℕ be an orthonormal basis of H with -A⁢en=λn⁢en⁢for all ⁢n∈ℕ for some (λn)n∈ℕ⊆(0,∞) with λn+1≥λn⁢for all ⁢n∈ℕ

Let t≥0. Is e-t⁢A⁢x:=∑n∈ℕe-λn⁢t⁢⟨x,en⟩H⁢en a well-defined bounded linear operator on H?

The actual question is: Is the series in (3) convergent? By the comparison test, it's sufficient to show that ∑n∈ℕe-λn⁢t is convergent.

Let M:=supn∈ℕ⁡λn∈(0,∞] and αn:=|e-λn+1⁢te-λn⁢t|=e-(λn+1-λn)⁢t⁢for ⁢n∈ℕ. By the ratio test, it's sufficient to show that αn≤q⁢for all ⁢n∈ℕ for some q∈[0,1). However, by (2) we only obtain αn≤1 for all n∈ℕ. Moreover, if M<∞, then λn+1=λn⁢for all ⁢n≥N for some N∈ℕ and hence ∑n∈ℕe-λn⁢t should be divergent.

So, do we need to assume M=∞? And how do we prove the convergence in that case?
