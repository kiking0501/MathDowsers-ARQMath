Let us assume that the prime divisors of S⁢(k) belong to {p1,p2,…,pm} for any k≥0. By Van Der Waerden's theorem, there are arithmetic progressions k1,k2,k3,…,kη of arbitrary length such that S⁢(k1),S⁢(k2),…,S⁢(kη) are multiples of some pμ, and we may assume without loss of generality kη>pμ. Newton's identities then imply that a1,a2,…,an are multiples of pη. We may define a~j=aj/pη and repeat the same argument, but since ℤ is a UFD an infinite descent is not allowed and the claim is proved.
