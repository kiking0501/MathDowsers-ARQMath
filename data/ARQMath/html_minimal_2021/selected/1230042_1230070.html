proof-writing induction factorial
Note that the inequality is equivalent to the more tractable-looking n!n+1<(n+1)!n.
The inequality says that the geometric mean of {1,2,…,n} is strictly less than the geometric mean of {1,2,…,n,n+1}. It might be easier to work out the analogous problem for the arithmetic mean as a warm-up. (In fact, if you can solve the analogous problem for the arithmetic mean, you could just take logs.)

No need to use induction.

(n!)1n<((n+1)!)1n+1⟸n!<((n+1)!)nn+1⟸(n!)n+1<((n+1)!)n

The last inequality holds since for all 1≤i≤n and i∈ℕ we have i<(n+1).
