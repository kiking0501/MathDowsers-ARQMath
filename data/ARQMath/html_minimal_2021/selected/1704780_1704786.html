Discrete Mathematics - Congruence

Prove that there does not exist n∈ℕ; such that n≡2(mod4) and n≡4(mod8).

What did you try?

If n≡4 (mod 8) then we can express n as n=8⁢k+4. This means n=4⁢(2⁢k+1) which shows that 4|n. This means n≡0 (mod 4) so it is impossible for a number to satisfy the two conguence equations you gave.
