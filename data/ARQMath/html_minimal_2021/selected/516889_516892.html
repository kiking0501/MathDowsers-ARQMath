Among three consecutive integers, one must be a multiple of three. Reason: if n=3⁢k, we're done. If n=3⁢k+1 then n+2=3⁢j is a multiple of three. If n=3⁢k+2, then n+1=3⁢m is a multiple of three. In any case, 3∣n⁢(n+1)⁢(n+2).
