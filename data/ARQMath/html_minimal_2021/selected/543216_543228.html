To prove that a general ring is a PID, you must (1) prove the ring is an integral domain (i.e. no zero divisors), and (2) prove that every ideal is principal.
This can be hard. Sometimes you just try to prove it. Sometimes you get lucky and can try other routes, like first showing it is a Euclidean Domain, since [EDs are PIDs](http://www.proofwiki.org/wiki/Euclidean_Domain_is_Principal_Ideal_Domain).
