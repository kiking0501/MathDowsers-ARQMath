@Siminore In fact I think I got it with cauchy criterion for improper integral.

Presumably, a∈ℝ. Clearly if a≤0, the integral diverges as ∫2⁢n⁢π(2⁢n+1)⁢πsin⁡tta⁢𝑑t doesn't go to 0. If a≥1 The integral again diverges as ∫01cos⁡xxa⁢𝑑x diverges.

What remains is 1>a>0 In this case, split the region into intervals of size π Taking the imaginary part, we have, ∫0∞sin⁡xxa⁢𝑑x=∑n=0∞∫n⁢π(n+1)⁢πsin⁡xxa You can check that this forms an alternating series with decreasing terms. Hence it converges. Similarly, the integral with cos also converges, and hence the whole thing converges.
