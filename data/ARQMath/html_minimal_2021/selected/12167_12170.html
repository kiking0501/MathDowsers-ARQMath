You can say whatever you want. However, if you want to be correct, and you are talking about cardinalities, then you cannot say that there are more rationals than integers.

The reason we can say that there are more reals than rational numbers is because they actually have greater cardinality, not just because the set of reals properly contains the natural numbers. While we can find a bijection between ℕ and ℚ, no function from ℕ to ℝ is surjective (and, naturally, no function from ℚ to ℝ can be surjective either).
