You can't apply the theorem that an→l implies bn=(a1⁢a2⁢⋯⁢an)1/n to this limit since the k-th term, i.e. 1+kn, depends on n.

If you take the natural log of the limitand(?) you get:

ln⁡[((1+1n)⁢(1+2n)⁢⋯⁢(1+nn))1/n]=1n⁢∑k=1nln⁡(1+kn),

which is a Riemann sum for ∫01ln⁡(1+x)⁢𝑑x.

So, as n→∞, we have 1n⁢∑k=1nln⁡(1+kn)→∫01ln⁡(1+x)⁢𝑑x=2⁢ln⁡2-1.

Exponentiating both sides gives us (1+1n)(1+2n)⋯(1+nn))1/n→e2⁢ln⁡2-1=4e as n→∞.
