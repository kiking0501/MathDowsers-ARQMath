Prime ideals in F⁢[x] when F is a field

Let F be a field. Prove that the polynomial ring F⁢[x] in one variable over F has infinitely many prime ideals. All I can think of if that if we can show there are infinitely many irreducible polynomials in F⁢[x] but I see no reason why that should hold, I have no information about the field F.

field-theory irreducible-polynomials

Assume there are only a finite number of irreducible monic polynomials, say p1⁢(X),p2⁢(X),…,pn⁢(X). Now since F⁢[X] is a UFD, irreducible = prime, so each pi⁢(X) is prime. Now consider the monic polynomial q⁢(X)=∏inpi⁢(X)+1. By construction, q⁢(X) is not divisible by any of the pi⁢(X), hence it is either prime=irreducible itself, or divisible by another prime polynomial greater than pn⁢(X), contradicting the assumption of a finite number of prime=irreducible monic polynomials.
