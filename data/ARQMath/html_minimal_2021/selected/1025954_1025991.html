For the lower bound in the second inequality, see my answer [here](https://math.stackexchange.com/questions/985822/the-equivalence-of-numerical-radius-and-spectral-norm/986707#986707). The upper bound follows simply from the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.

Take an eigenvalue λ where the spectral radius is attained, and the corresponding eigenvector v with ∥v∥=1.

Then you can say that $$\rho(A) = |\lambda| = |\lambda v^*v| = \left| \frac{v^*Av}{v^*v}\right|\le \gamma(A)$$ (the latter inequality is true by definition of γ⁢(A)).

On the other hand, γ⁢(A)=supv≠0⁡|v*⁢A⁢vv*⁢v|=supv≠0⁡|v*⁢A⁢v|∥v∥2≤∥A∥ by the definition of ∥A∥.

The last inequality should be done similarly.
