How do I prove analytically using co- ordinate geometry that the diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular to each other

Suppose I have a rhombus A⁢B⁢C⁢D with sides A⁢B,B⁢C,C⁢D and D⁢A as s units. With the help of concepts of co ordinate geometry I need to prove that the diagonals A⁢C and B⁢D are perpendicular to each other. How do I prove this particular property of rhombus?

Just co-ordinate geometry.

Say A⁢(0,0) and C⁢(p,q), then B and D are the points of intersection of circles x2+y2=1 and (x-p)2+(y-q)2=1. Then the coordinates of B and D are the solutions of this system of equations. If we substract these two we get 2⁢p⁢x+2⁢q⁢y=0⟹y=-pq⁢x and B,D define this equation (since they are on it). The line A⁢C has equation y=qp⁢x we have:

kB⁢D⋅kA⁢C=-pq⋅qp=-1⟹A⁢C⊥B⁢D

So this method can also be used to show that if two circles intersect, the chord joining the points of intersection is perpendicular to the line joining the centres. Or alternatively, if we know the chord and the line joining the centres are perpendicular already we have a potential co-ordinate free proof.
