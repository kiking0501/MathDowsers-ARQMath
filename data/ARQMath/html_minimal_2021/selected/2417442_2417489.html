A equation about determinant

I read from the Wiki that:

When A = D and B = C, the blocks are square matrices of the same order and the following formula holds (even if A and B do not commute) det⁡(ABBA)=det⁡(A-B)⁢det⁡(A+B).

How to prove this relation when A and B do not commute?

linear-algebra matrices determinant

Substitute the row i by Ri-Ri+n for each i∈{1,…,n}, where n is the dimension of A and B. We get det⁡(ABBA)=det⁡(A-BB-ABA). Now, consider the matrix (A-BB-ABA) and substitute the column Rn+j by Rn+j+Rj for each j∈{1,…,n} to get

det⁡(A-BB-ABA)=det⁡(A-B0BA+B).
