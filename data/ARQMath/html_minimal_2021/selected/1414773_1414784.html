Your inequality implies that f/g is locally bounded. So indeed any singularity (except maybe the one at infinity) is removable.

Concerning the second part of your question:

Claim If |h⁢(z)|≤1+|z|, then h⁢(z)=λ⁢z+μ with |λ|<1, |μ|<1.

Proof Define k⁢(z)=h⁢(z)-h⁢(0). Then it is easy to see that |k⁢(z)|≤2+|z|. Then the function k⁢(z)/z is bounded. Indeed, it is bounded in a neighborhood of 0 since k⁢(0)=0, and lim sup|z|→∞⁡|k⁢(z)|/|z|≤1. Then by Liouville's theorem, k⁢(z)/z=λ must be constant, moreover, |λ|≤1 by the last inequality. It remains to note that |h⁢(0)|≤1 by the assumption.
