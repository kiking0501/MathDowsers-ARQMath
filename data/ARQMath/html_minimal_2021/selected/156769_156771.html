Decreasing subsequence

Let {an} be a sequence of real numbers, where 0<an<1, such that limn→∞⁡an=0, (then every subsequence will converges to zero). Is there any way to find a subsequence of an which is decreasing to 0?

sequences-and-series

Yes: since the sequence converges to 0, for every ϵ⁢\gt⁢0 there exists N∈ℕ such that for all n≥N, we have 0⁢\lt⁢an⁢\lt⁢ϵ.

So define the sequence recursively: take a1. Then let ϵ=a12; we know there is an n1⁢\gt⁢1 such that an1⁢\lt⁢a12. Now take ϵ=an12; we know there exists n2⁢\gt⁢n1 such that an2⁢\lt⁢an12.

Lather, rinse, and repeat.
