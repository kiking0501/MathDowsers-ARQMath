Let A be a real n×n matrix. We say that A is a difference of two squares if there exist real n×n matrices B and C with B⁢C=C⁢B=0 and A=B2⁢−⁢C2. Now If A is a diagonal matrix, then I have to show that that it is a difference of two squares.

linear-algebra matrices

Write your matrix A as A++A-, where A+ is a diagonal matrix consisting of entries on the diagonal of A that are non-negative and A- is a diagonal matrix consisting of entries on the diagonal of A that are negative. Now let B=A+ and C=-A- and you are done.
