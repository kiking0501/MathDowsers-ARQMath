The structure of (ℤ/72⁢ℤ)*

I am trying to do the following question in preparation for my number theory exam.

Write down the structure of G=(ℤ/72⁢ℤ)* as a product of cyclic groups and find a set of generators for G.

I have seen a solution to this question and it given as follows. We know 72=23×32. Thus G=(ℤ/8⁢ℤ)*×(ℤ/9⁢ℤ)*. We know that (ℤ/8⁢ℤ)*≅C2×C2 and (ℤ/9⁢ℤ)*≅C6 because ϕ⁢(9)=6. Therefore as 2 and 6 are not coprime, G≅C2×C2×C6 and we expect to find 3 generators.

We know that (ℤ/8⁢ℤ)* is generated by 7,5(mod8) and we easily check that the order of 2(mod9) in (ℤ/9⁢ℤ)* is 6 so it is the generator of the latter group.

To find the generators for G we use the Chinese Remainder Theorem. For the first generator we want an element that is 7(mod8) and 1(mod9). For the second generator we want something that is 5(mod8) and 1(mod9). Finally for the last generator we want an element that is 2(mod9) and 1(mod8). This gives a=37,b=55 and c=65 respectively.

I don't understand the last paragraph of this solution. How do we know that solving these congruences give generators for G?

The hint by Alex is exact, but you may rather see it at work. As Theophile remarked, it must be a=55,b=37,c=64 , to fit the order you chose, so for example:

55=7=-1(mod8)⟹552=1(mod8) and since  55=1(mod9), we have that  552=1⋅1=1(mod8⋅9=72) , and this covers the first factor in G=C2×C2×C6 . Now just check the same is the same, mutatis mutandis, for the other two factors...(did you spot where we used (8,9)=1 ?)
