IMHO the key slip here comes in saying that '-1=i'. When working with reals we can often use order considerations (e.g. x≥0) to eliminate any sign ambiguity when talking about ±x, but for complex numbers that ambiguity becomes much more inherent.

1-1=-1 is only true in the sense that 1 over a square root of -1 is a square root of -1. However, there are two square roots of every non-zero complex number, so you have to make sure to pick the right one. For square roots of non-negative real numbers it works to consistently pick the non-negative square root, but no such rule exists for all complex numbers.
