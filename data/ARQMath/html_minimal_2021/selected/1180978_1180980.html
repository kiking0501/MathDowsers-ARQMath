We will show that a has exactly n different roots. Write z=r′⁢(cos⁡θ+i⁢sin⁡θ). Writing in polar form the equation zn=a gives:

r′⁣n⁢ei⁢θ⁢n=r⁢ei⁢φ

Solving this equation gives r′⁣n=r and i⁢θ⁢n=i⁢φ+i⁢2⁢π⁢k for k∈ℤn-1. In particular,

r′=rn,θ=φ+2⁢π⁢kn(0≤k≤n-1)

Hence, we have found n distinct roots of a complex number a and we can write an explicit formula for these roots:

\begin{equation} \zeta_k = \sqrt[n]{r} \left( \cos \frac{ \varphi + 2 \pi k}{n} + i \sin \frac{ \varphi + 2 \pi k}{n} \right), 0 \leq k \leq n-1 \end{equation}
