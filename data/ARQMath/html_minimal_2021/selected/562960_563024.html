We have to assume that f is monotone, or f-1 would not be well defined. For simplicity assume in addition that 0≤a<b,0≤f⁢(a)<f⁢(b). Let γ⊂[a,b]×[f⁢(a),f⁢(b)] be the graph of f, and at the same time of f-1; see the following figure.

Then ∫abf⁢(x)⁢𝑑x is the area bounded by x=a, x=b, y=0, and γ. In the same way ∫f⁢(a)f⁢(b)f-1⁢(y)⁢𝑑y is the area bounded by y=f⁢(a), y=f⁢(b), x=0, and γ.

Looking at the figure one immediately verifies that the sum of these two areas is =b⁢f⁢(b)-a⁢f⁢(a).
