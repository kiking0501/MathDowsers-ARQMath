What is the definition of an infinite sequence {xn}n∈ℕ? It's a function f with domain ℕ, except we write xn for f⁢(n)..... And |ℚ|=|ℕ| is an abbreviation for, or an equivalent to, the statement that there exists a bijection f:ℕ→ℚ. And that's all there is to it.
You may enjoy the small book Stories About Sets, by Vilenkin.

It is actually quit easy once you know that |ℚ|=|ℕ| as this implies that there exists a bijection f:ℕ→ℚ. Now simply let xn=f⁢(n).
