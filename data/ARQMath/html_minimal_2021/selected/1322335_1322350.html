To show that xn is convergent where |xn+1-xn|<1n2

Let xn be a sequence in R such that |xn+1-xn|<1n2 for all n∈N.

Show that the sequence is convergent.

If it were |xn+1-xn|=1n2, could take help of the fact ∑1n2 is convergent and of triangular inequality.

But what to do here? Please help. Thanks in advance.

real-analysis sequences-and-series

We know that ∑k≥11k2 is convergent, then for all ϵ>0 exists some N∈ℕ such that for all m,n>N we have ∑k=nm1k2<ϵ so ϵ>∑k=nm1k2>|xm+1-xm|+⋯+|xn+1-xn|≥|xm+1-xn| so it is a Cauchy sequence and so it is convergent.
