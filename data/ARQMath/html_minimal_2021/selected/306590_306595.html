[Here](http://math.stackexchange.com/questions/30732/how-can-i-evaluate-sum-n-0-infty-n1xn) is a similar question.

This is a power series: ∑n=0∞(n+1)⁢xn

For power series we can integrate term by term and we have:

∫(∑n=0∞(n+1)⁢xn)⁢𝑑x=∑n=0∞∫(n+1)⁢xn⁢𝑑x=∑n=0∞xn+1=∑n=1∞xn=x1-x

So if we differentiate x1-x with respect to x, and we get the sum of the original series:

(x1-x)′=11-x+x(1-x)2=1(1-x)2

As wanted. Of course we have to have |x|<1 for everything to converge and have meaning.
