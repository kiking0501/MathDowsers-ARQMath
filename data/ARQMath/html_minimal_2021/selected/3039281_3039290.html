real-analysis complex-analysis proof-explanation

Well the first equality, namely ∫-11f⁢(x+t)⁢Qn⁢(t)⁢𝑑t=∫-x1-xf⁢(x+t)⁢Qn⁢(t)⁢𝑑t follows just from the fact that f is 0 outside [0,1] which is one of the simplificating assumptions Rudin makes.

Now ∫-x1-xf⁢(x+t)⁢Qn⁢(t)⁢𝑑t=∫01f⁢(t)⁢Qn⁢(t-x)⁢𝑑t follows by the substitution t = t-x.

The fact that ∫01f⁢(t)⁢Qn⁢(t-x)⁢𝑑t is a poly in x follows from writing Qn⁢(t+x)=∑k=0nai⁢(t+x)k=∑k=0nbi⁢(t)⁢xk and now ∫01f⁢(t)⁢Qn⁢(t-x)⁢𝑑t=∑k=0n(∫01bi⁢(t)⁢𝑑t)⁢xk, where bi⁢(t) are just the functions(polys) obtained by expanding each (t+x)k.
