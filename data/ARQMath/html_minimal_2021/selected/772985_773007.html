Proving gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)>1 ⇒ p1∣a xor p2∣a using Euclid's Lemma

Consider the lemma: If gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)>1, then either p1∣a or p2∣a.

How can this be proved using Euclid's Lemma?

proof-writing

Hint By Euclid, if gcd⁡(a,p1)=1=gcd⁡(a,p2) then gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)=1, contra hypothesis. Hence at least one of the gcds is >1, so p1∣a or p2∣a (note gcd⁡(a,pi)=pi or  1, by pi prime).

Or, using the form of Euclid's Lemma that prime p∣a⁢b⇒p∣a or p∣b, let d=gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2) By d>1 it has a prime factor p and p⁢∣d∣⁢p1⁢p2⇒p∣p1⁢p2 hence p∣p1 or p∣p2 by Euclid. If p∣p1 then p=p1 so p1⁢∣d∣⁢a⇒p1∣a. Similarly p∣p2⇒p2∣a.
