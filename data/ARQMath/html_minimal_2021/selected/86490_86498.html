For the Inductive Step:

Suppose that we have

nn3n<n! and n!<nn2n.

We want to show that this implies that

(n+1)n+13n+1<(n+1)! and (n+1)!<(n+1)n+12n+1.

Under these assumptions, to see the first inequality holds, write: (n+1)n+13n+1amp;=(n+1)3(n+1)n3namp;=n+13(n+1n)nnn3na⁢m⁢p;l⁢t;n+13⁢(n+1n)n⁢n!  (using inductive hypothesis)amp;=(1+1n)n13(n+1)! and this reduces to showing that (1+1n)n⁢13<1. The other inequality can be handled similarly. You can probably save some paper by doing both inequalities at the same time, but if you are just starting out, I would suggest working with one inequality at a time.
