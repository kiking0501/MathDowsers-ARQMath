I'm interested in characterising maximal ideals in 𝔽p⁢[x,y]. More precisely, my problem is:

Find all possible cardinalities for fields of the kind A/I, where A=ℤ⁢[x,y]/(x2+y2) and I is a maximal ideal in A (knowing that these cardinalities are all finite).

It is easy to obtain all fields of cardinality p, p prime. What can we say about pn? And, more in general, what can we say about maximal ideals in 𝔽p⁢[x]? (We can't use Hilbert's Nullstellensatz since 𝔽p is not algebraically closed.)

Thank you in advance.

Another easy case is the case q=2n. Any finite field 𝐅q is easily seen to be isomorphic to a quotient of the ring A if q=2n.

If A/I is a finite field, other than the ones you found already and the ones above, then there is a surjecive ring morphism f:A→𝐅q, where q=#⁢(A/I), q=pn with p an odd prime and n>1. Let x¯ and y¯ be the images of x and y in 𝐅q. Since f is surjective, 𝐅q=𝐅p⁢(x¯,y¯). In particular, x¯ and y¯ cannot both be zero. Since x2+y2=0 in A, one has aN+P in 𝐅q. Hence x¯ and y¯ are both nonzero. Then, (x¯/y¯)2=-1 in 𝐅q, i.e., 𝐅q has a square root of -1. It follows that q≡1(mod4).

Conversely, if q≡1(mod4), let i∈𝐅q be a square root of -1, and let x¯ be a generator of the field extension 𝐅q/𝐅p. Let y¯=i⁢x¯. Let f:A→𝐅q be the morhism defined by f⁢(x)=x¯ and f⁢(y)=y¯. Then f is surjective. It follows that 𝐅q is isomorphic to a quotient A/I of the ring A.
