If you meant "infinite direct product", on the other hand, then the answer is "no." Consider ℤ-modules; then a module is projective if and only if it is free. So ℤ itself is projective, but ∏i=1∞ℤ is not free, hence not a projective ℤ-module.

Here's the answer to the original question:

The direct sum of free modules is free, even if the family is infinite.

Assume that {Pi}i∈I is a family of projective modules; for each i, there exists Mi such that Pi⊕Mi is free. Then (⊕i∈IPi)⊕(⊕i∈IMi)≅⊕i∈I(Pi⊕Mi) is a direct sum of free modules, hence free. Since there is a module M (namely, M=⊕Mi) such that (⊕Pi)⊕M is free, it follows that ⊕Pi is projective.

That is, any direct sum of projective modules is projective.
