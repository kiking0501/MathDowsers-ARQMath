Indeed, what is your definition of a straight line? Surely it is characterized by the fact that its velocity vector (in any parametrization) never changes direction. Can you see that this happens if and only if γ′′ is parallel to γ′?

Hint for an intuition:

Think at γ′⁢(t) as the velocity of a point moving along the trajectory γ⁢(t). So γ′′⁢(t) is the acceleration of the point. If the acceleration is always aligned with the velocity, i.e. γ′′⁢(t)=k⁢γ′⁢(t)⁢∀t than the velocity has always the same direction and this means that the trajectory is straight line.
