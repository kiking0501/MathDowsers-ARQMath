Similar to your solution, but I prefer writing it as (2⁢n)!⁢n!n!⁢n!=n!⁢(2⁢nn). Then, as the central binomial coefficient (2⁢nn)=(2⁢n)!n!⁢n! is larger then the average, 22⁢n2⁢n+1 but smaller then the sum of all the binomial coefficients, 22⁢n, we see that limn→∞⁡(2⁢nn)n=4, and as limits are multiplicative, we are then left with evaluating limn→∞⁡4n⁢n!n.
Also worth noting that the method in the answer you linked to can be used to solve this question. (I.e. using a power series)
