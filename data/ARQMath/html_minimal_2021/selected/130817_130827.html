I gave a proof of a more general statement than your "related question" here: http://mathoverflow.net/questions/16583/a-trigonometry-problem

Note that: cos⁡2⁢x=cos2⁡x-sin2⁡x=2⁢cos2⁡x-1. Therefore you have cos⁡2⁢π5=2⁢cos2⁡π5-1

Now, \begin{align*} \cos\frac{\pi}{5} - \cos\frac{2\pi}{5} = \cos\frac{\pi}{5} - 2\: \cos^{2}\frac{\pi}{5}+1 \end{align*} This is a quadratic equation of the form 2⁢x2-x-1=0 and solving this will give you the value of cos⁡π5 from which you can find the above value which you need.

Perhaps it should be mentioned that the quadratic equation has two solutions; but only one of them is positive, so there is no doubt which of the two solutions is cos⁡π5.
