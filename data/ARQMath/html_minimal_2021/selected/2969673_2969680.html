You may get rid of that k by noticing that k⁢(nk)=n⁢(n-1k-1) if k,n≥1.

We have ∑k=0n(nk)⁢zk⁢2n-k=(z+2)n. Differentiating with respect to z gives ∑k=0nk⁢(nk)⁢zk-1⁢2n-k=n⁢(z+2)n-1 and now substitute z=-1. Equivalently, you can use k⁢(nk)=n⁢(n-1k-1) immediately inside the sum.
