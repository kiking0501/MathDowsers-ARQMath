What is the motivation of definition of quadratic integer rings for D=1mod(4)

I'm self-studying a ring theory and have a question. Why do we define a quadratic integer ring differently for the case D=1mod(4)? Why don't we just say that OQ⁢[5]=ℤ⁢[5] ?

ring-theory definition
All algebraic integers in ℚ⁢(D) shall be in the ring.
First, we define algebraic integer. Then we *prove* the quadratic integer ring is as you say.

This is because, if D≡1mod4, 𝐙⁢[D] is not the ring of algebraic integers in the extension field 𝐐⁢(D). It is only contained in it. The algebraic integers in such an extension is: 𝒪𝐐⁢(D)=𝐙⁢[1+D2].
