The answers show how to prove that f=x4+1 is reducible modulo every prime p. If you want to show that f is irreducible in ℤ⁢[x], one way is to apply Eisenstein's criterion with p=2 to f⁢(x+1)=(x+1)4+1=x4+4⁢x3+6⁢x2+4⁢x+2.
What happens is that the splitting field of x4+1 is ℚ⁢(ζ8)/ℚ, which is not a cyclic extension, so there are no inert primes in that extension.
