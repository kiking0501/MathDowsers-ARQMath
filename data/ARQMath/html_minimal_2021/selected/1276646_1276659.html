What you are trying to prove is that "if x is large enough, then 1x gets close enough to 0." Approaching infinity represents the concept of allowing x to be arbitrarily large.

Here is how to state the proof, with some comments along the way. Let ε>0 be some arbitrary real number (this will represent "error" or "distance from 0"). Let N=1ε (this represents how close to infinity x must be). Whenever x>N, then 1x<1N, and since 1N=ε we have |1x-0|<ε (that is, the distance between 1x and 0 is less than the error ε). Therefore, limx→∞⁡1x=0.
