The equation defines a conic with a rational point (0,0,1). The other rational solutions can be parametrized by lines through that point. As with x2+y2=z2 the integer solutions can be deduced from the rational ones, and the quadratic form x2+y2-z2 has a large linear symmetry group allowing one to move between solutions. In these respects the theory is the same as the one for Pythagorean triples.

There is a difference in the structure of the symmetry group.
