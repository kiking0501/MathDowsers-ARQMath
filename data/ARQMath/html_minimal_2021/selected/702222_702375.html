Yes, you must show that the tempered distribution (1+|ξ|2)s/2⁢u^ is given by integration against an L2 function. The multiplication by (1+|ξ|2)s/2 does not change whether or not it is an a.e. pointwise function, but it does affect square integrability. For example, the Fourier transform of Dirac δ is (up to a constant) the function 1. This is not in L2⁢(ℝn), but (1+|ξ|2)-r/2⋅1 is L2 for r>n.
