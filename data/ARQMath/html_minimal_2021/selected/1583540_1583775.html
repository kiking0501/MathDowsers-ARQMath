Another classical example is the shift maps. Let S be the space of real sequences S={(a0,a1,a2,…):ai∈ℝfor all i} (which, essentially, is the space of functions ℕ→ℝ).

Define the "left shift map" f:S→S as f⁢((a0,a1,a2,a3,…))=(a1,a2,a3,…) (i.e., remove the first element from the sequence), and the "right shift map" g:S→S as g⁢((a0,a1,a2,…))=(0,a0,a1,a2,…) (i.e., add a zero at the beginning of the sequence).

Then, f∘g is the identity, but g∘f is not.
