Fiber products exist in the category of locally ringed spaces (see e.g. Gillam's paper), and this also provides a direct construction (without gluing!) of the fiber product of schemes and reveals its explicit structure as a locally ringed space.

If f:X→S and g:Y→S are morphisms of locally ringed spaces, then the fiber product X×SY has the following description: Elements are of the form (x,y,s,𝔭), where (x,y,s) lies in the underlying topological fiber product, i.e. x∈X, y∈Y with f⁢(x)=s=g⁢(y), and 𝔭⊆𝒪X,x⊗𝒪S,s𝒪Y,y is a prime ideal satisfying 𝔭∩𝒪X,x=𝔪x and 𝔭∩𝒪Y,y=𝓂y. The stalk of the structure sheaf at such a point is the localization (𝒪X,x⊗𝒪S,s𝒪Y,y)𝔭. As for the topology and the structure sheaf in general, one uses (as in the construction of affine schemes) localization at elements f∉𝔭.

Actually, all this can be derived from the universal property of local schemes and the universal property of fiber products (exercise).
