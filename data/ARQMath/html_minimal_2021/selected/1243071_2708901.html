Since people are rejecting my edits suggesting to write an answer, I do it here:

Let me first reformulate the question to contain all relevant details:

Assume as definition of projective module the following:

P is projective if every exact sequence M→P→0 splits, that is, for every epimorphishm f:M→P there exists a right inverse homomorphism f′:P→M such that f⁢f′=1P

Then, I need to prove the following implication:

If P is projective, show that P is a direct summand of a free R-module, that is, there exists a free module F such that F=P⊕T for some other module T.

Then let me add all the details that were neccessary to me based on @MooS' answer:

Choose generators of P to get a surjection from a free module F onto P. More precisely, prove that:

If a module M has a spanning set X⊆M then there is an epimorphism R(X)→M→0

If you need it, you can find the proof in the book Rings and Categories of Modules, theorem 8.1.

Then what you have is a exact sequence F→fP→0 and taking the kernel K of f we get an exact sequence 0→K→iF→fP→0.

Since P is projective, by your definition, there exists f′ such that f⁢f′=1P but this is one characterization of being a splitting sequence. Another characterization of splitting sequence is that the center is sum of the non-null extremes, showing that F=K⊕P.
