You can use the completeness axiom. You know that ℚ+ is bounded below, so you know that inf must exist. Similarly, it is not bounded above, and hence the least upper bound (ie supremum) cannot exist.

Show that 0 is a lower bound. Then show that no number greater than 0 can be a lower bound.

Zero is a lower bound, because x>0 for all x∈X. For any ϵ>0, we can find some n∈ℕ⊂X such that 1n<0+ϵ. Suppose L is another lower bound for X. Then if L>0, the previous sentence gives a contradiction (take ϵ=L2), hence we must have L≤0. Consequently zero is the greatest lower bound, that is, inf⁡X=0.

Suppose U∈ℝ is an upper bound. We have ℕ⊂X, and we can find some n∈ℕ such that U<n, which contradicts U being an upper bound.
