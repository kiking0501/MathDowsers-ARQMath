Perhaps the one weakness of this approach is that it does not provide an immediate way to pick out the value for n . While one of the roots corresponds to an obtuse angle and so can be rejected in the context of the geometrical situation, the other two roots produce sin⁡α≈ 0.9010 and sin⁡α≈ 0.2225 . The latter gives us α=π14 , which looks promising, but leads to a non-integral result for n . It is actually the other value that turns out to work, yielding α=5⁢π14 .
