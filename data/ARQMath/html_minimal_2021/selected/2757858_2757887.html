Note that 1/a⁢b should be evaluated from left to right giving b/a. I am sure you mean 1/(a⁢b). Stacked fractions are easier to read. What you are looking for is a+b+ca⁢b⁢c

Let X=1a⁢b+1a⁢c+1b⁢c.

By adding the three euations, we find that 1a+1b+1c=0 hence a⁢b+a⁢c+b⁢c=0. Note that from the firat equation 1a⁢b=a-bb=ab-1, and similar for the other equations, so that X=ab+bc+ca-3. We also have from the first equation that 1a⁢c=a-bc, and similar for the other equations, so that X=ac-bc+ba-ca+cb-ab Also, using (2), 0=(1a⁢b+1b⁢c+1c⁢a)⁢(a⁢b+b⁢c+a⁢c)=3+ac+bc+ab+ba+ca+cb. On the other hand (4)+2⁢(3) says 3⁢X=ac+bc+ab+ba+ca+cb-6. Together with (5) this gives us X=-3.
