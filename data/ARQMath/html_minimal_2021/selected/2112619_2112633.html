I think that that is the case. Also, log⁡(f⁢(x)) exists if f⁢(x)>0.

Notice ∂3⁡l⁢n⁢(f)∂⁡α3=Ff3, ∂3⁡l⁢n⁢(g)∂⁡α3=Gg, and ∂⁡l⁢n⁢(f+g)∂⁡α=H(f+g)3, where F,G,H are sums of derivatives of f,g, and combinations and multiples of f and g's derivatives, resp. They are discontinuous whenever f,f′,f′′,g,g′, or g′′ are discontinuous or f≤0, g≤0, or f+g≤0. Since the first two are assumed to be continuous, f,f′,f′′,g,g′,g′′ are continuous, f>0, and g>0 in their domain. This implies that f+g>0, and therefore it must be that the third equation is also continuous.
