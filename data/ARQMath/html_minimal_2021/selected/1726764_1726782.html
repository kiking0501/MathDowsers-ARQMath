How to derive ∑j2 from telescoping property

The book Real Analysis via Sequences and Series has a method of proving that ∑j=1nj=n⁢(n+1)2 that I've never seen before. The way they do it is by starting with ∑(2⁢j+1), using the fact that 2⁢j+1=(j+1)2-j2, and then using the telescoping property.

I find this method very aesthetically pleasing, but I have two questions about this: (1) what was the motivation for starting with 2⁢j+1? Why would that have come into the authors' minds as a way of deriving a formula for ∑j? And, a related question: (2), after that derivation the authors state that the formula for ∑j=1nj2 can be found in a similar way. I haven't been able to figure out which telescoping series I should equate this to. How can the formula for this summation be found similarly?
