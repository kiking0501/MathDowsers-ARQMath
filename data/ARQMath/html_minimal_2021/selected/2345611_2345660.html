Let X a compact Hausdorff topological space. Let {An}n∈ℤ+ a collection of countable closed, such that the interior of An is empty, for all n∈ℤ+. Show that, there exist x∈X such that x∉⋃n=1∞An.

Any Compact Hausdorff space is a Baire space. And any non-empty open set in a baire space is of the second category, that is it cannot be written as countable union of nowhere dense sets.

The sets An are all nowhere dense as they are closed and has an empty interior, and since X is open in itself so X≠⋃n=1∞An, hence there exists x∈X such that x∉⋃n=1∞An.
