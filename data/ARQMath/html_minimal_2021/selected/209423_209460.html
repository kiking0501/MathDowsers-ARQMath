geodesics and unit speed curves

Say we have 2 surfaces M and M^ that intersect perpendicularly --> ⟨n,n^="">=0</n,>^ along the curve of the intersection intersection, where n is the unit normal to M and n^ is the unit normal to M^.

Assume the intersection of M and M^ is the image of a unit speed curve γ that is a geodesic in both M and M^.

How can we show that γ is a straight line?

Assuming we're in 3-space (so that "the unit normal to a surface" makes sense):

For each point γ⁢(t) on the curve, consider γ′′⁢(t), the curvature of γ. Since γ is a geodesic on M, γ′′⁢(t) must have no component along M, so γ′′⁢(t) is parallel to n. But similarly, γ′′⁢(t) is parallel to n^, while n⋅n^=0. Therefore γ′′⁢(t)=0 for all values of t, so γ is a straight line.

In particular, you don't need M and M^ to intersect perpendicularly, just transversely, for any shared geodesic to be a straight line.
