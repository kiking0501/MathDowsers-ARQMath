How can I prove that there isn't an everywhere continuous function f:ℝ→ℝ that transforms every rational into an irrational and vice-versa?

Let 𝕀=ℝ\ℚ and suppose f⁢(𝕀)⊆ℚ. f⁢(ℝ)=f⁢(ℚ)∪f⁢(𝕀)⊆f⁢(ℚ)∪ℚ, which is countable. Since f is continuous, f⁢(ℝ) is connected. Thus, f⁢(ℝ) is countable and connected, hence it is a singleton {x} and f is constant.

Clearly, no constant function fulfils the conditions you require, hence there is no continuous f such that f⁢(𝕀)⊆ℚ and f⁢(ℚ)⊆𝕀.
