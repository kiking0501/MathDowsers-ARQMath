We can solve this without equations. Ask the following (auxiliary) question: how many flips you need to get either N heads or N tails. Then to get N heads only you need twice as many flips. Start with the question of how many flips you need to get either H or T. The answer is x=12⁢(1)+12⁢(1)=1. The reason is that there is 12 probability to get H, and after 1 flip you are done. The same for T. To get only one H you then need two flips. OK. Now we ask what it takes to get H⁢H or T⁢T. The result is x=12⁢(1+2)+12⁢(1+2). The number 2 appears because, say you flip H first, then you need on average 2 flips to get another H, as we learned earlier. The same for T. So you need 3 flips to get T⁢T or H⁢H, and you need 6 flips to get H⁢H only. And so on. You need 12⁢(1+6)+12⁢(1+6)=7 flips to get either H⁢H⁢H or T⁢T⁢T, and 14 to get H⁢H⁢H only. If you need H⁢H⁢H⁢H or T⁢T⁢T⁢T, then flip 12⁢(1+14)+12⁢(1+14)=15 times, or 30 times to get just H⁢H⁢H⁢H. The sequence is 1,3,7,15,… to get either heads or tails. The formula is easy to extract: you need 2N-1 flips to get either N heads or N tails, or 2N+1-2 to get N heads only. If N=5 we get the answer: 62.
