Fourier transform of tempered function

How can we prove that the Fourier transform of: (1/|x|α) is C⁢(1/|ξ|)n-α as a tempered distribution (in 𝒮′⁢(ℝn)) , where C is a constant.

Find the Fourier transform of f⁢(ω)=1|ω|α. This distribution is radial and homogeneous of degree -α. You can use the scaling properties of the Fourier transform to show that this means that its Fourier transform is radial and homogeneous of degree -n+α. Then you can show that this only happens when this Fourier transform is of the form cα⁢1|ξ|n-α.

So it remains to determine cα. For this you can use Plancherel's theorem in conjunction with the fact that e-π⁢|ω|2 is its own Fourier transform, so that you have ∫Rn1|ω|2⁢α⁢e-π⁢|ω|2⁢𝑑ω=cα⁢∫Rn1|ξ|n-2⁢α⁢e-π⁢|ξ|2⁢𝑑ξ You can turn both integrals into one dimensional integrals using polar coordinates and then solve for cα. The result will be a ratio of gamma functions.
