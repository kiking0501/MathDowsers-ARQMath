If p>5 is a prime number, then the last digit of p4-1 is 0.

If p>5 is a prime number, then the last digit of p4-1 is 0 (ex.: 74-1=2400). How do I prove this?

prime-numbers

If p4 is ending on 1, then p4-1 will end on 0. Hence we need to show that for all p>5,p4≡1(mod 10). According to Euler theorem if p and 10 are relatively prime the congruence pφ⁢(10)=p4≡1(mod 10) holds. Any primes greater than 5 will always be coprime with 10. This shows that for all p>5,p4-1≡0(mod 10) is true.
