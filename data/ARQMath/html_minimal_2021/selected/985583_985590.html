This equation is also Project Euler's introduction to diophantine equations in [problem 66] (https://projecteuler.net/problem=66) or, as I like to call it, the Project Euler hazing ritual.

The equation X2-d⁢Y2=1 for d a positive integer, is called Pell equation (while Pell actually had not much to do with it); having this name at hand it will be easy to find lots of information on it. It indeed has infinitely many integral solutions.

One reason this equation is relevant is that it solutions are linked to invertible elements in the ring of algebraic integers of real quadratic fields.
