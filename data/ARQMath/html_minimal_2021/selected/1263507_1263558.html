When a sequence converges, that means that as you get further and further along the sequence, the terms get closer and closer to a specific limit (usually a real number).

A series is a sequence of sums. So for a series to converge, these sums have to get closer and closer to a specific limit as we add more and more terms up to infinity. For example, to see if the infinite series ∑k=1∞(1n2) converges, we analyse whether the sequence of sums given by1,1+122,1+122+132,… gets closer and closer to a limit as we go further along this sequence (it turns out the limit is actually π26, try adding up a lot of terms on a calculator to see that the series tends to this limit).

Something diverges when it doesn't converge. Notoriously the series ∑k=1∞(1n) actually diverges, as an example.
