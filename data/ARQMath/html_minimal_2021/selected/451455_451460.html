For the contour method: write the integral as 12⁢∫-∞∞ and then deform the contour to run slightly below the real axis. Next express cosines in terms of exponentials. Then (for a,b>0) the integrals containing e-i⁢a⁢x, e-i⁢b⁢x can be closed in the lower half plane and therefore give zero. The integrals containing ei⁢a⁢x, ei⁢b⁢x can only be closed in the upper half plane and are therefore given by the residues of ei⁢a⁢x/(2⁢x2), ei⁢b⁢x/(2⁢x2) at x=0. These are easy to compute.
