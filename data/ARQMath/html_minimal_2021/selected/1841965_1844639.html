I think we are the same, I mainly studied this definition in "Real Analysis" by Folland. The definition can be justified as follows, note that the maps ωs⋅:φ(ξ)∈𝒮(ℝn)⟼ωs(ξ)φ(ξ)∈𝒮(ℝn), where ωs⁢(ξ):=(1+|ξ|2)s/2 is continuous with respect to convergence in Schwartz space, then we can define ωs⁢u∈𝒮′⁢(ℝn) by ⟨φ,ωs⁢u⟩=⟨ωs⁢φ,u⟩ ∀φ∈𝒮⁢(ℝn). The Fourier operator ℱ:𝒮′⁢(ℝn)⟶𝒮′⁢(ℝn) is an isomorphism, extension of ℱ:L1⁢(ℝn)⟶L∞⁢(ℝn), and we can define Λs:𝒮′⁢(ℝn)⟶𝒮′⁢(ℝn) by Λs⁢u=ℱ-1⁢(ωs⁢u^) ∀u∈𝒮′⁢(ℝn), it is said Fourier multiplier with symbol ωs. The space Hs⁢(ℝn) is then defined as

Hs⁢(ℝn):={u∈𝒮′⁢(ℝn):Λs⁢u∈L2⁢(ℝn)}

and by Plancherel theorem we have the norm

∥u∥Hs=∥Λs⁢u∥L2=∥ℱ⁢(Λs⁢u)∥L2=∥ωs⁢u^∥L2=(∫ℝn|u^⁢(ξ)|2⁢(1+|ξ|2)s⁢𝑑ξ)1/2

I think that says "regular distributions" because in general we have continuous inclusion Lp⁢(ℝn)↪𝒮′⁢(ℝn), with tempered distribution defined by "functions" uf or Tf with your notation.
