Continuity of metric

I recently came across this definition: Let (X,d) be a metric space and A be a nonempty subset of X. For each x∈X we define a distance from x to A by the equation

d⁢(x,A)=inf⁡{d⁢(x,a)|a∈A}

I would guess that such a function will be continuous. But is there an easy and rigorous way to prove it?

Hint

d⁢(x,A)=inf⁡{d⁢(x,a):a∈A}≤inf⁡{d⁢(x,y):y∈A}+inf⁡{d⁢(y,a):a∈A}

so d⁢(x,A)=inf⁡{d⁢(x,a):a∈A}≤inf⁡{d⁢(x,y):y∈A}+d⁢(y,A)

so d⁢(x,A)-d⁢(y,A)≤inf⁡{d⁢(x,y):y∈A}<d⁢(x,y)
