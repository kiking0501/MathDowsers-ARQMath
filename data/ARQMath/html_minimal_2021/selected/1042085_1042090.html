This can be generalized for arbitrary n≥1.

The number of elements of order n is k⋅φ⁢(n), where k is the number of cyclic subgroups of order n and φ is the Euler totient function. This follows from the fact that a cyclic subgroup of order n has exactly φ⁢(n) generators.

Then the question boils down to how to find k ?
