Can somebody provide me with a sequence of (real-valued) functions, say on [0,1] with the Lebesgue measure, such that the sequence converges in probability, or maybe in ∥⋅∥L2, but does not converge almost surely?

measure-theory probability-theory

Consider a sequence {Xn} of independent random variables such that P(Xn=1)=1n and P(Xn=0)=1-1n. For 0<ε<1/2 we have P(|Xn|≥ε)=1n which converges to 0 hence Xn→0 in probability. Since ∑n≥1P(Xn=1)=+∞ and the events {Xn=1} are independent, second Borel-Cantelli lemma ensures that P(lim supn{Xn=1})=1 hence the sequence {Xn} does not converge to 0 almost everywhere (in fact the set on which this sequence does not converge to 0 has probability 1).

Counter-example for convergence in Lp have already be provided, but I think the example I gave is also useful.
