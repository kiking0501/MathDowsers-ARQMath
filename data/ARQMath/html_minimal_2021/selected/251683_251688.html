If f is continuous we have that f⁢(ℝ) is an interval. Thus f⁢(ℝ) is uncountable. On the other hand, we have \begin{equation} f(\mathbb{R}) \subset f(\mathbb{R}\setminus \mathbb{Q}) \cup f(\mathbb{Q}) \end{equation} Thus f⁢(ℝ) is countable as union finite of countables. Contradiction.

f⁢(ℚ) is not contained in ℚ, since f⁢(ℚ)⊆ℝ∖ℚ, but of course f⁢(ℝ) is countable.
