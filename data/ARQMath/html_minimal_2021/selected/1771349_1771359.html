Sum of 1/nk of the first log⁡P numbers

In a Udacity course I'm told the following: ∑i=1log2⁡(P)1/2i=(P-1)/P

I've checked that it's true by entering it into Wolfram Alpha: https://www.wolframalpha.com/input/?i=sum+1%2F2^n,+n%3D1+to+log%282,P%29

Could someone help me understand how the (P-1)/P result is derived?

summation
You can easily show that for any positive integer n we have ∑i=1n12i=1-12n. Now put n=log2⁡P.
Hint: Use the https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geometric_series and the fact that 2log2⁡P=P

In general S⁢(k)=∑i=1k12i⟹12⁢S⁢(k)=S⁢(k)-12+12k+1⟹S⁢(k)=1-12k

It follows that your sum is S⁢(log2⁡(P))=1-12log2⁡P=1-1P=P-1P

(of course this is only valid when 1.252<2. For general P you have to round down to the nearest power of 2.)
