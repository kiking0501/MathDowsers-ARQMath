Is a closed map from X to T1 proper?

f:X⁢→⁢Y is a closed map. Suppose that the inverse image of each point in Y is a compact subset of X. Show that f-1⁢(K) is compact in X whenever K is compact in Y.

This is my homework and I understand Y is a T1 space. But what should I do next?

general-topology

A map f:X→Y which is continuous and closed and has the property that all fibers (the preimages of points) are compact is called perfect. A map is called proper if the preimages of compact sets are compact. So you want to show that each perfect map is proper.
Note that for every B⊂Y, the restriction f′:f-1⁢(B)→B is again proper. In particular, if K⊆Y is compact, then f′:f-1⁢(K)→K is a perfect map with a compact target space. For that case, you can find a proof at Perfect Map p:X→Y, Y compact implies X compact which also shows that the continuity is not needed.
