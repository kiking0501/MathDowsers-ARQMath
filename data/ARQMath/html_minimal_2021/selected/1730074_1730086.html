There are a couple of things to point out here. Let M be a smooth manifold.

  • Every tangent vector at p may be thought of as a derivation at p: indeed, if vp is a tangent vector at p and f:U→𝐑 is a smooth function in a neighbourhood U⊆M of p, then, vp⁢(f) can be thought of as the derivative of f at p in the direction of v. More precisely, for a curve γ defined around 0 (so that γ⁢(0)=p) which represents the tangent vector vp, one sets:

vp⁢(f)=d⁢(f∘γ)d⁢t|t=0.

Firstly, this is well-defined: that is, the definition of vp is independent of the curve γ that "represents" vp. (This is "per definitionem", like everything is.)

The name comes from the fact that vp:C∞⁢(U)→𝐑 satisfies the identity vp⁢(f⁢g)=f⁢(p)⁢vp⁢(g)+g⁢(p)⁢vp⁢(f).

  • Equipped with the above interpretation as derivation at a point p, we may view a smooth vector field X as a derivation ℒX:C∞⁢(M)→C∞⁢(M) on the algebra C∞⁢(M) by setting ℒX⁢(f)⁢(p)=Xp⁢(f). (Check that this makes sense!)

See Lee's Introduction to Smooth Manifolds, a wonderful book on differential topology.
