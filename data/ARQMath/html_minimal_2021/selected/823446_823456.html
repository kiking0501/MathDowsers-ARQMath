Outline: In the interval (1/2,1) our function is -1.

In the interval (1/3,1/2), our function is 1.

In the interval (1/4,1/3), our function is -1.

In the interval (1/5,1/4), our function is 1.

And so on. The intervals have length 11⋅2, 12⋅3, 13⋅4, and so on.

So the integral ought to be -11⋅2+12⋅3-13⋅4+14⋅5-15⋅6+⋯.

If we want a closed form, note that ln⁡(1+x)=x-x22+x33-x44+x55-x66+⋯. Now calculate ∫01ln⁡(1+x)⁢𝑑x. This is the same as what we obtain when we integrate the series (1) term by term.
