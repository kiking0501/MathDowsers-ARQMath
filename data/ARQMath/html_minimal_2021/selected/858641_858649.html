Squeeze Theorem with Factorial

Is it correct to squeeze 3⁢n(n+1)! between 0 and 1n!? The proposed left side makes logical sense to me, however bounding the right hand side to prove the limit goes to 0 is giving me a bit more trouble.

calculus limits
Write 3⁢n=3⁢(n+1)-3.

Note that since n<n+1, one have that 0≤3⁢n(n+1)!≤3n! Using Squeeze Theorem, it can be easily seen that the limit is zero.
