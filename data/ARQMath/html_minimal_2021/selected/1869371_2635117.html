To proof using the element argument:

(A-B)∪(C-B)⊆(A∪C)-B

We start as follows

x∈(A-B)∩(C-B)

x∈(A∩B¯)∩(C∩B¯) difference law

(x∈A∧x∈B¯)∧(x∈C∧x∈B¯) def union

(x∈A∧x∈C)∧(x∈B¯∧x∈B¯) associative + commutative laws

(x∈A∧x∈C)∧x∈B¯ idempotency law

x∈(A∩C)∩B¯ def intersection

x∈(A∩C)-B difference law

And that concludes the proof.
