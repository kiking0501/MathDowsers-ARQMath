Parsing what you've said in the comments, we would normally say that -1212 is the '12'th root of -12. Of course this is a bit of a mouthful and I don't think anyone would actually say this. By the usual rules of surds, we have the equality -1212=(-12)1/12=(-12)2=14.

Thanks a lot, this answer is perfect. Could you also pl tell me why you say that such an expression is very rare?
Well, in practice people don't often take the xth root of something unless x is a natural number greater than 1. At least, we wouldn't say it in those terms. We're much more likely to talk about the 1/xth power instead, as it's perhaps less ambiguous, and easier to manipulate. Of course, if you're learning this stuff in class, what we do *in practice* might not matter - so you shouldn't listen to this if you need to learn something to pass a class.
