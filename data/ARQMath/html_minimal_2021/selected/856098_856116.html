To expand on my comments, let ϵ>0 be given, then our limit, in polar coordinates is of the form

f⁢(r,θ)=r5⁢cos3⁡θ⁢sin2⁡θr4⁢(cos4⁡θ+sin4⁡θ)

this satisfies

|f⁢(r,θ)-0|≤|r|⁢|cos3⁡θ⁢sin2⁡θcos4⁡θ+sin4⁡θ|≤rmθ=δmθ

with mθ the minimum value of cos4⁡θ+sin4⁡θ for 0≤θ<2⁢π. What is this minimum?

Taking derivatives, we see that it is possible when -4⁢cos3⁡θ⁢sin⁡θ+4⁢sin3⁡θ⁢cos⁡θ=0 (*)

three cases

(i) θ=0,π then the function's value is 1.

(ii) θ=π/2,3⁢π/2 again, value 1.

(iii) cos⁡θ⁢sin⁡θ≠0 then we have sin2⁡θ=cos2⁡θ by manipulating (*). This only happens when θ is on the line y=±x, whence both sin4⁡θ and cos4⁡θ are positive, so the minimum is a positive number, in fact it's easily seen to be mθ=12

Hence we choose δ=ϵ2 and we are guaranteed convergence to 0, as claimed.
