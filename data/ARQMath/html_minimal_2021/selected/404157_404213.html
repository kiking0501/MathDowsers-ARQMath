Since n and n5 have the same parity, f⁢(n):=n5-n is divisible by 2. It is also divisible by 5, since f⁢(0)=0 and f⁢(n+1)-f⁢(n)=…=5⁢n⁢(n3+2⁢n2+2⁢n+1). More generally, for every prime p, f⁢(n):=np-n is divisible by p, this is Fermat's little theorem. In fact, f⁢(n+1)-f⁢(n)=∑0<k<p(pk)⁢nk and p∣(pk) for 0<k<p.
