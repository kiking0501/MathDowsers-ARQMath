Let φ:L→L an automorphism. If φ fix a root, is it the identity?

Let L/K a field extension, φ:L⟶L an automorphism and α∈L. Let P the minimal polynomial of α over K⁢[X] and α1,…,αn all roots of P (not necessarily in L). I know the σ act transitively on the roots of P. I was curious about something (may be I'm wrong), but I have the intuition that if P fix one root of P in L, then it fix all roots in L, i.e. it's the identity. Is it true ore false ? And if it's false, is there a certain condition that yield this result true ? (I was wondering about something like normal extension).

If L is Galois, then this is true iff K⁢(α) already contains all the roots α1,…,αn (which it usually doesn't). Indeed, we may assume L=K⁢(α1,…,αn) since every automorphism of K⁢(α1,…,αn) extends to L. We are then asking whether every automorphism of L that fixes the subfield K⁢(α) is the identity. By the fundamental theorem of Galois theory, this is true iff K⁢(α)=L.

For an explicit example, let K=ℚ, let P⁢(x)=x4-2, let L⊂ℂ be the splitting field of K, and let α=24 (the positive real 4th root). Then there is an automorphism of L that swaps the two square roots of -2, but fixes the two square roots of 2 (including α).
