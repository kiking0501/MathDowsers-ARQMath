Consider the identity (1+x)2⁢n=(1+x)n⋅(1+x)n. By the binomial theorem we have (1+x)n=∑k=0n(nk)⁢xk, so multiplying out we compute the right hand side as (1+x)n⋅(1+x)n=∑k=02⁢n(∑j=0k(nj)⁢(nk-j)⁢xk). But the LHS is just (1+x)2⁢n=∑k=02⁢n(2⁢nk)⁢xk; equating coefficients of xn we get (2⁢nn)=∑j=0n(nj)⁢(nn-j). Finally, using the identity (nj)=(nn-j) gives the desired result.

(and use the identity that (ni)=(nn-i))
