If the first postulate is clear, then aeven-beven=a2⁢n-b2⁢n=(a2)n-(b2)n, which is divisible
through nℤ. But a2-b2=(a-b)⁢(a+b). It should then be obvious why this trick can
not be applied for odd values of the exponent, implying (3). As far as the second group is con-
cerned, try giving small values to n, such as 3 or 5, and compute (a+b)⁢(a2-a⁢b+b2) or
(a+b)(a4-a3b+a2b2-ab3+b4). Notice how the terms just cancel each other out, due to
sign-alternation, and all that remains is only the first and the last. Then try applying a similar
trick to even values of n, and notice how that would force the greatest powers of a and b to be
of opposite signs.
