Convergence of exponential of a Matrix

Let A be a square matrix. Is the exponential eA of A is always converging?

The answer is yes. Do you know what a matrix norm is? For instance, have you seen a definition such as $$ \|A\|_2 = \max_{\|x\| = 1} \|Ax\|? $$

We define eA=∑k=0∞1k!⁢Ak We wish to show that the sequence of partial sums SN=∑k=0N1k!⁢Ak converges to S=eA. To that end, note that for the (sub-multiplicative) matrix norm ∥⋅∥ of your choosing, we have ∥S-SN∥=∥∑k=N+1∞1k!⁢Ak∥≤∑k=N+1∞1k!⁢∥Ak∥≤∑k=N+1∞1k!⁢∥A∥k The bound on the right approaches zero since the sum ∑1k!⁢∥A∥k converges (to e∥A∥, in fact).
