Let V be a vector space over a field k with basis e1,…⁢en. The dual V∗ is defined to be the vector space consisting of all linear transformations from V to k. The definitions of addition and scalar multiplication in V∗ are obvious, as is the fact that V∗ is actually a vector space.

Every element in V can be uniquely written as a sum c1⁢e1+⋯+cn⁢en, where ci are in k. For each j, define a function ej∗:V→k by the formula ej∗⁢(c1⁢e1+⋯+en⁢vn)=cj You can check that for any x,y∈V, and any c∈k, that ej∗⁢(x+y)=ej∗⁢(x)+ej∗⁢(y), and ej∗⁢(c⁢x)=c⁢ej∗⁢(x). In other words, ej∗ is a linear transformation from V to k, so ej∗∈V∗.

The basic result is that the elements ei∗ form a basis for the vector space V∗. Is this what you're having trouble proving?
