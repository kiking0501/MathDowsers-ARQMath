Just to explain my earlier comment: This notion of the dimension of a topological space is one of many notions (see also small inductive dimension, large inductive dimension, and Lebesgue covering dimension). It is particularly well-suited to Noetherian spaces, and typically not that useful outside that context (for example, every Hausdorff space has dimension 0, since the only irreducible sets are points). So when talking about the dimension of a bare topological space, with no other context, you should always specify what you mean by dimension.
