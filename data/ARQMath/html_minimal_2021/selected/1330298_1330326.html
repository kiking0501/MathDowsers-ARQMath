Find the sum of reciprocals of divisors given the sum of divisors

Let d1,d2,⋯⁢dk be all the factors of a positive integer 'n' including 1 and n. Suppose d1+d2+d3+⋯+dk=72. Then find the value of 1d1+1d2+⋯+1dk.

Attempt: Consider, d1,d2,⋯⁢dk are the factors of n ascending order. Then

d1⁢dk=n, d2⁢dk-1=n, ⋯, dk⁢d1=n ............... (1)

Assume, 1d1+1d2+⋯+1dk=P Then nd1+nd2+⋯+ndk=n⁢P i.e dk+dk-1+⋯+d2+d1=n⁢P (using 1) i.e 72=n⁢P, or P=72/n.

Problem: I have considered that number of divisors of n are even as every divisor d of n has a twin divisor n/d. But it is not true if n is a perfect square i.e n=d2 as in this case n has odd number of divisors (for example, 4=22, 4 has three divisors 1,2&a⁢m⁢p;4).
