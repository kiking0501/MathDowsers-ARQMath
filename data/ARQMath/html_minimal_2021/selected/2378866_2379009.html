Everything you say is correct. You have the parametrization 𝐫⁢(θ,z)=(cos⁡θ,sin⁡θ,z), giving |𝐫θ×𝐫z|≡1, hence the surface element on S is given by d⁢ω=d⁢(θ,z). There is no Jacobian determinant here, but a scaling factor from the measure d⁢(θ,z) in the parameter plane to the surface measure d⁢ω in 3-space. This scaling factor happens to be 1 in the case at hand. You end up with the integral of your first attempt, obtained using "geometric intuition". Given the parametrization (1) the condition 0≤z≤1+x⁢y translates into 0≤z≤1+cos⁡θ⁢sin⁡θ.
