Two good sources are Gelfand, Shilov, Generalized functions, Volume 1, which gives a rigorous treatment and also has some very lucid explanations for the concepts, and Kanwal, Generalized Functions: Theory and Applications.

You need to impose some conditions on the supports of functionals f and g in order for f*g to be well-defined. If g is an ordinary locally integrable function, you take the functional induced by g. The definition will be (f*g,ϕ)=(f,x↦(g,y↦ϕ(x+y))), from which it's easy to find f*δ.
