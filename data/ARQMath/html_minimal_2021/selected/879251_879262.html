Below are the basic congruence rules. Let A,B,a,b be any integers.

Congruence Sum Rule A≡a,B≡b⇒\color⁢#⁢c0fA+B≡a+b   (mod⁢m)

Proof m|A-a,B-b⇒m|(A-a)+(B-b)=\color⁢#⁢c0fA+B-(a+b)

Congruence Product Rule A≡a,and B≡b⇒\color⁢blueAB≡ab   (mod⁢m)

Proof m|A-a,B-b⇒m|(A-a)⁢B+a⁢(B-b)=\color⁢blueAB-ab

Congruence Power Rule \color⁢A≡a⇒\color⁢#⁢c00An≡an (mod⁢m)  for all naturals n.

Proof For n=0 it's  1≡1 so true. A≡a,An≡an⇒\color⁢#⁢c00An+1≡an+1, by the Product Rule, so the result follows by induction on n.

Polynomial Congruence Rule If f⁢(x) is polynomial with integer coefficients then A≡a⇒f⁢(A)≡f⁢(a)(modm).

Proof By induction on n= degree f. Clear if n=0. Else f⁢(x)=f⁢(0)+x⁢g⁢(x) for g⁢(x) a polynomial with integer coefficients of degree &⁢lt;n. By induction g⁢(A)≡g⁢(a) so \color⁢#⁢0⁢a⁢0⁢A⁢g⁢(A)≡ag⁢(a) by the Product Rule. Hence f⁢(A)=f⁢(0)+\color⁢#⁢0⁢a⁢0⁢A⁢g⁢(A)≡f⁢(0)+\color⁢#⁢0⁢a⁢0⁢a⁢g⁢(a)=f⁢(a) by the Sum Rule.

Beware that such rules need not hold true for other operations, e.g. the exponential analog of above AB≡ab is not generally true (unless B=b, so it follows by the Power Rule, or the Polynomial Rule with f⁢(x)=xb.
