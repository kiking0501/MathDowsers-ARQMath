Subset of Power Set of Natural Numbers

I was given a question today which asks to find a subset of the power set of natural numbers having cardinality 2ℵ0 and in which any two subsets have finitely many elements in their intersection.

My solution was to consider the sequences of rationals converging to a real number, for every real, and then to take the bijection of those sequences of rationals to sequences of naturals.

Can one prove this more directly? That is, show an explicit subset of the power set?

What is more explicit than that, by the way? You can take a very specific subset of rationals for each irrational (the continued fraction expansion) if you want to avoid seeming to use choice. (For the rational q, you can just use {q-2-k}.)

For each infinite set of integers A⊂ω, associate the infinite set of integers SA={∑i∈A∩n2i:n<ω}. If i∈A∖B then all but finitely many elements of SA have a "1" in the i-th place in their binary expansion, and no elements of SB do, so SA∩SB is finite. It remains to observe that the are continuum many infinite sets of integers A⊂ω.
