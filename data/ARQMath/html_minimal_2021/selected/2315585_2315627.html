In number theory, you should always separate what is convergent and what is divergent.

Define the constant α=∑p,k≥2ln⁡ppk By comparison with ∑n≥11n2=∑n≤x1n2+𝒪⁢(1/x) we have ∑pk≤x,k≥2ln⁡ppk=α+𝒪⁢(1/x),∑p≤x,k≥2ln⁡ppk=α+𝒪⁢(1/x)

So that

∑p≤xln⁡pp-1=∑p≤x∑k≥1ln⁡ppk=∑p≤xln⁡pp+∑p≤x∑k≥2ln⁡ppk=∑p≤xln⁡pp+α+𝒪⁢(1/x)

And ∑n≤xΛ⁢(n)n=∑p≤xln⁡pp+∑pk≤x,k≥2ln⁡ppk=∑p≤xln⁡pp+α+𝒪⁢(1/x)
