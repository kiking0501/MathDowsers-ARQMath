Hi the Optimal Sampling Theorem works fine here because you don't need bounded stopping times if you know that the process X is uniformly integrable. Indeed in that case Optimal Sampling Theorem works for every stopping times (even unbounded ones) and your argument is simplified.

As you may know a (right-)continuous martingale (Xt) is uniformly integrable if and only if it is converging in L1 as t→+∞ which is the case as Xt converges almost surely to 0. (reference "Karatzas and Shreve Brownian motion and stochastic calculus" problem 3.20 page 18 condition a and b)).

Let's apply fully this idea, and let's denote Ta the first time X attains a (with a>X0>0 so we discard the trivial case) which is a stopping time but not a bounded one. Nevertheless we don't mind because X is UI, so that by we have by Optimal Sampling Theroem :

X0=E[X0]=E[XTa]=a.P[Ta<∞]+X∞.P[Ta=∞]
=a.P[Ta<∞]+0.P[Ta=∞]=a.P[sups≥0(Xs)≥a]

Which leads to your result ( taking the sup with 1 if x≤X0).
