Combinatorial proof of ∑i=0n(ni)2=(2⁢nn) by using a binary string of length 2⁢n

I was wondering if there is any combinatorial proof of ∑i=0n(ni)2=(2⁢nn) It is obvious that the RHS of the equation counts the number of binary strings of length 2⁢n with exactly n 1's, but how about the LHS?

binomial-theorem combinatorial-proofs

Suppose that we have n girls and n boys, and we want to pick n people out of them. One way is (2⁢nn). Another way is to pick i girls, and n-i boys, which is (ni)×(nn-i)=(ni)2. Summation over i gives LHS.
