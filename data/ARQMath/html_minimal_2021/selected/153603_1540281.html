Pick any point inside the square. The distances from the corners to that point have the relation a2+c2=b2+d2 where the lines to that point are labeled a,b,c,d going clockwise. Finding any point inside the unit square where all distances from the corners to that point are rational is an unsolved problem.

If you did find such a point you would find integer solutions to those distances and the diophantine equation above. You also would have found eight pythagorean triple triangles that can be assembled into a square.
