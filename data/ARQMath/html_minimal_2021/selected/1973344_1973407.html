No, your second to last equality of limits is not trivially true. At that point you do not even know yet whether the limit of the difference quotient exists.

Hints:

  • For x sufficiently close to c we have f′⁢(x) bounded in an interval based on L±ε.
  • This implies that f⁢(c) must be found between the lines with slopes L-ε and L+ε through (x,f⁢(x)) for x sufficiently close to c. Here the continuity of f should play a role.
  • Since this can be achieved for all ε>0 the conclusion should follow.

Perhaps someone else will provide a completely different approach, but this was my intuition regarding this problem.
