That contour of integration is equivalent to the contour consisting of a circle C0 of radius ϵ around the origin and two half-lines C+=(∞→ϵ) and C-=(ϵ→∞).

  • The value of the integrand at the point z=-ϵ on the circle is real and negative.

  • Turning by π counterclockwise around the origin produces a phase factor ei⁢π⁢s, since on the corresponding demi-circle x is parameterized as x=ϵ⁢ei⁢φ, φ∈[π,2⁢π]. The integral over C- will therefore be given by -ei⁢π⁢s⁢∫ϵ∞xs-1⁢d⁢xex-1.

  • Similarly, turning by π clockwise, the integral over C+ gives e-i⁢π⁢s⁢∫ϵ∞xs-1⁢d⁢xex-1.

  • Now let ϵ→0, then the integral over C0 vanishes whereas (1) and (2) give the quoted result.
