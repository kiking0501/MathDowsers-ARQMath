More generally, if R is a commutative ring, then (R⁢[x]/(xn+1))* is the direct product of the subgroups R* and 1+(x) (as a multiplicative group!). This can be verified directly.

Key observation #1:

If n is a nilpotent element of a ring, then the geometric sum formula 11-n=1+n+n2+n3+⋯ shows that 1-n is invertible.

Key observation #2:

In your ring the constant term of the product of cosets of a0+a1⁢x+a2⁢x2+⋯ and b0+b1⁢x+⋯ is a0⁢b0.

[Edit:] The point of these observations was to identify the set of units of your ring. Compare with Martin Brandenburg's comment! [/Edit]
