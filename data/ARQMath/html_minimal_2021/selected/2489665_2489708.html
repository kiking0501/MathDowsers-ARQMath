The archimedean property argument is incorrect, for example, let ϵ=12.
Unfortunately, **not**: Your proof is not correct. Archimedean property does not imply that there exists n such that n⁢ϵ>nk+1, since both sides depend on n. After all, this would imply ϵ>nk≥1, which is a contradiction whenever ϵ<1.

You must prove that for any ε>0 there exists N∈ℕ such that for n>N it happens that nkan<ε This is quite hard to prove directly. To prove that limn→∞⁡nkan=0 We can use L'Hopital rule k times and find that limn→∞⁡nkan=limn→∞⁡k⁢nk-1an⁢(log⁡a)=…=limn→∞⁡k!an⁢(log⁡a)k=0
