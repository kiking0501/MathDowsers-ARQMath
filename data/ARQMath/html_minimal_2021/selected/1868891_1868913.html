Let P,Q the subgroups with order p,q. As @Jyrki indicated earlier these are the only nontrivial subgroups of G. Let a∈P,b∈Q have orders p,q. Consider the element a⁢b. Since p,q are relatively prime a is not a power of b and vice versa. Then a⁢b is not in P,Q. Since a⁢b is an element of G, the order of a⁢b must divide the order of G. Then the order of a⁢b is in {1,p,q,p⁢q}. As a,b are in different groups they are not inverses so it is not 1. a⁢b is not in P or Q so a⁢b does not have order p or q and therefore must have order p⁢q. Then G is generated by a⁢b. Then G is cyclic.
