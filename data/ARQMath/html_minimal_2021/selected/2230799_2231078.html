You might want to start by drawing the graph of the function f defined by f⁢(x)=2+1x on x>0, just to understand what is going on.
Moreover: before going for monotonicity, try to compute the first few terms to get a sense of what may happen. (They are *not* monotone.)
It oscillates and converges to 1+2.
Interestingly, WolframAlpha obtains an explicit solution to this recurrence relation: https://www.wolframalpha.com/input/?i=x%5Bn+%2B+1%5D+%3D%3D+2+%2B+1%2Fx%5Bn%5D,+x%5B1%5D+%3D%3D+2. The convergence to 1+2 is then obvious. But having to know the explicit solution seems like overkill!
You can find several posts about this sequence on this site. For example, by [searching for xn+1=2+1xn](https://approach0.xyz/search/?q=%24x_%7Bn%2B1%7D%3D2%2B%5Cdfrac%7B1%7D%7Bx_%7Bn%7D%7D%24&p=1) in Approach0.
