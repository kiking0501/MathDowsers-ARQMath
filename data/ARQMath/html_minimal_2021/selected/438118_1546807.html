johan paul, Edited answer

The first Borel-Cantelli lemma yields ℙ⁢(lim supn→∞⁡|Xn|>n)=0. As for each n {|Xn|>n}⊂⋃k=n∞{|Xk|>k}, it follows that \begin{align} 0&=\mathbb P\left(\bigcap_{n=1}^\infty\bigcup_{k=n}^\infty \{|X_k|>k\}\right) \\&=\mathbb P\left(\lim_{n\to\infty}\bigcup_{k=n}^\infty \{|X_k|>k\} \right)\\ &=\lim_{n\to\infty}\mathbb P\left(\bigcup_{k=n}^\infty \{|X_k|>k\} \right)\\ &\geqslant\lim_{n\to\infty}\mathbb P(|X_n|>n) \end{align} and hence limn→∞⁡ℙ⁢(|Xn|>n)=0. Further, ⋂k=n∞{|Xk|>k}⊂{|Xn|>n} so that \begin{align} \mathbb P\left(\limsup_{n\to\infty} |X_n|\leqslant n\right) &= \mathbb P\left(\bigcap_{n=1}^\infty\bigcup_{k=n}^\infty\{|X_k|\leqslant k\}\right)\\ &=1 - \mathbb P\left(\bigcup_{n=1}^\infty\bigcap_{k=n}^\infty \{|X_k|>k\} \right)\\ &=1 - \mathbb P\left(\lim_{n\to\infty} \bigcap_{k=n}^\infty\{|X_k|>k\} \right)\\ &=1 - \lim_{n\to\infty}\mathbb P\left(\bigcap_{k=n}^\infty\{|X_k|>k\}\right)\\ &\geqslant1 - \lim_{n\to\infty}\mathbb P(|X_n|>n)\\ &= 1, \end{align} which implies that ℙ⁢(lim supn→∞⁡|Xn|n⁢\leqslant⁢1)=1.
