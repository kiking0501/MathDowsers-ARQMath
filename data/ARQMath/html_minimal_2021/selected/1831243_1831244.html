Is it ok to use Kronecker delta function to find if one of its variables belongs to a half open interval?

Kronecker "delta" function is generally defined as δ⁢(i,j)=1 if i is equal to j, otherwise 0.

How about if j is not an integer? I mean let j is a half open interval defined as j=(0,1] and i has any value on interval [0,1], then can we use Kronecker delta to find if i belongs to j? In other words, can we define like: δ⁢(i,j)=1 if i is in j, 0 otherwise?

What you are looking for is the indicator function: 𝟏A⁢(x)={1a⁢m⁢p;if ⁢x∈A0a⁢m⁢p;otherwise In your case, A=(0,1] (I have used x instead of i and A instead of j since it is more customary to use lower case letters at the end of the alphabet for real numbers and upper case letters for sets).

Note, as asmeurer points out, that the Kronecker delta δi,j can be written in terms of the indicator function: δi,j=𝟏{j}⁢(i)=𝟏{i}⁢(j).

That having been said, the answer to your original question is yes, since there is nothing stopping you from defining δi,j=𝟏j⁢(i) when j is a subset of the real numbers and i is a real number, but this might confuse your readers, and thus I strongly recommend against it.

Worth pointing out that δi,j is a special-case of the indicator function, 𝟏{j}⁢(i).
