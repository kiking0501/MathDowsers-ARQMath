algorithms divisibility
For each integer in your chosen range, write down the prime factorization of each one. Note which prime factors will be sufficient to produce a product that represents each of the integers. The product of the ones that do this will be the smallest number evenly divisible by all the integers. That is the process of finding a "least common multiple", as **SwapnilTri** notes below. For 1 through 10, these are 2,3,2(⋅2),5,(2⋅3),7,2(⋅2⋅2),3(⋅3),(2⋅5). The product of what is not in parentheses is 23⋅32⋅5⋅7= 2520 .
