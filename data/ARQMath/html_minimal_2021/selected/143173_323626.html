In fact, one advantage of the argument in john w.'s answer is that it can be extended further to prove (a+b)n≤pn⁢an+qn⁢bn, where 1p+1q=1. In particular, one can choose the coefficient of an very close to 1 by paying the price of a larger coefficient of bn.

The proof is achieved by arguing either (a+b)≤p⁢a or (a+b)≤q⁢b holds. This is true since otherwise one would get a contradiction that (1p+1q)⁢a+b>a+b.
