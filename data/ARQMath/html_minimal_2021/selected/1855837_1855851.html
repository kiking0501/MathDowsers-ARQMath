Your proof is fine.

Here is a more concise proof:

(a+b,a-b)=(a+b-(a-b),a-b)=(2⁢b,a-b)=(2⁢b,a+b)

(a,b)=(a+b,b)=(b,a+b)

It is easy to show that (2⁢x,y)≥(x,y)

Therefore (a+b,a-b)=(2⁢b,a+b)≥(b,a+b)=(a,b)

The OP's proof is both more concise and general: it implies that if ni=ji⁢a+ki⁢b for integers j,k then (a,b)∣a,b ⇒ (a,b)∣n1,n2 therefore (a,b)≤(n1,n2) because every common divisor is ≤ *greatest* common divisor. Further (a,b)∣(n1,n2) since c∣n1,n2⇔c∣(n1,n2), which is the [universal property of the gcd](http://math.stackexchange.com/a/1189430/242) (= the definition in general rings).
