Firstly, note that your proof must be wrong, because if it weren't, we could simply take k=1, go through the same process, and prove that the harmonic series converges, which is not true. The error, specifically, is at the end: the ratio test requires more than that the ratio is less than 1 for all n: it requires that there is some l<1 (constant for all n) such that the ratio is less than l for all n. None of these sequences satisfy this, since (nk(n+1)k)→1 as n→∞.

Instead, you can use any of the methods here (which it's very unlikely that anybody expected you to produce on your own, but the second (by Harald Hanche-Olsen) is probably the one that's most reasonable), or the Cauchy Condensation Test.
