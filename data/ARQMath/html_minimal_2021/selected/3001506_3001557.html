Very roughly, "uniformly continuous" means "having bounded derivative".
Indeed, the function f⁢(x)=1/x will be uniformly continuous on any interval of the form (ε,∞) for any ε>0. The failure to be uniformly continuous only happens in a neighborhood of 0, so if you prevent the variable from being able to get too close, you ensure uniform continuity (see Lord Shark the Unknown's comment, too).
Though as a caution on taking Lord Shark the Unknown's comment too literally - for example, f⁢(x)=x and f⁢(x)=x⁢sin⁡(1/x) are both uniformly continuous on (0,1) even though the derivative is unbounded in both cases.
