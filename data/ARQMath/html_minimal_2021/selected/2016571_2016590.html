Many ways to do this. The best way I think involves this lovely identity: n3-(n-1)3=3⁢n2-3⁢n+1

Hence, we just do: \begin{split} n^3 & = n^3-(n-1)^3+(n-1)^3-(n-2)^3 + \ldots + 1^3-0^3 \\ & = (3n^2-3n+1) + (3(n-1)^2-3(n-1)+1) + \ldots + (3\cdot 1^2 -2\cdot 1 + 1) \\ &= 3 \sum_{i=1}^n i^2 -3\sum_{i=1}^n i + n \end{split} Given that we know that ∑i=1ni=n⁢(n+1)2, we get: ∑i=1ni2=n3-n3+n⁢(n+1)2=n⁢(n+1)⁢(2⁢n+1)6 Now, a change of indices does it: put j=i-1, then j goes from 0 to n-1: ∑j=0n-1j2=n⁢(n-1)⁢(2⁢n-1)6
