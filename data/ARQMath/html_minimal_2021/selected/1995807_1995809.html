Prove that π has 2 digits that repeat infinite number of times

How can I prove that π has 2 digits repeating an infinite number of times in its decimal expansion? Proving that 1 digit repeats an infinite number of times is easy – π is irrational, and by the definition of irrational number, a digit must be repeated an infinite number of times?

HINT: Assume only one digit appears infinitely many times, then after finitely many decimal places we will have only the repeating digit appearing. This means that π is periodic (has a repeating decimal pattern), hence it's rational, which is a contradiction.
