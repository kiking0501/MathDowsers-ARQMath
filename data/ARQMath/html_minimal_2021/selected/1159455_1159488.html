It is fairly approachable if you split up according to whether Q is true or false.

For conciseness, call the two formulas be Φ and Ψ. We can show that each of them is equivalent to (R∧Q)∨(¬⁢P∧¬⁢Q), and therefore they are equivalent to each other.

More precisely, I will show that Ξ∧Q≡¬⁢R∧Q and Ξ∧¬⁢Q≡¬⁢P∧¬⁢Q, for Ξ∈{Φ,Ψ}. We then have Ξ≡a⁢m⁢p;Ξ∧(Q∨¬⁢Q)≡a⁢m⁢p;(Ξ∧Q)∨(Ξ∧¬⁢Q)≡a⁢m⁢p;(R∧Q)∨(¬⁢P∧¬⁢Q)

Thus there are four computations to do: Φ∧Q≡amp;(P→Q)∧(Q→R)∧Q≡amp;(P→Q)∧Q∧R≡a⁢m⁢p;Q∧R

Φ∧¬⁢Q≡amp;(P→Q)∧(Q→R)∧¬Q≡amp;(P→Q)∧(¬Q∨R)∧¬Q≡amp;(P→Q)∧¬Q≡amp;(¬Q→¬P)∧¬Q≡a⁢m⁢p;¬⁢P∧¬⁢Q

Ψ∧Q≡amp;(P→R)∧(P↔Q∨R↔Q)∧Q≡amp;(P→R)∧((P↔Q∧Q)∨(R↔Q)∧Q))≡amp;(P→R)∧((P∧Q)∨(R∧Q))≡amp;(P→R)∧(P∨R)∧Q≡a⁢m⁢p;(¬⁢P∨R)∧(P∨R)∧Q≡a⁢m⁢p;((¬⁢P∧P)∨R)∧Q≡amp;(⊥∨R)∧Q≡a⁢m⁢p;R∧Q

Ψ∧¬⁢Q≡amp;(P→R)∧(P↔Q∨R↔Q)∧¬Q≡amp;(P→R)∧((P↔Q∧¬Q)∨(R↔Q)∧¬Q))≡amp;(P→R)∧((¬P∧¬Q)∨(¬R∧¬Q))≡amp;(P→R)∧(¬P∨¬R)∧¬Q≡a⁢m⁢p;(¬⁢P∨R)∧(¬⁢P∨¬⁢R)∧¬⁢Q≡a⁢m⁢p;(¬⁢P∨(R∧¬⁢R))∧¬⁢Q≡amp;(¬P∨⊥)∧¬Q≡a⁢m⁢p;¬⁢P∧¬⁢Q
