How to prove ⌊⌊ab⌋c⌋=⌊ab×c⌋

If a,b,c are positive integers, how to prove ⌊⌊ab⌋c⌋=⌊ab×c⌋

integers

Given any integer n and m>0, the remainder theorem says there are unique integers q,r so that n=q⁢m+r;0≤r<m. It's easy to verify that [nm]=q. (Because q⁢m≤n=q⁢m+r<q⁢m+m so q≤nm<q+1).

So....

Let a=b⁢k+r;0≤r<b. Let k=c⁢m+s;0≤s<c.

Then [ab]=k and [[ab]c]=m.

a=(c⁢m+s)⁢b+r=m⁢(b⁢c)+(s⁢b+r) where 0≤s⁢b+r≤(c-1)⁢b+r<b⁢c. So [ab⁢c]=m.
