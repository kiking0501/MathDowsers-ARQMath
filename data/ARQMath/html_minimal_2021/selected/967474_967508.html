I take it by "complete" you mean that the fn have dense linear span or, equivalently, they have zero orthogonal complement. This we can see as follows. Suppose that ⟨g,fn⟩=0 for all n. Then |⟨g,en⟩|=|⟨g,en-fn⟩|≤∥g∥⁢∥en-fn∥. Take squares and sum over n. This yields ∥g∥2≤S⁢∥g∥2 where S<1 is the sum from your assumption. Thus g=0, as desired.
