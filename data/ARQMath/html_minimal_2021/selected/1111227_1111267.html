integration

In=∫-ππsin⁡n⁢x(1+2x)⁢sin⁡x⁢𝑑x=∫0πsin⁡n⁢x(1+2x)⁢sin⁡x⁢𝑑x+∫-π0sin⁡n⁢x(1+2x)⁢sin⁡x⁢𝑑x

Make the substitution x→-x in the second integral, then it becomes:

In=∫0πsin⁡n⁢x(1+2x)⁢sin⁡x⁢𝑑x+∫0πsin⁡n⁢x(1+2-x)⁢sin⁡x⁢𝑑x=∫0πsin⁡n⁢xsin⁡x⁢𝑑x

For n≥2,

In-In-2=∫0πsin⁡n⁢x-sin⁡(n-2)⁢xsin⁡x⁢𝑑x=2⁢∫0πcos⁡(n-1)⁢x⁢𝑑x=0

Hence, In=0 if n is even and π if n is odd.
