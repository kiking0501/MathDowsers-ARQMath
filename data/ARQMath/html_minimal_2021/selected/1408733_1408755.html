It's not true that any homomorphism from S1 to ℝ must be trivial. As an abstract abelian group, ℝ is just an uncountable-dimensional ℚ-vector space, and hence ℝ/ℤ is an uncountable-dimensional ℚ-vector space plus ℚ/ℤ (the subgroup of roots of unity). Killing this extra factor leaves you with lots of interesting homomorphisms S1→ℝ (almost none of which are continuous).

Ext1⁢(-,-) sends direct sums in the first variable to direct products, and as mentioned in the comments, as an abstract abelian group

S1≅(⊕Xℚ)⊕ℚ/ℤ

where X is uncountable. So it suffices to compute Ext1⁢(ℚ,ℤ) and Ext1⁢(ℚ/ℤ,ℤ). The former group turns out to be an uncountable-dimensional rational vector space, while the latter is a bit more complicated: see these notes and this paper for details.
