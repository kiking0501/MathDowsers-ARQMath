Maple gives an answer of π2⁢ln⁡(2).
Integrate by part first. If you don't know how to compute the remaining integral, look at answers of this [question](http://math.stackexchange.com/q/37829/59379).

Let y=arcsin⁡x⇒sin⁡y=x⇒cos⁡y⁢d⁢y=d⁢x, then ∫01arcsin⁡(x)x⁢𝑑x=∫0π2y⁢cot⁡y⁢d⁢y. Now use IBP by taking u=y and d⁢v=cot⁡y⁢d⁢y⇒v=ln⁡(sin⁡x), then \begin{align} \int_0^{\Large\frac\pi2}y\ \cot y\ dy&=\left.y\ln(\sin y)\right|_0^{\Large\frac\pi2}-\int_0^{\Large\frac\pi2}\ln(\sin y)\ dy\\ &=-\int_0^{\Large\frac\pi2}\ln(\sin y)\ dy. \end{align} The last integral can be evaluated by using property ∫abf⁢(x)⁢𝑑x=∫abf⁢(a+b-x)⁢𝑑x. We obtain ∫0π2ln⁡(sin⁡y)⁢𝑑y=-π2⁢ln⁡2, where ∫0π2ln⁡(sin⁡y)⁢𝑑y=∫0π2ln⁡(cos⁡y)⁢𝑑y ⇒ by symmetry. Thus ∫01arcsin⁡(x)x⁢𝑑x=π2⁢ln⁡2.
