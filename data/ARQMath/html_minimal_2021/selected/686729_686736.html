Some differentiable functions such as sin⁡x and cos⁡x have something called a Taylor Series expansion. The Taylor Series expansion is a way to represent the function using an infinite series (which are calculated from a differentiable point of the function). Taylor series is a type of power series and uses the following formula: f⁢(a)+f′⁢(a)1!⁢(x-a)+…+f(n)⁢(a)n!⁢(x-a)n+…=∑n=0∞f(n)⁢(a)n!⁢(x-a)n, where a is the point of a function that is infinitely differentiable.
