How to solve equations of the form zn=f⁢(c,i), z⁢ ∈ℂ?

That is, the L.H.S. is a complex number to nth power and the R.H.S. is some expression involving constant(s) c and i.

Taking the nth root seems to be problematic.
Is e.g. 1+i even defined?

if you have lhs , rhs you can just square and then perform algebra

Any expression involving some constants and i can be written as r⁢ei⁢θ. This way, the problem becomes zn=r⁢ei⁢θ, which we can solve by simply taking the n-th root. The solutions will then be {rn⁢ei⁢(θ+2⁢k⁢πn)|k∈{0,1,⋯,n-1}} Also, yes, i+1 is definitely defined, and it is, in polar coordinates, equal to 24⁢ei⁢π8.
