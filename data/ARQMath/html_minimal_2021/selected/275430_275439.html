HINTS: To show that f is continuous at an irrational x, show for any n∈ℤ+ you can choose δ>0 small enough so that the interval (x-δ,x+δ) contains no fraction with a denominator ≤n in lowest term. Use the fact that between two rationals with denominator m there is a gap of at least 1m.

To show that x is discontinuous at a rational mn, let ϵ=mn (or any smaller positive real), and just observe that every non-empty open interval in ℝ contains an irrational number.
