How many integers m such that Euler Totient Function φ⁢(m) = a given integer?

(I didn't find any other posts related to this but I think this is a weird because my question seems like a very natural one.)

Let φ denote the Euler Totient Function. Given m an even number, can we find all the positive integers n such that φ⁢(n)=m? If not, at least can we tell how many there are? Are there infinite? Why?

I strongly believe this is a hard task.

Thank you in advance.

You are looking at the inverse of the totient function, which happens to be multi-valued. Do have a look at this similar question on the number of n which satisfies ϕ⁢(n)=m for a given m: http://mathoverflow.net/questions/109509/the-inverse-of-the-euler-totient-function
There won't be infinitely many solutions. There can't be any prime factors of bigger than m+1, and for each other prime p, if pk>m+1 then pk+1 cannot be a factor of n.

Finite. φ⁢(n)≥n2
