Here's a simplification of Zhen Lin's argument. We know (p→s)→(q→p) Contrapose this: (q∧¬⁢p)→(p∧¬⁢s) Now I'm going to prove q→p by contradiction. We assume its negation q∧¬⁢p and from this and (B) by modus ponens we can then conclude p∧¬⁢s and in particular p. However (C) also trivially implies ¬⁢p which is a contradiction.

Thus, given (A) I have proved q→p (And in contrast to Zhen I didn't even need the ¬⁢p premise to do so. On the other hand, my proof is not intuitionistically valid).

Now the story explicitly tells us ¬⁢p. Therefore, by modus tollens and (D), we have ¬⁢q. Q.E.D.
