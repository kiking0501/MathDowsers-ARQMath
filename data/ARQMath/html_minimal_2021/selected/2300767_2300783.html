x is defined as the nonnegative square root of x when x≥0. However, as ℂ is not ordered, we cannot distinguish i and -i by signs. The imaginary unit is preferably defined as the number such that i2=-1. In many situations, like solving polynomial equations with real coefficients, it is perfectly ok to replace i by -i. So -1=i is just a convention, in order to keep the notation single-valued.

There are two imaginary numbers z such that z2=-1. The crux of the definition of i is that you choose one of those two number as i (and, as you point out, everything looks the same with either choice). That doesn’t quite come across in your answer.
