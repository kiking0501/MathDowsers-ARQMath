To provide an alternate, maybe somewhat too over-loaded proof of this fact: every finite division ring is commutative.
It amounts to the same thing as showing that the Brauer group of any finite field is trivial, for then the finite division rings are all matrix rings. Since they are division rings, this implies that they are fields.
