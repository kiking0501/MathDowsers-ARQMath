Linearity of the Lie product in each component, and the cyclic format of the Jacobi identify, allows you to deal with four cases.

  1. x,y,z∈L, that's Jacobi in L.

  2. x,y,z∈D, that's Jacobi in D.

  3. x,y∈L, and z∈D, that's the fact that the elements of D are derivations of L.

  4. x∈L, and y,z∈D, that's the fact that D is a Lie subalgebra of Der⁡(L).
