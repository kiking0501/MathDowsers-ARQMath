I personally think *How to prove it* is less formal on its logic part. If one wants to learn the logic on the right way, see "Logic, Sets and Recursion" by Robert Causey. Just read the Chapter 1 of that book, you'll know why I consider it to be more rigorous, formal and "right".

While not exactly an introduction to set theory per se I considered An Introduction to Proofs and the Mathematical Vernacular by Martin V. Day to be of great help in getting acquainted with a number of concepts and notations.
Although it's focus lies on reading, writing and checking proofs it provides all the necessary information to understand all those proofs. In my opinion it manages to keep the language simple and aids students to find their way by pointing out important caveats.
