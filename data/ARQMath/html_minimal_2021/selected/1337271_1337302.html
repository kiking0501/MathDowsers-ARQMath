x0,x1,…⁢xn are distinct points and A⁢(x)=∏j=0n(x-xj), Ak⁢(x)=∏j=0,j≠kn(x-xj), Lk⁢(x)=Ak⁢(x)Ak⁢(xk). Prove each of the following:
a) ∑k=0nLk⁢(x)=1 and ∑k=0nAk′⁢(x)A′⁢(xk)=0 for all x
b)∑k=0n1A′⁢(xk)=0. [Hint:Use part a with suitable values of x]

lagrange-interpolation

Note first that A⁢(x)=Ak⁢(x)⁢(x-xk), so differentiating both sides and putting x=xk we get

A′⁢(xk)=Ak⁢(xk).

Thus ∑k=0n1A′⁢(xk)=∑k=0n1Ak⁢(xk) is to be found.

Now ∑k=0nLk⁢(x)=∑k=0nAk⁢(x)Ak⁢(xk)

Since ∑k=0nLk⁢(x)=1 as a polynomial in x, we can look at the coefficient of xn in this polynomial and equate it to 0.

Each Ak⁢(x)=∏j=0j≠kn(x-xj) is a monic polynomial of degree n so coefficient of xn in Ak⁢(x) is 1.

Thus coefficient of xn in ∑k=0nLk⁢(x) is ∑k=0n1Ak⁢(xk) and is thus equal to 0.
