Every equivalence relation on ℤ that is compatible with the addition structure is either the identity or the relation ≡(modn)

Let R be an equivalence relation on ℤ such that the operation on the quotient set ℤ/R given by the rule [x]R+[y]R=[x+y]R is well-defined. Show that R must either be the identity relation (x⁢R⁢y⇔x=y) or the relation "mod n" for some n.

It's easy to show that the two relations satisfy the operations on the quotient set ℤ/R. I'm having trouble proving there does not exist another relation such that it satisfies operations on the quotient set.
