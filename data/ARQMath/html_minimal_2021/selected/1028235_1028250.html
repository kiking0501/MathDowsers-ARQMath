Linearity of a linear transformation

I know that for a transformation to be linear it must satisfy these two conditions:

f⁢(x+y)=f⁢(x)+f⁢(y)

f⁢(α⁢x)=α⁢f⁢(x)

Why the transformation is called linear? What does being linear mean? Where the linearity comes from?

linear-algebra

This comes from the fact that applications like x↦a⁢x verify the conditions you mention. And such applications represent lines, therefore the term linear.
