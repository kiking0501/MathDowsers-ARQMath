The limit can be reduced to limx→1⁡xx-1x-1⁢1x+1 and the first factor is the derivative at 1 of the function f⁢(x)=xx, so there's no real way to avoid derivatives in a way or the other. The second factor has limit 1/2, so it doesn't bother too much.

Since f⁢(x)=ex⁢log⁡x, the derivative is f′⁢(x)=ex⁢log⁡x⁢(1+log⁡x) and so f′⁢(1)=1.

Otherwise you can substitute x=1+t and consider limt→0⁡e(1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t)-1t The numerator can be expanded as 1+(1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t)+o⁢((1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t))-1=(1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t)+o⁢((1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t)) Now (1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t)=(1+t)⁢(t+o⁢(t))=t+o⁢(t) and so o⁢((1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t))=o⁢(t); thus the limit is limt→0⁡e(1+t)⁢log⁡(1+t)-1t=limt→0⁡t+o⁢(t)t=1 and therefore limx→1⁡xx-1x-1⁢1x+1=1⋅12=12
