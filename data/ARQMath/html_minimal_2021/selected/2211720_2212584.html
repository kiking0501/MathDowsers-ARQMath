I hope you know the formula for f and yes you don't need to actually know the formula for f-1. Just put x=f⁢(t) and the integral becomes ∫f-1⁢(a)f-1⁢(b)t⁢f′⁢(t)⁢𝑑t This requires that you know the values of f-1⁢(a),f-1⁢(b). They may be found in certain cases even without deriving a general formula for f-1.

But note that this will never be in terms of ∫abf⁢(x)⁢𝑑x but rather in terms of ∫f-1⁢(a)f-1⁢(b)f⁢(x)⁢𝑑x.
