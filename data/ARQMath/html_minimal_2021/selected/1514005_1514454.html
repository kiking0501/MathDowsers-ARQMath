(Complete revamp of my previous answer.)

Extend f by f⁢(x)=f⁢(-x) for x∈[-π,0), then f is 2⁢π periodic and so for any ϵ>0 there is a trigonometric polynomial q such that ∥f-q∥∞=sup|x|≤π⁡|f⁢(x)-q⁢(x)|<ϵ. (Recall that a trigonometric polynomial is of the form q⁢(t)=a0+∑k=1n(ak⁢cos⁡(k⁢t)+bk⁢sin⁡(k⁢t))). (See, for example, Rudin's "Real & Complex Analysis", Theorem 4.25.)

Let q~⁢(t)=12⁢(q⁢(t)+q⁢(-t)) and note that ∥f-q~∥∞≤12⁢(∥f-q∥+∥f-q∥)<ϵ (where we used evenness of f for the last part). Hence we may assume that q is even to begin with, in particular, q has the form q⁢(t)=a0+∑k=1nak⁢cos⁡(k⁢t)=∑k=0nak⁢cos⁡(k⁢t).

Note that, by assumption, ∫-ππf⁢(x)⁢q⁢(x)⁢𝑑x=0.

Then ∫-ππf2⁢(x)⁢𝑑x=∫-ππf⁢(x)⁢(f⁢(x)-q⁢(x)+q⁢(x))⁢𝑑x=∫-ππf⁢(x)⁢(f⁢(x)-q⁢(x))⁢𝑑x, hence ∫-ππf2⁢(x)⁢𝑑x≤∥f∥∞⁢2⁢π⁢ϵ. Since ϵ>0 was arbitrary, we see that ∫-ππf2⁢(x)⁢𝑑x=0. Since f is continuous, and f2⁢(x)≥0 for all x we see that f⁢(x)=0 for all x.
