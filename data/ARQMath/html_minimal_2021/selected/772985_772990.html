Proving gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)>1 ⇒ p1∣a xor p2∣a using Euclid's Lemma

Consider the lemma: If gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)>1, then either p1∣a or p2∣a.

How can this be proved using Euclid's Lemma?

proof-writing

The only divisors of p1⁢p2 are 1, p1, p2, and p1⁢p2.

Therefore if gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)≠1 then either gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)=p1 or gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)=p2 or gcd⁡(a,p1⁢p2)=p1⁢p2.

Hence either p1∣a or p2∣a or p1⁢p2∣a, and if we take "or" to be inclusive, this means either p1∣a or p2∣a.

(I don't know why you have "xor" in the subject line. The proposition is true with an inclusive "or", but not with an exclusive "or".)
