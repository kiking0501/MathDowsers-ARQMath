Approximating a continuous function with compact support by simple functions from above?

Let f∈Cc⁢(X),f:X→ℝ be a continuous function with compact support on a measure space X with f≥0.

Allegedly it is possiple to approximate that function by a monotonic decreasing sequence of simple functions sn of the form sn⁢(x)=∑jcj⁢χKj⁢(x), where the Kj are compact.

Well, if I divide the co-domain into intervals of the form [j-12n,j2n], I get compact sets within the support of f by f-1⁢([j-12n,j2n]), but then these sets are not disjoint and I can't define a simple function of the form described above.

How can I remedy that?

An idea I have is dividing the co-domain into intervals of the form (j-12n,j2n] and then taking f-1⁢([a,j2n]), where [a,j2n]⊂(j-12n,j2n] is the largest compact interval that is contained in (j-12n,j2n].

Does this work?

After some research, I think I can answer my own question.

The problem I ran into was definition-wise. A simple function is defined as a function that assumes only a finite number of distinct values. The requirement that the Kj's in the representation sn⁢(x)=∑j=1ncj⁢χKj⁢(x) be disjoint is however not part of the definition. Often this is convenient, since the representation of sn⁢(x) is unique for distinct sets Kj. However, if you don't need uniqueness you can just have the sets Kj not necessarily disjoint and find a sequence of those functions that converges to an f pointwise.

So, the big misunderstanding here, for me, was that I thought that for a function to be a simple function the "defining sets" had to be disjoint which is not necessary at all.
