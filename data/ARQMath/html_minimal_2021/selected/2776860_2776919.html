Approximation to a partial sum

Just like ∑k=1n1k can be approximated by log(n), is there a similar approximation for the sum ∑k=1n1k2

sequences-and-series summation harmonic-numbers
You can apply Euler-Maclaurin to both. The crudest asymptotic for the second is π2/6+O⁢(1/n) but this can be improved.
Euler-Maclaurin says that ∑k=1n1k2∼π26-1n+12⁢n2-16⁢n3+130⁢n5-142⁢n7+…

The answer is that, using ψ1⁢(x) the trigamma function, we have ∑k=1n1k2=ψ1⁢(1)-ψ1⁢(n+1). It is known that ψ1⁢(x)=1x+12⁢x2+⋯=∑k=0∞Bkxk+1 asymptotically using Bernoulli numbers.
