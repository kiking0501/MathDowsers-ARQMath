Is it the root of some polynomial with real coefficients? Then it's a complex number. (One can further weaken that to only needing _complex_ coefficients.)

The standard definition of a complex number is any number that can be written as: a+b⁢i where a,b∈ℝ and i=-1. So even an imaginary number, that is a number of the form b⁢i, is a complex number since it can be written as: 0+b⁢i.
