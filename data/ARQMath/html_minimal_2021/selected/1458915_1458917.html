How do you show that ||f+g||≤||f||+||g||?

I want to show that ||f+g||≤||f||+||g||. We are given the definition that ||f||:=supx∈E⁡|f⁢(x)|.

So here is my approach.

By definition we have, \begin{align*} ||f + g|| & = \sup_{x \in E}|(f + g)(x)| \\ & = \sup_{x \in E}|(f(x) + g(x)| \\ & = \sup\{|f(x) + g(x)| : x \in E\}. \end{align*} Now suppose supx∈E⁡|f⁢(x)|:=L and supx∈E⁡|g⁢(x)|:=M. Then by the Approximation Property for Suprema, we know that ∀ϵ>0, ∃x∈E such that L-ϵ<|f⁢(x)|≤L and M-ϵ<|g⁢(x)|≤M. Adding the two inequalities we get (L-ϵ)+(M-ϵ)<|f⁢(x)|+|g⁢(x)|≤M+L. By the triangle inequality |f⁢(x)+g⁢(x)|≤|f⁢(x)|+|g⁢(x)|≤L+M. In particular, |f⁢(x)+g⁢(x)|≤L+M.

But this is not saying that supx∈E⁡|f⁢(x)+g⁢(x)|≤L+M.

Any help?

real-analysis

If |f⁢(x)+g⁢(x)|≤L+M for all x, then L+M is an upper bound, so sup⁡|f⁢(x)+g⁢(x)|≤L+M.

As for the argument, you don't need any ϵ: for any x, |f⁢(x)+g⁢(x)|≤|f⁢(x)|+|g⁢(x)|≤L+M.
