You can stretch space by horizontal and vertical factors 1/a and 1/b so that the ellipse transforms into the unit circle, known to have area π. As the stretching transforms scale the area in the same proportion, the initial area is π⁢a⁢b. Note that the trick will not work for the perimeter, as the stretching transforms do not scale the arc lengths.

You have the ellipse equation x2/a2+y2/b2=1. The area of this ellipse is well known: A=π⁢a⁢b.
