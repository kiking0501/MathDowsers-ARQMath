You skipped the x4 term, not sure if that's intentional or not.
Another route is to start with the usual geometric series and subtract off the first three terms directly.

A more do-it-from-scratch approach:

Denote your sum S. Then x⋅S=x4+x5+⋯. Now subtracting S-x⁢S=x3. Solve for S, and you're done.

This is exactly the same method used to express a repeating decimal as a fraction, and a variation of it will work for finding the generating function for any repeating sequence.
