Well, to begin with if z=0 then $x^2 + y^2 = (x-y)y(-x) =-y^2x - x^2y $ and x2⁢(1+y)=y2⁢(1-x) will have infinite solutions if x=-y and x2⁢(1-x)=x2⁢(1-x) for all real x. That's inefficient but sufficient to show there are infinite solutions
