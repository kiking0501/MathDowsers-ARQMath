When can we plug an inequality into a different equation?

Let's say we have something like this:

P⁢r⁢(|Yn-p|<e)≥1-p⁢(1-p)e2⁢n

Let's say we know that p⁢(1-p)≤14

Now if I rearrange all the terms in the first equation I get:

p⁢(1-p)≥e2⁢n⁢(1-P⁢r⁢(|Yn-p|<e))

Since this shows that p⁢(1-p) is great than RHS I can safely replace p(1-p) with something that is even greater.

The question is, is there another way to know that I can immediately replace the p(1-p) with 1/4 or is the only way to rearrange all the terms to verify?

inequality

The right hand side of your original equation is just a number, depending on p and n. By considering the quadratic f⁢(p)=p⁢(1-p), and maximising it, we see that f⁢(p)≤14 for all p. Hence we can say 1-p⁢(1-p)e2⁢n≥1-1/4e2⁢n, noting that we're looking at -p⁢(1-p), and so f⁢(p)≤14 for all p becomes -f⁢(p)≥-14 for all p. But we know that P⁢(|Y/n-p|<e) is at least the term in the right. Hence we have P⁢(|Y/n-p|<e)≥1-p⁢(1-p)e2⁢n≥1-1/4e2⁢n.
