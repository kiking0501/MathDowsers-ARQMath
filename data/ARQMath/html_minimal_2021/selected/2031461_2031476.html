A little digression to speculate on the source of the problem.

The test for divisibility by 9 is well known: the remainder is the sum of the digits (mod 9).

The test for divisibility by 11 is a little less well known: look at the alternating sum of the digits. That works because odd powers of 10 are -1(mod11) while even powers are 1.

Now note the lovely fact that 7×11×13=1001. That means you can find the remainder mod 1001 and hence mod 7, 11 and 13 by alternately adding and subtracting "digits" in groups of three - essentially thinking of the number as written in base 1000.
