How to prove ⌊⌊ab⌋c⌋=⌊ab×c⌋

If a,b,c are positive integers, how to prove ⌊⌊ab⌋c⌋=⌊ab×c⌋

integers

Let a=b⋅q1+r1, with 0≤r1⁢\lt⁢b.

Then ab=q1+r1b, and since 0≤r1⁢\lt⁢b, we can say that

⌊ab⌋=⌊q1+r1b⌋=q1

Now let q1=c⋅q2+r2, with 0≤r2⁢\lt⁢c.

Then q1c=q2+r2c, and since 0≤r2⁢\lt⁢c, we can say that

⌊⌊ab⌋c⌋=⌊q1c⌋=⌊q2+r2c⌋=q2

Now we consider ⌊ab⁢c⌋. Using the same variables in the forms we used above, we say

\begin{align*} \frac {a}{b c} &= \frac {a/b}{c} \\ &= \frac{q_1 + \frac{r_1}b}c \\ &= \frac{(c\cdot q_2 + r_2) + \frac{r_1}b}c \\ &= \frac{c \cdot q_2}c + \frac{r_2}c + \frac{\frac{r_1}b}c \\ &= q_2 + \frac{r_2}c + \frac{r_1}b \cdot \frac1c \end{align*}

Now we take the floor. We know that r2<c, that r1<b, and that 0<1c≤1 (since c∈ℤ+). Thus

⌊ab⁢c⌋=⌊q2+r2c+r1b⋅1c⌋=q2

Hence ⌊⌊ab⌋c⌋=q2=⌊ab×c⌋
