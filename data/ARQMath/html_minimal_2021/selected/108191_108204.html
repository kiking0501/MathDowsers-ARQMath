A general hint, and, hopefully, motivation for the process behind the other answers:

For δ>0 and positive constants a1,a2,… with ∑i=1∞ai finite given, construct f to satisfy each of the following conditions:

  • f is nonnegative and integrable on any set [a,b].
  • f is identically 0 off the intervals A1, A2, ⋯; where, Ai∩Aj=∅ for i≠j, and j∈Aj for each j.
  • For each j, we have ∫Ajf⁢(x)⁢𝑑x=aj and supx∈Aj⁡f⁢(x)>δ.

In other words, the graph of f consists of disjoint positive spikes of common amplitude δ whose widths converge to 0 fast enough so that the sum of their areas is finite.

You could even set things up so that the amplitudes tend to infinity.
