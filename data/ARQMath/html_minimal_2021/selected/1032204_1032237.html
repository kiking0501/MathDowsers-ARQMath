You should state explicitly that n=N+1 or, preferably, get rid of n altogether, which I did in my answer.

Cover X by the open subsets Ui={yi≠0}⊂X and show that X∩Ui⊂Ui is a submanifold.

Explicitly:

For i=0, say, we have the identification U0=ℝN+1×ℝN where in that identification (y1,⋯,yN)∈ℝN is identified with [1:y1⋯:yN]∈ℙN(ℝ).
In this set-up X∩U0 consists of the pairs ((x0,x1,⋯,xN),(y1,⋯,yN))∈ℝN+1×ℝN such that xi=x0⁢yi for i=1,⋯,N, so that X∩U0 consists of all pairs ((x0,x0⁢y1,⋯,x0⁢yN),(y1,⋯,yN))∈ℝN+1×ℝN These pairs clearly form a submanifold of ℝN+1×ℝN, parametrized by x0,y1,⋯,yN and isomorphic to ℝN+1 .

We have thus proved that X∩U0 is a submanifold of U0=ℝN+1×ℝN and a similar proof shows that X∩Ui is a submanifold of Ui for i=1,⋯,N.
