Not every first order high degree ODE is a Clairaut DE. These are only of the form y=x⁢y′+f⁢(y′) and derived equations obtained by variable changes. The perfect factorization of the derivative of the DE y′=y′+x⁢y′′+f′⁢(y′)⁢y′′⟹0=y′′⁢(x+f′⁢(y′)) into a term containing the second derivative and another only containing the first order derivative (and the variables) is characteristic for all these variants. It is easy to see that most variations in coefficients or by additional terms will destroy this factorization.
