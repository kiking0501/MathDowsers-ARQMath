And I'm looking for the limit of the ratio of consecutive terms in this sequence. The sequence goes as follows:

12,25,54,117,250,525,…

With the ratio of consecutive terms going as follows:

2.08,2.16,2.17,2.14,2.10,2.07,…

The difference equation an+1-2⁢an=n2 can be solved by first finding the general solution an=k⁢2n for the homogeneous equation an+1-2⁢an=0, where k is a free parameter to be determined later, and then guessing a particular solution of the form An=c⁢n2+d⁢n+e to the inhomogeneous equation (1). Plugging this form for An into (1) and equating coefficients gives us three equations in the three unknowns c,d,e. Solving, we get c=-1, d=-2, and e=-3, so An=-(n2+2⁢n+3).

The solution to the original equation is then the sum of the general and particular solutions: an=k⁢2n-(n2+2⁢n+3) With the initial condition a1=12 we find k=9. Since an grows exponentially this explains why n2/an→0 as n→∞.
