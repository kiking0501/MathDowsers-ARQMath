Given the linear system x+y+z=7;x+y-z=5;-x-y+z=3

How do you show that:

a) Plane 1 meets plane 2 in a line b) Plane 1 meets plane 3 in a parallel line

For plane 1 and plane 2, you add the two equations and get x+y=6. Then you can obtain the parametric equation for the line assuming x as the parameter. {x=ty=6-tz=1
You can then got a picture of the line. It is in a horizontal plane with z=1. Calculating the tangent direction of the line, you get (1,-1,0)

For plane 1 and plane 2, you can do similar work. The line equation is, {x=ty=2-tz=5 The tangent direction is (1,-1,0), which is in the same direction as the first line but on different plan (i.e. z=5). It is thus in parallels to the first line.
