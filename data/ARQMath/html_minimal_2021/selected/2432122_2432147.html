Proving Triangle Inequality for Specific Metric

Let (X,d) be a metric space, x∈X, and A⊆X nonempty. Define d⁢(x,A)=inf⁡{d⁢(x,a)|a∈A}.

If y∈X and y≠x, show that d⁢(x,A)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,A).

My work so far: Let ϵx=inf⁡{d⁢(x,a)|a∈A} and let ϵy=inf⁡{d⁢(y,a)|a∈A}. Then, ∃δ≥0 such that ϵx+δ=d⁢(x,a) for some a∈A and ϵy+δ′=d⁢(y,a). Since (X,d) is a metric space, we have that d⁢(x,a)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,a). But that only gives us ϵx+δ≤d⁢(x,y)+ϵy+δ′. My hope was to somehow be able to cancel the δ's and substitute back in to find the desired inequality, but that doesn't seem to be possible. Is this approach doable?

Your are not correctly using the properties of the infimum. If ϵy=inf⁡{d⁢(y,a)|a∈A} then for all δ>0 there is an a0∈A such that d⁢(y,a0)<ϵy+δ.

Then ϵx=inf⁡{d⁢(x,a)|a∈A}≤d⁢(x,a0)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,a0)<d⁢(x,y)+ϵy+δ.

Now use that δ>0 can be arbitrarily small.
