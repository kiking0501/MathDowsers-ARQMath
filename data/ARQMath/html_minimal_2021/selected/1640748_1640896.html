This equality isn't quite true in general. There's going to be an extra constant term on the right side from integration; the differentiation on the left eliminates the constant of integration there.

Left hand side: \begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}\int f(x)\,dz &= \frac{d}{dx}(z \cdot f(x) + C_1) \\ &= z \cdot f'(x) \end{align*}

Right hand side: \begin{align*} \int \frac{d}{dx}f(x)dz &= \int f'(x)\,dz \\ &= z \cdot f'(x) + C_2 \end{align*}
