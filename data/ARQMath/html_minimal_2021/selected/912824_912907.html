Does the sum of the reciprocals of all primes of the form 4⁢k+1 converge?

Let S={p∈ℤ+:p⁢is prime and⁢p≡1mod 4}.

Is ∑p∈S1p finite or infinite, and where can I find more information about it?

The sum of the reciprocals of all primes diverges. Now, primes with residue 1 or 3 m⁢o⁢d⁢4 are approximately equidistributed in the long run, so it is a very safe bet to say this diverges too.

I'll add some references from the literature starting with the Meissel–Mertens constant defined by (the sum is over all the the primes p) : M:=limn→∞⁡(∑p≤n1p-ln⁡ln⁡n)=γ+∑p(ln⁡(1-1p)+1p) (obtained after noticing the precise divergence of the sum of the reciprocals of the primes)

Concerning the primes modulo k (with k=4 in your case) you may start with this article by Steven Finch "Mertens' Formula" (see page 4 for p≡1mod4) and the relation M4,1=γ2-ln⁡(4π⁢K1)+∑p≡1mod4(ln⁡(1-1p)+1p) with K1 the Landau-Ramanujan constant for counting integers of the form a2+b2 (see too Finch 2.3 and Mathworld).
