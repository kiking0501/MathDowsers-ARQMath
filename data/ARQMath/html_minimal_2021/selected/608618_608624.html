That is correct.

As x→2 from the left, f⁢(x)→1 from below; therefore, limx→2-⁡f⁢(f⁢(x))=limx→1-⁡f⁢(x)=-1

But as x→2+, we have the exact same behavior: f⁢(x) approaches 1 from below, so the limit is again -1. It's possible to make such limit computations since f is continuous on an interval to the left of 1.
