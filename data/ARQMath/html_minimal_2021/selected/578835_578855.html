The A.M-G.M inequality should help here. In symbols, the inequality says that for all real numbers a⁢1,a⁢2,…,an, a1+a2+…+ann≥(a1⁢a2⁢…⁢an)1n Here let a1 a2 a3 be the three numbers. Hence a1+a2+a33≥(a1⁢a2⁢a3)13 Also let a2=2⁢a1 Hence we get, a1+2⁢a1+a33 Now the minimum value will occur if a3=3⁢a1

The AM-GM you first wrote gives equality iff a1=a2=a3, which is clearly not the case here.
