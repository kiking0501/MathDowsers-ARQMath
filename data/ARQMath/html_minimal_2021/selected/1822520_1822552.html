You seem to want to prove that, for a topological space X, the following conditions are equivalent:

  1. for all x∈X and all open subsets U such that x∈U, there exists an open set V such that x∈V and V⊆V¯⊆U

  2. for all x∈X and all closed subsets F such that x∉F, there are disjoint open sets U and V such that x∈V, F⊆U

Proof of 1⟹2

Let x∈X and let F be a closed subset with x∉F. Then W=X∖F is an open set and x∈W; by hypothesis, there exists an open set V with x∈V and V⊆V¯⊆W. Then U=X∖V¯ is an open set and V∩U=∅.

Proof of 2⟹1

Let x∈X and let U be an open set with x∈U. Then F=X∖U is a closed set and x∉F. By assumption, there are disjoint open sets V and W such that x∈V and F⊆W. The second condition means U=X∖F⊇X∖W=G. Since V∩W=∅, we have V⊆G; therefore V¯⊆G. Thus V⊆V¯⊆U.

You're not choosing carefully the sets, this is the source of your problems.
