You pick a card from a full playing-card deck, and success would be picking a spades card. If any of these task are successful, then you win the game. What is the probability of winning?

The way I have solved is:

Have I done it correctly?

They key to evaluate the correct probability is that any of the tasks is successful... ie. you may get heads and not get six and spades. But you may get both heads and spades but not get six.
That does not work because of overlap. For a discussion on the formula provided by AnalysisStudento414, you may want to read about the inclusion-exclusion formula. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inclusion-exclusion_principle

A much simpler way is to take the complement of P(none of the events occur)
