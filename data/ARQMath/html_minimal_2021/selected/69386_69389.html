A somewhat detailed hint for the right hand side inequality:

Suppose r:=lim⁡sup⁡an+1an. (If the above expression is ∞, then there is nothing to prove. So assume 0≤r<∞.) Fix any ϵ>0. This means that there exists N such that for n≥N, we have an+1an≤r+ϵ. From this, can you deduce that for n≥N, we have anaN≤(r+ϵ)n-N⁢qvar_$ Rearranging a bit, an≤(r+ϵ)n⁢(aN(r+ϵ)N), so that an1/n≤(r+ϵ)⁢(aN(r+ϵ)N)1/n. Can you take it from here?

You do not have to work so hard for the left hand side inequality: there is a simple way to obtain the left hand side inequality using the right hand side one in a black-box way. I will leave you to figure it out.
