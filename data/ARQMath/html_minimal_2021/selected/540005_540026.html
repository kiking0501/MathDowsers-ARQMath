Convergence of exponential matrix sum

Let A be an n×n matrix. Consider the infinite sum B=∑k=1∞Ak⁢tkk! Each term Ak⁢tkk! is an n×n matrix. Does the sum B always converge? (i.e. does the sum for each of the n2 entries always converge?)

Not that it matters from a convergence perspective, but did you mean the indexing to start at k=0 or k=1?

Treat A as a linear transformation ℝn→ℝn. As it has an operator norm and ||Ak||≤||A||k. Then the series

B=∑k=0∞(A⁢t)kk! converges absolutely, as

∑k=0∞||(A⁢t)kk!||≤∑k=0∞(||A||⁢t)kk!=e||A||⁢t

As the space of linear transformation is complete, the series converges for all t.
