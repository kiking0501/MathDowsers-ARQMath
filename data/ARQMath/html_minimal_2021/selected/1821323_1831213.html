You can say more: 32⁢m+1-3 is in fact always divisible by 24 ;) See JasonM's answer for an idea of how you might prove this.

Let S⁢(m) be the statement: 32⁢m+1-3 is divisible by 4

Basis step: S⁢(1):

⇒32⁢(1)+1-3=32+1-3

=33-3

=27-3

=24, which is divisible by 4

Inductive step:

Assume S⁢(k) is true, i.e. assume that 32⁢k+1-3 is divisible by 4

⇒32⁢k+1-3=4⁢A; A∈ℕ

⇒32⁢k+1=4⁢A+3

S⁢(k+1): 32⁢(k+1)+1-3

=32⁢k+2+1-3

=32⁢k+3-3

=32∙32⁢k+1-3

=9⁢(4⁢A+3)-3

=36⁢A+27-3

=36⁢A+24

=4⁢(9⁢A+6), which is divisible by 4

So, S⁢(k+1) is true whenever S⁢(k) is true.

Therefore, 32⁢m+1-3 is divisible by 4.
