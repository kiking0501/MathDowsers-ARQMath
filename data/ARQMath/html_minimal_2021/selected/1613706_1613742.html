Let S be the set of integers that can bet written in that form:

S={c1⁢n1+c2⁢n2+⋯+ck⁢nk∣c1,…,ck∈ℤ}

Then S is non-empty and has a positive integer in it (why?) Let d be the least positive integer in S.

Then assume d does not divide ni for some i. Write ni=d⁢q+r with 0≤r<d, by the division algorithm.

Now show that r=ni-d⁢q∈S. So 0≤r<d. Since d does not divide ni, we have r>0. So r is a smaller positive integer in S, reaching a contradiction.

Therefore, d∣ni for all i. Since they have no common factor, you are done - d=1∈S.
