Echalon decomposition in binary shuffle (Hopf) algebras

Consider a binary shuffle algebra 𝒲 of two letters a,b. As usual the concatination of two words u=u1⁢…⁢um, v=v1⁢…⁢vn is defined as: u∙v:=u1⁢…⁢um⁢v1⁢…⁢vn and the shuffle product is defined recursively as: (k∙u)*(l∙v):=k∙(u*(l∙v))+l∙((k∙u)*v) where k,l∈{a,b} are some letters... If necessary, the coproduct of w∈𝒲 is: Δ⁢(w)=∑u∙v=wu⊗v.

An element c∈𝒲 is said to be in echalon form of weight N, if it is the concatination exponent of some linear combination of a and b, i.e.: c=(α⁢a+β⁢b)N=∑k=0NαN-k⁢βk⁢(aN-k*bk).

The problem I have is to prove (relatively easy) that any shuffle element am*bn can be represented as a combination of echalon elements of wieght m+n and to find a formula (the hard part) for this, e.g. something like: am*bn=∑k=1m+nγk(m,n)⁢(αk(m,n)⁢a+βk(m,n)⁢b)m+n⁢qvar_$ Examples: a*b=a⁢b+b⁢a=12⁢((a+b)2-(a-b)2), a2*b=a2⁢b+a⁢b⁢a+b⁢a2=12⁢((a+b)3-(a-b)3-2⁢b3)⁢…
