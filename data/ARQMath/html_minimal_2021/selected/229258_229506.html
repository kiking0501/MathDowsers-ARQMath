(I only just saw this: if you want it brought to my attention, you have to put `@Brian` somewhere in the comment.) Yes, now you’ve got it. To say that x is the minimum element of S you must say first that x∈S, which you’ve done, and then that x is minimal. That means that x is less than or equal to everything in S. Thus, for each y∈S, you want either x<y, which means x∈y, or x=y. In some of your earlier attempts you were forgetting to allow for the x=y possibility.

Hint:

You can notice either of the following observations:

  1. n<m⇔n∈m; and
  2. n≤m⇔n⊆m.

Therefore if S is a set of natural numbers, its minimum is the element which is either the member of everyone else; or a subset of everyone (including itself, of course).
