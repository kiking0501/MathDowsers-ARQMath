Relation between joint probability and marginals for two dependent random variables?

Consider two continuous real valued random variables X and Y. Let f⁢(X,Y) be their joint probability distribution and fX⁢(X),fY⁢(Y) their marginals. Suppose that X and Y are dependent. Is there any relation linking f⁢(X,Y) with the product fX⁢(X)⁢fY⁢(Y), e.g. f⁢(X,Y)≤fX⁢(X)⁢fY⁢(Y)?

Of course fX⁢(x) and fY⁢(y) (hence their product) can be expressed in fX,Y⁢(x,y), but that's all. Based on the inequality that you mention as example it can even be proved that X and Y are independent after all.

As far as I know, there is no simple relation such as the one you propose in the case of dependant variables. But the relation between the joint distribution and the marginal distributions is described by a function called copula, which is the joint cdf of a random vector with uniform marginals.
