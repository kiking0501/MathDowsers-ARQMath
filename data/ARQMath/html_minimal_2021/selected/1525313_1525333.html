Any p consecutive integers are congruent modulo p, in some order, to 1,2,3,…,p.

So the sum S of their squares is congruent, modulo p, to 12+22+⋯+p2. By a standard formula, this sum is p⁢(p+1)⁢(2⁢p+1)6. Thus
6⁢S≡p⁢(p+1)⁢(2⁢p+1)≡0(modp). It follows that p divides 6⁢S. But p≥5, so p does not divide 6. It follows that p divides S.
