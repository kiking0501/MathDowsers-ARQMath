Using Taylor series is working too hard! Recall the definition of ln (or, at least, a property): ln⁡(x)=∫1x1x⁢𝑑x If x∈ℕ, we also have from the definition of the integral ∫1x1x⁢𝑑x≥∑n=2x1n

You can see that pictorially here (picture stolen from Paul's Online Math Notes):

We also have ∑n=2x3n≥∑n=1x1n for n∈ℕ (which you can prove by induction or otherwise), so that 3⁢ln⁡(x)≥∑n=1x1n and thus ∑n=1x1n=O⁢(ln⁡(x)) as desired.
