Combinatorial proof of ∑i=0n(ni)2=(2⁢nn) by using a binary string of length 2⁢n

I was wondering if there is any combinatorial proof of ∑i=0n(ni)2=(2⁢nn) It is obvious that the RHS of the equation counts the number of binary strings of length 2⁢n with exactly n 1's, but how about the LHS?

Vandermonde's identity ∑i=0k(mi)⁢(nk-i)=(m+nk) and (nk-i)=(nn-k+i) Set k=m=n

Proof

Suppose a committee consists of m men and n women. In how many ways can a subcommittee of k members be formed? The answer is (m+nk) The answer is also the sum over all possible values of k, of the number of subcommittees consisting of k men and k-i women: ∑i=0k(mi)⁢(nk-i)
