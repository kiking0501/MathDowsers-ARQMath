Let Y be a topological space and f:Z⁢→⁢Y be a continuous map. Then f⁢∘⁢p:S1⁢→⁢Y is continuous and constant on any fibre of p, and in particular on the dense set T={exp⁡(2⁢π⁢i⁢2⁢k);k⁢∈⁢ℤ}.

For any Hausdorff space X, any topological space Y and any two continuous maps f:X⁢→⁢Y, g:X⁢→⁢Y: If f and g coincide on a dense subspace of X, then both are equal. (See here.)

In particular, since f⁢∘⁢p (as above) is constant on a dense subspace of the Hausdorff space X=S1, f⁢∘⁢p is itself constant. By the surjectivity of p, f must be constant as well.
