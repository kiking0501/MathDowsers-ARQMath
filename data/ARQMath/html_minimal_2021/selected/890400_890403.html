Find polynomial whose root is sum of roots of other polynomials

We have two numbers α and β.
We know that α is root of polynomial Pn⁢(x) of degree n and β is root of polynomial Qm⁢(x) of degree m.

How do you find polynomial Rn⁢m⁢(x) which has root equal to α+β without finding values of roots?

All polynomials are with integer coefficients.

One more question, can it be found using matrix determinant?

The elementary symmetric polynomials in the αi+βj are symmetric in the αi hence can be expressed in the elementary symmetric polynomials in the αi, where the coefficients are symmetriuc in the g⁢(x,y,z)=x24+y225-z, hence can be expressed in the elementary symmetric polynomials in the g⁢(x,y,z)=x24+y225-z. For details read about resultants.

Example: If P⁢(x)=x2+a⁢x+b and Q⁢(x)=x3+c⁢x2+d⁢x+e, then -a=α1+α2, b=α1⁢α2, -c=β1+β2+β3, d=β1⁢β2+β1⁢β3+β2⁢β3, e=-β1⁢β2⁢β3. Now the coefficients of the polynomial R⁢(x)=x6+f⁢x5+g⁢x4+h⁢x3+i⁢x2+j⁢x+k can be found like this: For example -f=(α1+β1)+(α1+β2)+(α1+β3)+(α2+β1)+(α2+β2)+(α2+β3)=3⁢(α1+α2)+2⁢(β1+β2+β3), hence f=3⁢a+2⁢c. And k=(α1+β1)⁢(α1+β2)⁢(α1+β3)⁢(α2+β1)⁢(α2+β2)⁢(α2+β3), which can be transformed to α13⁢α23+α12⁢α22⁢(α1+α2)⁢(β1+β2+β3)+…=b3+b2⁢a⁢c+…, and the other coefficients are also symmetric, but the expressions tend to grow a bit.
