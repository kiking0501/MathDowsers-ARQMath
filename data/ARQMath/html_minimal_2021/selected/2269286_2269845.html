Your proposed answer is \color⁢#⁢f⁢00⁢𝖼𝗈𝗋𝗋𝖾𝖼𝗍 whenever the sum starts at n=0. Because it starts at n=1, you have to subtract \color⁢#⁢f⁢001 to your proposed result. See my answer below.

∑n=1m∏k=1n2⁢k-12⁢kamp;=∑n=1m(n-1/2n)amp;=∑n=1m(-1)n(-1/2n)amp;=∑n=1m(-1)m(-1m-n)(-1/2n)amp;=(-1)m(-3/2m)-1amp;=(m+1/2m)-1amp;=Γ⁢(m+32)Γ⁢(m+1)⁢Γ⁢(32)-1amp;=Γ⁢(m+32)1m+1⁢Γ⁢(m+2)⁢12⁢Γ⁢(12)-1amp;=2⁢(m+1)πΓ⁢(m+32)Γ⁢(m+2)-1 Explanation:
(1): convert product to Binomial Coefficient
(2): convert to Negative Binomial Coefficient
(3): rewrite (-1)n
(4): Vandermonde's Identity
(5): convert from negative binomial coefficient
(6): express binomial coefficient with Gamma functions
(7): apply x⁢Γ⁢(x)=Γ⁢(x+1)
(8): Γ⁢(12)=π
