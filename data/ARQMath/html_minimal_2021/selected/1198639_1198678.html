Is there any formula for number of divisors of a×b?

Let a and b be two numbers,

Number of divisors of a is n1;
Number of divisors of b is n2;

How to find the number of divisors N of product a×b by using known number of divisors of factors n1 and n2?

If a=20 and b=21, then n1=6 (number of divisors of a), n2=4 (number of divisors of b. Then a×b=420 and N=32 (number of divisors of a×b).

Is there a formula to find N in terms of n1 and n2?

As lhf said, if a and b have no common divisors (other than 1, that is gcd⁡(a,b)=1), then

τ⁢(a⁢b)=τ⁢(a)⋅τ⁢(b)

Where τ⁢(n) is the number of positive divisors of n. However, when they do have common divisors, it is not as simple. There is, however a general way to do calculate τ⁢(n) if you know the prime factorization of n:

n=p1α1⁢p2α2⁢…⁢pnαn

All divisors are then of the form:

d=p1β1⁢p2β2⁢…⁢pnβn

With 0≤βi≤αi. Thus, each βi can be chosen in αi+1 different ways, and by the multiplicative principle:

τ⁢(n)=(α1+1)⁢(α2+1)⁢…⁢(αn+1)

Since you can easily find the prime factorization a⁢b if you know the prime factorization of a and b (by just adding the exponents), I think this would answer your question. Also note that the property noted by lhf can easily be showed from this formula, because two numbers are coprime if and only if they don't share any prime factors.
