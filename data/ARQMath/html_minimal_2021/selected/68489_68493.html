I think it is not true for a ring without identity! For example take 2⁢ℤ and its maximal ideal 4⁢ℤ.

Here’s a proof that doesn’t involve the quotient R/A.

Suppose that A is not prime; then there are a,b∈R∖A such that a⁢b∈A. Let B be the ideal generated by A∪{a}; B={x+a⁢r:x∈A⁢ and ⁢r∈R}. Clearly A⊊B, so B=R, 1R∈B, and hence 1R=x+a⁢r for some x∈A and r∈R. Then b=b⁢1R=b⁢(x+a⁢r)=b⁢x+b⁢a⁢r. But b⁢x∈b⁢A⊆R⁢A=A, and b⁢a⁢r∈A⁢r⊆A⁢R=A, so b∈A. This contradiction shows that A is prime.
