By Stirling's Approximation, we have x!≈2⁢π⁢x⁢(xe)x, and so for large n (x!)1/x≈(2⁢π)1/2⁢x⁢x1/x⁢xe. Thus, [(x+1)!]1/(x+1)-(x!)1/x≈(2⁢π)1/2⁢(x+1)⁢(x+1)1/(x+1)⁢(x+1)e-(2⁢π)1/2⁢x⁢x1/x⁢xe. Now, (2⁢π)1/2⁢x⁢x1/x→1 as x→∞, so we have that [(x+1)!]1/(x+1)-(x!)1/x≈x+1e-xe=1e.

Stirling's approximation has error O⁢(1x) (and in fact is very good even for modestly small x), and hence we can translate the above asymptotic approximations to prove rigorously the desired limit limn→∞⁡[[(x+1)!]1/(x+1)-(x!)1/x]=1e.
