I can't really answer your question, but I think I can see why it might split like that:

xy=yx generally means that ey⁢log⁡x=ex⁢log⁡y, which occurs when y⁢log⁡x=x⁢log⁡y, so log⁡yy=log⁡xx.

If you graph f⁢(x)=log⁡xx, you will see that the only region where f is not injective is (1,∞), in which f first rises rapidly from 0 to a maximum of 1/e at x=e and then declines gradually forever, approaching 0.

So giving y in terms of x or x in terms of y means giving one point in terms of the other, and it's not surprising that odd things would happen when they cross.
