a) We know that f′′≥0 in [0,∞)⟹f′ is strictly increasing in (2,∞) and that f is convex in the same interval.

This is false. For instance, if f′⁢(x)=1 for all x∈(2,∞), then f′′⁢(x)=0≥0 for all x...but f′ is not strictly increasing. There's a real difference between "less than" and "less than or equal to".
