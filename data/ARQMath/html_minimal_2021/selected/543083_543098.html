Solving for coefficients of a polynomial in terms of roots of another

This was a homework assignment a while back (already turned in) that I'm not totally sure how to approach. I think it might be simple but I can't wrap my mind around it. If someone could give me a tip that would be great.

I'm given a polynomial f=x3+a⁢x2+b⁢x+c with roots α1,α2,α3. How do I find the coefficients of the following two polynomials, in terms of a,b, and c:

  1. the monic cubic with roots α12,α22,α32
  2. the monic cubic with roots α1+α2,α1+α3,α2+α3

What I know:

I understand that by viete's formula, a=α1+α2+α3, b=α1⁢α2+α1⁢α3+α2⁢α3, and c=α1⁢α2⁢α3. But is there an elegant way to solve for the relationship between the coefficients for (1) and (2) using the coefficients of f? It seems resistant to a clean solution, since the relationship isn't necessarily linear.

Hint: α12+α22+α32=(α1+α2+α3)2-2⁢(α1⁢α2+α1⁢α3+α2⁢α3)=a2-2⁢b; there's one of the coefficients for the first polynomial you want. By the way, your formulas for the coefficients are missing some minus signs.
