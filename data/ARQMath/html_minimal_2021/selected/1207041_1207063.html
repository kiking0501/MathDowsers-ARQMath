The notation 𝖯⁢((A∣B)∣C) is not standard.   There should only be one bar between the event being measured and the condition.   When conditioning over two events, take the conjunction.

Both 𝖯⁢(A∣B,C) and 𝖯⁢(A∣B∩C) mean the conditional probability of A given B and C.

𝖯⁢(A∣B∩C)=𝖯⁢(A∩B∣C)𝖯⁢(B∣C)=𝖯⁢(A∩B∩C)𝖯⁢(B∩C)
