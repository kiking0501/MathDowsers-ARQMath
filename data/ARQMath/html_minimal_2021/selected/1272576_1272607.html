No. of Comparisons to find maximum in n Numbers

Given n numbers, we want to find the maximum. In order to find the maximum in a minimal amount of comparisons, we define a binary tree s.t. we compare n1′=max⁡(n1,n2), n2′=max⁡(n3,n4); n1′′=max⁡(n1′,n2′) and so on.

How many maximum comparisons are needed to find the maximum of those n numbers?

When drawing and trying out on paper, I came up with n-1 comparisons, but I'm not able to prove this. Any ideas? It's more or less easy to see if n is divsible by 2.

It is fairly straight-forward to prove with induction that k-1 comparisons are enough to find the maximum of n1,…,nk.

  • Clearly we need no comparisons to find the maximum of n1.

  • Consider now the numbers n1,…,nk and assume that we can find the maximum of k-1 numbers with k-2 comparisons. Then we need only one last comparison because max⁡(n1,…,nk)=max⁡(max⁡(n1,…,nk-1),nk).

On the other hand, this is the best case possible, too: suppose that n1 is the maximum of n1,…,nk. To decide that it actually is the maximum you need to compare it with each of n2,…,nk, i.e. you need n-1 comparisons.
