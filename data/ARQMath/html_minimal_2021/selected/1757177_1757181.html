If you cannot integrate it exactly, then estimate it.

Hint: sin⁡(n⁢x) is bounded above by 1 and below by -1. So, the integrand is bounded above by 1n⁢x and below by -1n⁢x. Now, if you integrate these on the interval a to π, you'll get an upper and lower bound on the integral. When you let n→∞, the bounds will coincide to 0.
