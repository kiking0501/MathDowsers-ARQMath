Property kept under base change and composition is preserved by products

The following is true? Why?

Let P be a property of morphisms preserved under base change and composition. Let X→Y and X′→Y′ be morphisms of S-schemes with property P. Then the unique morphism X×SX′→Y×SY′ has property P.

Yes this is true.

The canonical morphism X×SX′→Y×SY′ is the composition of X×SX′→Y×SX′ and Y×SX′→Y×SY′. The latter verify property P because they are obtained by base change (the first one is X→Y base changed to Y×SX′, the second one is similar). As P is stable by composition, your canonical morphism satisfies P.
