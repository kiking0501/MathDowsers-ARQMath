The application of Darboux is very simple. Suppose f′⁢(a)=z1 and f′⁢(b)=z2. Then, between a and b, f′ has to take every value between z1 and z2, both integer and non-integer. If it can't be an integer, then z1=z2 and the derivative everywhere is that constant value.
