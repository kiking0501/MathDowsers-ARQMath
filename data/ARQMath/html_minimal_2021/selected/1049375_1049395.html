Since you have |A|=|C|, consider a bijection f:A→C. Similarly, consider a bijection g:B→D.

Now a bijection h:A×B→C×D should be given by (a,b)↦(f⁢(a),g⁢(b)).

To show injective, consider: h⁢(a1,b1)=h⁢(a2,b2). We have h⁢(a1,b1)=(f⁢(a1),g⁢(b1))=(f⁢(a2),g⁢(b2)). As f,g are bijective, we have a1=a2 and b1=b2. So h is injective.

Showing surjectivity is quite similar. Pick (c,d)∈C×D. Since f,g are surjective, there exist a∈A and b∈B such that f⁢(a)=c and g⁢(b)=d. Thus, h⁢(a,b)=(c,d) and we have surjectivity.
