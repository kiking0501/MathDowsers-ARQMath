How, I can find the, Maximum Value of the function f⁢(x)=xn⁢(1-x)n for a natural number n≥1 and x∈[0,1].

calculus

First, find the maximum of x⁢(1-x), and let it have value Q. You can do this by completing the square, AM-GM, or differentiation (depending on how much you know).

Then since the exponentiation function x→xn is a strictly increasing function for x∈[0,∞] and Form⁢(x), if follows that Qn will be the maximum value of your function.
