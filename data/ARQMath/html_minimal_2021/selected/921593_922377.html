There are many ways to generalise Nesbitt's inequality. This way is perhaps more aptly called Shapiro's inequality (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shapiro_inequality). As you may see from the link, for larger n, the inequality may not even hold. It does for n=6.

∑i=16xixi+1+xi+2≥3 where, the indices are taken cyclically. Wlog, assume ∑i=16xi=1.

We start with the fact that f⁢(s)=11-s is convex on the interval [0,1). Applying Jensen Inequality, ∑i=16xi1-(xi+xi-1+xi-2+xi-3)≥11-∑i=16xi⁢(xi+xi-1+xi-2+xi-3)

⇔∑i=16xixi+1+xi+2≥1(∑i=16xi)2-∑i=16xi⁢(xi+xi-1+xi-2+xi-3)

Now, (∑i=16xi)2-∑i=16xi⁢(xi+xi-1+xi-2+xi-3)=(x1+x4)⁢(x3+x6)+(x1+x4)⁢(x2+x5)+(x2+x5)⁢(x3+x6)

Since, 1=((x1+x4)+(x3+x6)+(x2+x5))2≥3⁢((x1+x4)⁢(x3+x6)+(x1+x4)⁢(x2+x5)+(x2+x5)⁢(x3+x6))

the desired inequality follows.
