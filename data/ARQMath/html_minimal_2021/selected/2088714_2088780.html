Take any prime divisor of lcm⁢(a1,…,an) and let it be p. The exponent of p in lcm⁢(a1,…,an) is max⁡{a1p,…,anp}, where aip is the exponent of p in the prime decomposition of ai. So WLOG let the maximum be aip.

But now as ai∣bi we have that bip≥aip and namely max⁡{b1p,…,bnp}≥bip≥aip≥ max⁡{a1p,…,anp}. Therefore we the exponent of any prime p in the prime decomposition of lcm⁢(b1,…,bn) is always greater or equal to the exponent of p in the prime decomposition of lcm⁢(a1,…,an). Hence lcm⁢(a1,…,an)∣lcm⁢(b1,…,bn)
