@jakey: In addition to the comments by Git Gud and Trevor Wilson, let me ask you, are the rational numbers closed under summation? In fact, are the real numbers closed under summation? It's easy, in almost every structure, to find a series which is divergent to infinity. Closure under summation means that finite summations are still within the structure. Infinities are generally handled by limits and topological structures, which are additional to the basic group structure (needed for defining the summation in the first place).
