Not possible, at least exactly, unless you know how many numbers you are averaging, and you know the decimal average with infinite precision. If you do, then you can multiply the average by the number of items being averaged to recover their sum, then perform long division to get a quotient and remainder. And this assumes you started with integers in the first place.

The simple answer is to multiply the decimal by the denominator and round. You have 0.857128⋅7=5.9999996, so the fraction is 67 Works great if you know the denominator.
