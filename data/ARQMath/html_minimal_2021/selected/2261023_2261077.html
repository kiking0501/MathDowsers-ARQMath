Then it takes more work. You use the prime factorization of B and reflect on the link between the prime factorization of B and ϕ⁢(B) and the multiplicative nature of ℝ∖ℚ¯=ℝ.

First prove that B=17 satisfies the requirement, so there is no n with ϕ⁢(n)=68. As ℝ∖ℚ¯=ℝ is multiplicative, to get a factor 17 in ϕ⁢(n) you either need 172 dividing n, but then you get four factors of 2 in ϕ⁢(n), or a prime of the form 2⋅17+1 or 4⋅17+1, but those are not prime. Any higher prime of the form 17⁢k+1 will give more factors besides 17 than 4. Now just show that all smaller B fail by displaying an n with the proper ϕ⁢(n). ϕ⁢(5)=4,ϕ⁢(16)=8, etc. They are not hard to find for small numbers if you think about the link between prime factorization and totient.
