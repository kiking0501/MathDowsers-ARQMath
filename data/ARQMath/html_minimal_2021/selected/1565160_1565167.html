Two continuous functions intersection

Suppose f,g:[a,b]→ℝ are continuous functions such that f⁢(a)≤g⁢(a) and f⁢(b)≥g⁢(b). Prove that f⁢(c)=g⁢(c) for at least one c∈[a,b]

Is this the full question? What is c? Are you supposed to show such a c exists in [a,b]?

Consider h:[a,b]→ℝ defined by h⁢(x):=f⁢(x)-g⁢(x). This function is continuous on [a,b] and you know that h⁢(a)=f⁢(a)-g⁢(a)≤0 and h⁢(b)=f⁢(b)-g⁢(b)≥0. Now apply the Intermediate Value Theorem to h⁢(x); there exists c∈[a,b] such that h⁢(c)=0. But this means that f⁢(c)-g⁢(c)=0, i.e. f⁢(c)=g⁢(c).
