Describing the image and nullspace of a linear map

Let V be the space of all continuous functions on [a,b]. Define the map T according to T⁢[f]⁢(x)=∫abf⁢(t)⁢sin⁡(x-t)⁢dt. I want describe the image and kernel (nullspace) of this map. In order to do this, I've first noticed that T⁢[f]⁢(x)=(-∫abf⁢(t)⁢sin⁡(t)⁢dt)⁢cos⁡(x)+(∫abf⁢(t)⁢cos⁡(t))⁢sin⁡(x). This means that the image T⁢[V] is a subspace of the space spanned by {cos⁡(x),sin⁡(x)}. The kernel consists of all functions f such that ∫abf⁢(t)⁢sin⁡(t)⁢dt=∫abf⁢(t)⁢cos⁡(t)⁢dt=0. I'd like some help on showing that T⁢[V]=span⁡{cos⁡(x),sin⁡(x)}, and perhaps getting a clearer characterization of the kernel. Notice that if [a,b]=[-π,π] we can use the orthogonality of sin and cos, but I'm interested in a general interval. Thanks!

Since T is linear, to prove that T⁢[V]=span⁡{cos⁡(x),sin⁡(x)} it is enough to find functions f and g such that T⁢[f]=cos, T⁢[g]=sin. You can try functions of the form f⁢(x)=A⁢x+B. This will give a system of two equations in the two unknowns A and B (except perhaps for a excepcional set of values a and b.)
