How to show that (x+y)a≥xa+ya given that a≥1 and x,y≥0? Also, how to prove that the reverse inequality holds when 0≤a≤1?

real-analysis

We deal with a≥1. If a=1 there is nothing to do. So let a⁢\gt⁢1. Fix y, and let f⁢(x)=(x+y)a-xa-ya.

We have f⁢(0)=0, and f′⁢(x)=a⁢((x+y)a-1-xa-1). So f′⁢(x)≥0 if x≥0. It follows that for fixed y, f⁢(x) is non-decreasing.
