@ivana14 I think I see what you mean. I think you're thinking of when you have an equation such as y′′-2⁢y′-2⁢y=0, and you 'guess' a solution of the form y=em⁢x which, after you substitute it and divide everything by em⁢x, is equivalent to substituting m2 for y′′, m for y′ and 1 for y.

Multiply by 2 your differential equation:

2⁢y=2⁢x⁢y′+(y′)2⇔ 2⁢y=(y′+x)2-x2⇔ 2⁢y+x2=(y′+x)2

Setting u:=2⁢y+x2,

the last expression in (1) becomes:

u=(12⁢u′)2⇔u′=2⁢u⇔u′2⁢u=1

equivalent, by integration, to: u=x+C (C being an arbitrary constant), that is to say:

u=(x+C)2.

It suffices now to plug (3) into (2) in order to get the general solution:

y=C⁢x+12⁢C2.
