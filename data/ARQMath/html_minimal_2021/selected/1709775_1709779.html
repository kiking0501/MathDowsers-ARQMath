@rhody It is called nth roots of unity, or complex exponential, or De Moivre's formula, or polar form of a complex number.
It would probably help to think of this in terms of a polygon of n sides, each side subtending an angle of 2⁢π/n at the center of the circumscribing circle. The coordinates of each point in the argand plane gives the roots. As @HenryW. said, it would be easier to do this in polar coordinates, as we are working with circles. Try to visalise it (draw it or something). The answer should become more comprehensible then.
