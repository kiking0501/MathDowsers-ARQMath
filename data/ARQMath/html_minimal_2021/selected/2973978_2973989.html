This may be difficult, since exponentials for non-integer exponents are defined in a rather complicated way. But, you could argue that if we let t=r-s, we get:

ar=as+t=as⋅at

Since a>1 and t>0, we have that at>1 (can you convince yourself of this?). Therefore ar>as (in particular since ar>0).
