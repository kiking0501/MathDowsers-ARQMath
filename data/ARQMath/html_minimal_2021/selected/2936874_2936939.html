If your calculation is right, you have shown that (k+1)3+2⁢(k+1) has the form 3⋅a for some integer a. Done.
As an alternative approach, you could note that n3+2⁢n3=n+n3-n3 which is an integer exactly when n3-n3=n⁢(n-1)⁢(n+1)3 is an integer...

n3+2⁢n=n⁢(n2+2), now, check that all square numbers will leave a remainder 0(means divisible by 3) or, 1 when we divide n by 3. Hence, when 3|n we have 3|n⁢(n2+2) and when n=3⁢k+1 or 3⁢k+2, then 3|n2+2⟹3|n⁢(n2+2).
