Hopefully you already know that ω (defined as the intersection of all inductive sets) is an ordinal in the first place.

Hopefully you also know that the ordinals are totally ordered by ∈ -- that is, for ordinals α,β we always have either α∈β or α=β or β∈α.

What is an infinite ordinal? Usually "finite" is defined to mean, "equinumerous with an element of ω". So if α is an ordinal that is not finite, then in particular it is not itself an element of ω. Therefore we have either ω=α or ω∈α. In either case we have ω⊆α.
