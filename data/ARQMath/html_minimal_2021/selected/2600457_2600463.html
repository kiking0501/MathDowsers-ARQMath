@stochastic Euler totient function

such a number is of the form 2a⁢p1⁢p2⁢…⁢pn where every pi is a distinct fermat prime.

It is easy to see these numbers work.

To see other numbers dont work first notice that no odd prime can appear with exponent larger than 1 (simply apply the formula).

It is also clear that if p is a dividor of the number then p-1 is a power of 2. hence p is of the form 2n+1. It has been shown that primes of the form 2n+1 are also of the form 22n+1
