As it was said before, A must not be lineary dependent. The easy way to define it is to make a matrix, where coefficients are the coefficients from linear combinations of vectors from A through B, and then calculate its determinant. If det = 0 ⇒ A is not linearly independent. For example, If B={(1,0,0),(0,1,0),(0,0,1)}, A={(1,1,1),(1,0,1),(0,1,0)} then matrix is \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, it's det = 0, so A is not lineary independent
