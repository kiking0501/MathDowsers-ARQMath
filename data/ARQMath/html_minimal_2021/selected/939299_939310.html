No. The squaring map you write, i.e. z↦z2 if you write S1⊆ℂ, has degree 2, so cannot be null-homotopic since it is the composition of the inclusion with the squaring map, i.e. your map decomposes as

S1⟶iℂ∖{0}⟶z↦z2ℂ∖{0}

This induces the ×2 map on ℤ, hence it's not null-homotopic.
