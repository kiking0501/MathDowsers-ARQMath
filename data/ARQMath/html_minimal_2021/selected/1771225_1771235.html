As others have pointed out, to prove anything here you need more assumptions. Maybe you know that the answer is quadratic (so f⁢(n)=a⁢n2+b⁢n+c for suitable constants {a,b,c}. That would be enough. Even knowing it was cubic would be enough (as you have four data points). But even knowing that it is a polynomial isn't enough (you can fit lots of polynomials of high degree through these data points, for example).
In the same vein as lulu's comment: if you only allow polynomials, n2-n is the solution of the lowest degree.
