Suppose I want to place a log curve between points (x0,y0)⁢a⁢n⁢d⁢(xf,yf). My equation will be of the form: y=a⁢log⁡(x)+b. If I know two points, there is a unique logarithmic curve joining them. Your job is to find the numbers a and b which describe this curve. Then, y0=a⁢log⁡(x0)+b and yf=a⁢log⁡(xf)+b. This is a system of two equations in two variables. Note that yf-y0=a⁢log⁡(xfx0)⟹a=yf-y0log⁡(xf/x0). After calculating a, you can easily find b.
