Write the terms as 1[(1+1n)n]n. Think of using a well-known limit and the Comparison Test.
Root test should work.

Even if you don't know many limits, note that by expanding (1+1n)n and dropping all terms except the two lowest order terms, one has (1+1n)n≥1+n⋅1n=2. Thus 0≤(nn+1)n2=1[(1+1n)n]n≤12n. The convergence of your series now follows by comparison to the convergent geometric series ∑n=1∞(1/2)n.

(Using the "well-known" limit limn→∞⁡(1+1n)n=e, you can conclude that the terms (1+1n)n eventually exceed 2; this will suffice for comparing your series with ∑n=1∞(1/2)n. Of course, if you do make use of this limit, using the Root Test is somewhat easier.)
