n⁢(n+1)2. These are the Triangular Numbers
These are triangular numbers. tn is simply the sum of the numbers from 1 to n. It is easy to see that tn=n⁢(n+1)/2, for example if n is even, say n=10, you can group the numbers from to 10 in 10/2=5 pairs whose sum is always 10+1=11, i.e., (1,10), (2,9), (3,8), (4,7), and (5,6). Generalizing this reasoning you can see that if n is even you can split the numbers into n/2 pairs whose sum is n+1, thus tn=n⁢(n+1)/2. You make a similar reasoning when n is odd.
