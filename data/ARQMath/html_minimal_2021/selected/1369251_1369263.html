NOTES:

Remark 1: When making a substitution of variables in a definite integral, the limits of integration change accordingly. In this example, the substitution was x=32⁢tan⁡θ. When x=1 at the lower limit, tan⁡θ=23⟹θ=arctan⁡(2/3). Similarly, when x=2 at the upper limit, tan⁡θ=43⟹θ=arctan⁡(4/3).

Remark 2:
To evaluate sin⁡(arctan⁡(2/3)), we recall that the arctangent is an angle whose tangent is 2/3. A picture sometimes facilitates the analysis wherein we draw a right triangle with vertical side of length 2 and horizontal side of length 3 forming a right angle.

Note that the angle the hypotenuse makes with the horizontal side is arctan⁡(2/3). Inasmcuh as the hypotenuse is of length 22+32=13, we see sin⁡(arctan⁡(2/3))=213 and thus csc⁡(arctan⁡(2/3))=132.
