Indeed it is not. To see this, note that there's an injection from the set of non-terminating decimal expansions of real numbers between 0 and 1 into it by sending 0.a1⁢a2⁢a3⁢… to (a1,a2,a3,…). Since (0,1) is uncountable, so is your set. Alternatively, you can echo Cantor's diagonal argument in your set.
