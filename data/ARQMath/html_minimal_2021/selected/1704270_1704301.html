Assume that ||f||X*=0. I want to prove that f is the zero-functional, i.e. that f⁢(x)=0 for all x∈X, so pick one such x. By definition of ||f||X*, there is a sequence Cn>0 of numbers, such that Cn→0, and such that |f⁢(x)|≤Cn⁢||x||. Since |f⁢(x)| is independent of n and non-negative, this shows that |f⁢(x)|=0, or f⁢(x)=0.
