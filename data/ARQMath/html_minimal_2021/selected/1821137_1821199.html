Volume of Solids of Revolution

Why the volumes of solids of revolution about x axis of two plane curves that have the same area are different?

Let's take for example f⁢(x)=1 between 0 and 1 that has the same area as g⁢(x)=cos⁡(x) between 0 and π/2 but the volume of the solids of the revolution about x axis is different.

What am I missing?

calculus
The nature of the curves themselves.
A triangle and a squadre of the same perimeter are not bound to have the same area

Analytically, the reason is that the integral for the area enclosed between the plane curve and the x axis over the interval [a,b] is given by

A=∫abf⁢(x)⁢𝑑x

while the volume of the solid of revolution about the x is given by

V=∫abπ⁢f2⁢(x)⁢𝑑x

Consequently, the integrals for area and volume are different.

So when we have two curves f1⁢(x) and f2⁢(x) such that

A1=A2

there won't be any reason to conclude

V1=V2
