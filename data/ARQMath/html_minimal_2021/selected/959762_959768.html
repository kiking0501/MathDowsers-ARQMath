Why is P⁢(lim sup⁡An)>0 sufficient for P⁢(lim sup⁡An)=1?

Why for independent events, the necessary condition for P⁢(lim sup⁡An)>0 is sufficient for P⁢(lim sup⁡An)=1?

probability probability-theory limsup-and-liminf

Because, in full generality, lim sup⁡An belongs to the tail sigma-algebra generated by the sequence (An) and because, in the present case, the sequence (An) is independent hence, according to Kolmogorov's zero-one law, the tail sigma-algebra is trivial in the sense that every event in it has probability 0 or 1.
