It should help a bit to write the parenthesis as a single fraction, then invert the sides of the inequality, leading to (1+1/d)2⁢d≤e2. Take the logarithm of that, and you're perhaps in more familiar territory. (Hint: The graph of the logarithm lies below its tangent at 1.)

As usual everything follows from ex≥1+x.

For x∈ℝ and n∈ℕ such that 1+xn>0 this inequality leads to

ex=exn⋅n≥(1+xn)n

and taking reciprocals

e-x≤(1+xn)-n=(1-xn+x)n.

Take x=1 to get

1e≤(1-1n+1)n

and therefore

1e2≤(1-1n+1)2⁢n.
