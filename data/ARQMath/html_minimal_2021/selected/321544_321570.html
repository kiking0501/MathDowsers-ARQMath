Question about divisibility of the factorial

I've found this question in the book devoted to Lie's groups. How to prove that n! is devided by n1!⁢n2!⁢…⁢nk!, where n=n1+n2+…+nk and n, ni are natural numbers?

number-theory lie-algebras

There are several ways to do this.

One is to notice that n!n1!⁢n2!⁢…⁢nk! is the multinomial coefficient, that is, the (integer) coefficient of x1n1⁢…⁢xknk in the expansion of (x1+⋯+xk)n.

Another is to note that the symmetric group Sn, of order n!, contains a subgroup isomorphic to Sn1×⋯×Snk, of order n1!⁢n2!⁢…⁢nk! The second argument is basically the same of @WouterZeldenthuis
