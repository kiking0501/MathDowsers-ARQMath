Matlabs sum for ∑n=11051n is actually quite spot on, it's not a fault of machine accuracy. The series is called the Harmonic series and is known to diverge very slowly. In fact, it behaves approximately like γ+ln⁡n where γ is the Euler-Mascheroni constant, about 0.57721. At n=105 we indeed expect about 0.57721+5⁢ln⁡10≈12.0901.

Matlab is also not saying anything incorrect for the original series, its results were just interpreted incorrectly (in a similar way to the previous example). The sum grows quite slowly. Consider if we order the sum like this: ∑m=1∞∑n=1∞1m2+n2.

By Residue Calculus methods, one can show that the inner sum is equal to π⁢m⁢coth⁡(π⁢m)-12⁢m2.

coth tends to 1 exponentially quickly so one expects ∑m=1k∑n=1∞1m2+n2≈π⁢ln⁡k2+6⁢γ⁢π-π212 for large k.
