Claim 2 below should answer the question.

Since the only unit in ℕ is 1, we have

p is prime iff p∣a⁢b⟹p⁢∣a∨p∣⁢b

p is irreducible iff a∣p⟹a=1∨a=p

Claim 1: p is prime ⟹ p is irreducible

Proof: Assume that a∣p. For some b, we have p=a⁢b Since p∣a⁢b, we know that p∣a or p∣b.

Case p∣a: for some c, we have a=p⁢c. Therefore, (1) implies that a⁢b⁢c=a. Since the only unit in ℕ is 1, we have that b=c=1. Therefore a=p.

Case p∣b: for some c, we have b=p⁢c. Therefore, (1) implies that a⁢b⁢c=b. Since the only unit in ℕ is 1, we have that a=c=1. Therefore, a=1.

Thus, assuming that p is prime and a∣p, we have shown that a=1 or a=p.

QED

Claim 2: p is irreducible ⟹ p is prime

Proof: Assume that p is irreducible, p∣a⁢b, and p∤a. Let g be the smallest positive element of the set S={a⁢x+p⁢y:x,y∈ℤ} If g∤p, then there is an r so that 0⁢\lt⁢r⁢\lt⁢g and q⁢g+r=p. However, then r=p-q⁢(a⁢x+p⁢y)=a⁢(-q⁢x)+p⁢(1-q⁢y)∈S but g is the smallest positive element of S. Therefore, g∣p. Similarly, g∣a.

Since p is irreducible, g=1 or g=p. Since p∤a and g∣a, we must have g=1. Therefore, we have x,y so that 1=a⁢x+p⁢y Since p∣a⁢b, for some c, we have a⁢b=p⁢c. Multiply (4) by b to get b Equation (5) says that p∣b.

Thus, assuming that p is irreducible and p∣a⁢b, we have shown that if p∤a, then p∣b.

QED
