You say "somehow" but in fact what you're saying is perfectly fine. What you've saying is that Y's distribution conditional on X is normal with parameters depending on X. Models of this type are common, for instance mixture models.

As for your question, in order to have E⁢(eX⁢eY)=E⁢(eX)⁢E⁢(eY) you'd need independence (or some reason to expect that particular factorization would hold in the absence of independence, which is possible but unusual.) But here X and Y are not independent (knowing X tells you a lot of probabilistic information about Y) so I see no reason why it would hold.
