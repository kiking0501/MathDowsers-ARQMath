This is the kind of question that is better done by [software](http://www.wolframalpha.com/input/?i=factor+x^32-x+mod+2)
Your task is essentially to list all the degree five irreducible polynomials over ℤ2. See [this thread](http://math.stackexchange.com/q/998563/11619) for several ways of doing this (Qiaochu's answer below is the same approach Dilip Sarwate used there).

It sounds like you know that x32-x is x⁢(x-1) times all irreducible polynomials of degree 5. There are 6 of these out of the 32 (monic) polynomials of degree 5. You can rule out most of these by checking for linear factors: first, everything divisible by x is reducible, which leaves 16 possibilities (constant term must be 1). Incidentally, this rules out every example you wrote down, some of which aren't even of degree 5.

Second, everything divisible by x+1 is reducible, which leaves 8 possibilities (sum of terms must be odd), namely

x5+x+1 x5+x2+1 x5+x3+1 x5+x4+1 x5+x3+x2+x+1 x5+x4+x2+x+1 x5+x4+x3+x+1 x5+x4+x3+x2+1

6 of these are irreducible, so you want to rule out 2 more. These 2 are the ones of the form (irreducible quadratic)(irreducible cubic). There's exactly one irreducible quadratic, namely x2+x+1, so the only thing you need to do is check what's divisible by this. x2+x+1 divides x3-1, so you can reduce the exponents mod3 to do this. You get that x5+x+1 and x5+x4+1 are divisible by x2+x+1 and that's it; the other 6 are the irreducibles of degree 5.

In total this took less than 5 minutes, I think.
