As a rule of thumb, whenever you have an "all-the-things" expression, symmetry is likely exploitable somehow. Induction is when each thing follows from the previous thing(s), but there is nothing connecting the primes in this way for induction to work.
Don't worry, it's a little bit cruel to ask someone who's being introduced to number theory to prove such statement. It's called Wilson's Theorem and Wilson himself only conjectured it; the proof came out one year later by Lagrange.
