The value is π26. See here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basel_problem for a (somewhat) elementary proof.

You can also consider that the sum corresponds to generalized harmonic numbers; so Sn=∑i=1n1i2=Hn(2) For large values of n, the asymptotic expansion is Sn=π26-1n+12⁢n2-16⁢n3+O⁢(1n4)

Let us try using n=10 which is not a large number. The exact summation would lead to 19683291270080≈1.549767731 while the formula would give π26-5716000≈1.549767400.

This can be generalized to ∑i=1n1ik=Hn(k) for which nice formulae are obtained when k is even (for odd values of k, this would involve ζ⁢(k) you will learn sooner or later).
