What is z4=-4 using the exponential form method, and with the answers written as z=x+y⁢i?

I know how to algebraically solve equations like this and find square roots of complex numbers, but how would you solve equations like that with the n-th power of z being greater than 2?

complex-numbers

In exponential form, z4=-4=4⁢ cis ⁢π, where cis ⁢θ is shorthand for ei⁢θ=cos⁡θ+i⁢sin⁡θ. Then z0=2⁢ cis ⁢π4 (since (2)4=4), and zk = 2⁢ cis ⁢(1+2⁢k)⁢π4, for k=1,2,3.

In rectangular form, let z=a+b⁢i with real a and b. Then z4=a4+4⁢a3⁢b⁢i-6⁢a2⁢b2-4⁢a⁢b3⁢i+b4, which gives

4⁢a3⁢b-4⁢a⁢b3=0

and

a4-6⁢a2⁢b2+b4=-4

Well, a≠0 and b≠0, since either a or b being zero would force z4 to be a non-negative real value, so we can divide the first equation by 4⁢a⁢b to get

a2-b2=0

or a2=b2. Then the second equation becomes

-4⁢a4=-4

or a4=1, and a=±1, meaning b=±1.
