The indefinite integral of a function must always be continuous. This is a fact apparent by it's definition: area under a curve.

One can prove the following

THM Let f:[a,b]→ℝ be Riemann integrable over its domain. Define a new function F:[a,b]→ℝ by F⁢(x)=∫axf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t Then F is continuous. That is, the map f↦∫axf sends ℛ⁢[a,b] to 𝒞⁢[a,b].

PROOF Let c∈[a,b]. Then F⁢(x)-F⁢(c)=∫cxf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t

Since f is integrable, we know it is bounded, say |f⁢(x)|≤M over [a,b]. Then -∫cxM⁢𝑑t≤∫cxf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t≤∫cxM⁢𝑑t

which means -M⁢(x-c)≤∫cxf⁢(t)⁢𝑑t≤M⁢(x-c)

Thus we get |F⁢(x)-F⁢(c)|≤M⁢|x-c|

Taking x→c the squeeze theorem says limx→c⁡F⁢(x)=F⁢(c). ▲
