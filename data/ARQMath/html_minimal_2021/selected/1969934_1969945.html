It depends on your definition of e.
Usually, we prove that the sequence given by an=(1+1n)n is increasing and bounded, hence convergent, and just call e its limit. With this assumption, there is nothing to prove.
Perhaps have a look at [this post](http://math.stackexchange.com/questions/96606/how-to-prove-lim-n-to-infty-11-nn-e) and other questions [linked there](http://math.stackexchange.com/questions/linked/96606)

An algebraic way is Binomial Expansion, which is given by Now every term in front there goes to 1 for n→∞, so that the limit gives limn→∞⁡(1+1n)n=1+1+12!+13!+⋯=e.
