This is the polynomial extended Euclidean algorithm. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extended_Euclidean_algorithm#Polynomial_extended_Euclidean_algorithm

For integers, if you're trying to find an inverse of a mod n using Euclidean algorithm, you would divide n by a, find the remainder r1, then divide a by r1, find the remainder r2; divide r1 by r2, etc., until the remainder becomes 1. Here you're looking for the inverse of x3+x2+1 mod x4+x2, so you divide x4+x2 by x3+x2+1, find the remainder x+1, then divide x3+x2+1 by this remainder x+1, this time the remainder is already 1.
