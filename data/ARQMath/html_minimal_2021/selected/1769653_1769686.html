I don't think that the purpose of the exercise is to derive (1) (see this MSE question or that one), one only needs to prove that ∫0+∞cos⁡(x)x⁢d⁢x exists (which is easy thanks to integration by parts) and not equal to 0. The original integral is convergent (as an improper Riemann integral) by Dirichlet's criterion, since cos⁡x has a bounded primitive while 1x is decreasing to zero. It follows that: I=∫0+∞cos⁡(x)x⁢𝑑x=I⁢B⁢P12⁢∫0+∞sin⁡xx3/2⁢𝑑x=12⁢∫0πsin⁡(x)⁢∑n≥0(-1)n(x+n⁢π)3/2⁢d⁢x but the last sum is a positive function on (0,π), since: ∑n≥0(-1)n(x+n⁢π)3/2=∑n≥0(1(x+2⁢n⁢π)3/2-1(x+(2⁢n+1)⁢π)3/2). It follows that I>0.
