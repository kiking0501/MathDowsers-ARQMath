The explanation in the text is indeed unclear. The idea is to fix one element from the set, say, 1 in your example, and then pair every subset that does not contain 1 with one that does contain 1. This gives the pairing: ∅⇔{1},{2}⇔{1,2},{3}⇔{1,3},{2,3}⇔{1,2,3}

The pairing is simply obtained by removing 1 if it were in the set, or adjoining 1 if it was not in the set. This operation changes the parity of the subset and is a reversible process, thus a bijection.
