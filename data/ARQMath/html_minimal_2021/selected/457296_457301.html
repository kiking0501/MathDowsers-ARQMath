You don't necessarily have gcd⁡(2⁢a,2⁢b)=d, and d=1 does happen. Use gcd⁡(a+b,a-b)=gcd⁡(a+b,2⁢b). If p is a prime dividing b, can it divide a+b?
If you have established that d divides 2⁢a and 2⁢b, then it follows that d=1 or d=2 as a and b are relatively prime.
The error in your proof is that d|2⁢a and d|b is not that gcd⁡(2⁢a,2⁢b)=d because you have proven only d divides 2⁢a and 2⁢b but not that it is the greatest such number. So, what we do get is that d|gcd⁡(2⁢a,2⁢b)=2⁢gcd⁡(a,b)=2.
