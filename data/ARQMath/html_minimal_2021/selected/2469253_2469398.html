Note that you were only given a regular parametrized curve, not an arclength-parametrized curve, although of course you could reparametrize by arclength. But what regularity tells you is that α′⁢(t)⋅α′⁢(t)>0 for all t.

So let's take your equation α⁢(t)+λ⁢(t)⁢α′⁢(t)=P, where P is a constant vector, and subtract α⁢(t) from both sides. Now dot with α′⁢(t) and we get λ⁢(t)=(P-α⁢(t))⋅α′⁢(t)∥α′⁢(t)∥2. The denominator is never 0, and both numerator and denominator are smooth functions of t, so λ is likewise a smooth function of t.
