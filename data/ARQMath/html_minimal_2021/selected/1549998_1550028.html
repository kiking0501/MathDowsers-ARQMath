@labbhattacharjee, this appears to be a different identity.

Consider a combinatorial argument. Let Λ={a,b,c} be our alphabet. We select the i positions in an n letter word for {a,b} characters in (ni) ways and multiply by 2i as we have a word (is each selected slot a a or is it a b?). The remaining n-i slots are all c's. Now we add up over all possible values of i:

∑i=0n(ni)⁢2i

Observe that we have enumerated all strings in Λn. There are 3n such strings.
