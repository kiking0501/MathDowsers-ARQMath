I spelled this argument out in some detail, so that I could understand it. The argument is based on https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-GQFljOVZ7I, but elaborated to my tastes.

Define θ⁢(x)=2⁢ψ⁢(x)+1. Then, θ⁢(x)=(∑n=-∞1e-n2⁢π⁢x)+1+(∑n=1∞e-n2⁢π⁢x)=∑n=∞∞e-n2⁢π⁢x

The Poisson summation formula. is, ∑n-∞∞f⁢(n)=∑k=-∞∞∫-∞∞f⁢(y)⁢e-2⁢π⁢i⁢k⁢y⁢x⁢𝑑y Substituting f⁢(n)=e-n2⁢π⁢x and f⁢(y)=e-y2⁢π⁢x gives,

θ⁢(x)=∑n-∞∞e-n2⁢π⁢x=∑k=-∞∞∫-∞∞e-y2⁢π⁢x⁢e-2⁢π⁢i⁢k⁢y⁢𝑑y=∑k=-∞∞∫-∞∞e-π⁢x⁢(y2+2⁢i⁢y⁢kx)⁢𝑑y

Complete the square by adding and subtracting a term i2⁢k2x2 θ⁢(x)=∑k=-∞∞∫-∞∞e-π⁢x⁢(y2+2⁢i⁢y⁢kx+i2⁢k2x2-i2⁢k2x2)⁢𝑑y

Substituting y2+2⁢i⁢y⁢kx+i2⁢y2⁢k2x2=(y+i⁢kx)2 and i2⁢k2x2=-k2x2 gives, θ⁢(x)=∑k=-∞∞∫-∞∞e-π⁢x⁢((y+i⁢kx)2+k2x2)⁢𝑑y=∑k=-∞∞e-π⁢k2x⁢∫-∞∞e-π⁢x⁢(y+i⁢kx)2⁢𝑑y

as e-π⁢k2x is not a function of y, and so can be moved outside the integral.

An argument can then be made using path integrals that that follow a rectangle around the complex plain that, ∫-∞∞e-π⁢x⁢(y+i⁢kx)2⁢𝑑y=∫-∞∞e-π⁢x⁢z2⁢𝑑z=1π⁢x⁢∫-∞∞e-z2⁢𝑑z=ππ⁢x=1x

The argument uses the fact that a closed integral around no poles is zero, and also relies on terms at infinity going to zero. This is standard complex analysis, but it is not easy to see without a diagram. It really should be proved separately.

Also using, θ⁢(1x)=∑k=1∞e-k2⁢πx

θ⁢(x)=∑k=-∞∞e-π⁢k2x⁢∫-∞∞e-π⁢x⁢(y+i⁢kx)2⁢𝑑y=θ⁢(1x)x

Substituting back θ⁢(x)=2⁢ψ⁢(x)+1 gives the equation in terms of ψ.

2⁢ψ⁢(x)+1=2⁢ψ⁢(1x)+1x

The proof uses a result of the form, ∫-∞∞e-(y+i⁢b)2⁢𝑑y=∫-∞∞e-z2⁢𝑑z

Consider ∫e-z2⁢𝑑z arround a rectangular path P given by -∞→∞→∞+i⁢b→-∞+i⁢b→-∞. From Cauchy's integral theorem this integral must be zero because it is a closed loop, and e-z2 has no poles. ∫Pe-z2⁢𝑑z=0 so, 0=∫-∞∞e-y2⁢𝑑y+limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y+i⁢x)2⁢𝑑x+∫∞-∞e-(y+i⁢b)2⁢𝑑y+limY→-∞⁡∫b0e-(Y+i⁢x)2⁢𝑑x

0=∫-∞∞e-y2⁢𝑑y+limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y+i⁢x)2⁢𝑑x-∫-∞∞e-(y+i⁢b)2⁢𝑑y-limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y-i⁢x)2⁢𝑑x

And I claim that, limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y+i⁢x)2⁢𝑑x=limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y-i⁢x)2⁢𝑑x=0

which gives, 0=∫-∞∞e-y2⁢𝑑y-∫-∞∞e-(y+i⁢b)2⁢𝑑y

To prove the claim, firstly, limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y±i⁢x)2⁢𝑑x=limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y2±2⁢i⁢Y⁢x-x2)⁢𝑑x=limY→∞⁡∫0be-Y2⁢e±i⁢Y⁢x⁢ex2⁢𝑑x Consider the complex magnitude, |limY→∞⁡∫0be-Y2⁢e±2⁢i⁢Y⁢x⁢ex2⁢𝑑x|≤limY→∞⁡∫0b|e-Y2|⁢|e±2⁢i⁢Y⁢x|⁢|ex2|⁢𝑑x

But, |e±2⁢i⁢Y⁢x|=1 and limY→∞⁡|e-Y2|=0 and ∫0b|ex2|⁢𝑑x is finite giving,

limY→∞⁡∫0b|e-Y2|⁢|e±i⁢Y⁢x|⁢|e-x2|⁢𝑑x=limY→∞⁡∫0b|e-Y2|⁢|e-x2|⁢𝑑x=(limY→∞⁡|e-Y2|)⁢∫0b|ex2|⁢𝑑x=0 So,

|limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y±i⁢x)2⁢𝑑y|≤0⟹limY→∞⁡∫0be-(Y±i⁢x)2⁢𝑑y=0
