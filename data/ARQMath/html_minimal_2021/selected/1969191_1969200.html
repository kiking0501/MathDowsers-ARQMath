An alternative approach, not among the answers at the linked duplicate (and not an ϵ-δ proof, but still useful for others):

Though most texts and courses assign your problem before they introduce the ratio test for convergence of series, it is perfectly valid to deduce that your limit is zero by using the ratio test.

Define xn=ann!. Then xn+1/xn=an+1, which obviously tends to 0 as n→∞. By the ratio test, the series ∑n=1∞xn converges. Hence limn→∞⁡xn=0.
