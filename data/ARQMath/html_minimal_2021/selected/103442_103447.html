Direct sum of subsets of an commutative group

Let A be an abelian group, written additively, and let n be a positive integer such that n⁢x=0 for all x∈A. Assume that we can write n=r⁢s, where r,s are positive relative prime integers. Let Ar consist of all x∈A such that r⁢x=0, and similarly As consist of all x∈A such that s⁢x=0. Show that every element a∈A can be written uniquely in the form a=b+c with b∈Ar, and c∈As.

I defined f:Ar×As→A by (b,c)↦b+c, I showed that Ar,As are subgroups of A and that this map defines an isomorphism.

Can you guys think of another (elementary, please) way of going about this problem? Any suggestions would be appreciated.

Given a∈A, let m be the least positive integer such that m⁢a=0. Since n⁢a=0, then m|n. Since gcd⁡(r,s)=1, we have: gcd⁡(m,r)⁢gcd⁡(m,s)=gcd⁡(m,r⁢s)=gcd⁡(m,n)=m. Let d=gcd⁡(m,r), and let e=gcd⁡(m,s). We also have d|r and e|s, hence gcd⁡(d,e)=1. Hence there exist α and β such that α⁢d+β⁢e=1.

Now verify that s⁢(α⁢d⁢a)=r⁢(β⁢e⁢a)=0, and that a=α⁢d⁢a+β⁢e⁢a.

This gives existence. To get uniqueness, suppose that a=b+c=b′+c′. Then b-b′ has order dividing r, c′-c has order dividing s; since b-b′=c-c′, then b-b′ has order dividing gcd⁡(r,s).
