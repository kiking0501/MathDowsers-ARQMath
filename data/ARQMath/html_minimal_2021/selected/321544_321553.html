Question about divisibility of the factorial

I've found this question in the book devoted to Lie's groups. How to prove that n! is devided by n1!⁢n2!⁢…⁢nk!, where n=n1+n2+…+nk and n, ni are natural numbers?

number-theory lie-algebras

Since n! is the number of ways of ordering a set of size n, each ni! should be interpreted as the number of ways of ordering a subset of n. Simply apply a counting argument.

I see this shows ≤, but does it show divisibility? If we look at the symmetries instead of the orderings then Lagrange's applies, as Andreas mentions.
