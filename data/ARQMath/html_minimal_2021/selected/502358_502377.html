general-topology

Here are two examples of synthetic bases on ℝ which generate different topologies:

  • ℬ1={(a,b):a<b∈ℝ}.
  • ℬ2={[a,b):a<b∈ℝ}.
  • ℬ3={{a}:a∈ℝ}.

The topology generated by ℬ1 is the usual (Euclidean metric) topology on ℝ. The topology generated by ℬ2 is the Sorgenfrey topology (or lower-limit topology). The topology generated by ℬ3 is the discrete topology on ℝ.

There are simpler examples (since any topology is also a base for itself, and so any two distinct topologies on the same set will be bases for different topologies).

Munkres seems to define the topology generated by a base ℬ slightly differently, opting instead to define it to be τ={U⊆X:(∀x∈U)(∃B∈ℬ)(x∈B⊆U)}. This is easily seen to be equivalent to the definition you have given, and the Theorem you mention is essentially just a formal statement of this fact.
