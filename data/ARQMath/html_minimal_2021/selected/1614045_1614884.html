Heuristically, you can make a good guess if you have a continuous function f:[a,b]→ℝ such that that f

  1. is bounded on [a,b] (there exists M, such that |f⁢(x)|≤M for all x∈[a,b])

  2. It doesn't visually "wiggle" too much, or is smooth almost everywhere. The topologist's sine curve is a typical counter-example to boundedness implies uniform continuity, but you can see by looking that it just wiggles too much.

For intuition's sake consider the two following function: f⁢(x)=1x. This function is not uniformly continuous n [0,∞], but i⁢s uniformly continuous on [1,∞], You can verify this result for practice, but just looking at the function, it is smooth and bounded, and will not "Wiggle" too much, meaning that you can catch each open neighborhood around f⁢(x) with a single δ.
