Example of a Power Series Given Interval of Convergence

This was a thought question assigned to our calc II class, and I wasn't sure how to approach it.

Give an example of a power series whose interval of convergence is (0,43]. Show that it has this interval of convergence.

Try ∑(x-2/3)nn. It is by the way not quite right, but the fix should not be hard.

I'll start with something I know and see if I can get to what you want.

First of all, you want a series that converges on a half-open interval. The simplest one I can think of is ∑n=1∞xnn which converges for -1≤x<1.

To make this converge at the upper limit, change the sign of x so the series becomes ∑n=1∞(-x)nn which converges for -1<x≤1.

The interval of convergence is 2 (=1-(-1)). To make it 4/3, multiply x by 24/3=32. The series then becomes ∑n=1∞(-3⁢x/2)nn which converges for -23<x≤23.

Finally, you want the interval of convergence to start at 0 instead of -2/3. To do this, subtract 2/3 from x, making the series ∑n=1∞(-3⁢(x-2/3)/2)nn=∑n=1∞(-3⁢x/2+1)nn which converges for 0<x≤43.
