If by ℕ5 you mean the cartesian power of ℕ, then yes, since the cartesian product of two countable sets is countable (and by induction 5 sets).
I would recommend trying to find two injections and using the cantor bernstein theorem.

Let g:ℕ2→ℕ be any bijection, for example the Cantor pairing g⁢(n,m)=12⁢(n+m)⁢(n+m+1)+m Now define f⁢(n1,n2,n3,n4,n5)=g⁢(g⁢(g⁢(g⁢(n1,n2),n3),n4),n5)
