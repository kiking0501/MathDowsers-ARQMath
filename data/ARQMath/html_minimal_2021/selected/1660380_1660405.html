I think you're in the right track: any homomorphism from any group G towards any group is completely determined by its action on some generating set of G. If we talk of automorphisms then generators are mapped to generators

In our case, G:=ℤ6 is a cyclic group of order 6, and thus an automorphism is completely determined by its action on a generator of G.

Since there are two elements of order six here, namely  1,5(mod6) , every automorphism must map any of them into itself or the other one, and thus clearly Aut(G)≅ℤ2
