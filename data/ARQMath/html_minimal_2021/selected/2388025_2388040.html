Generalization of algebraic Young's inequality to n variables.

The classical formulation of Young's inequality is \begin{equation*} xy \leq \frac{x^p}{p} + \frac{y^q}{q}, \quad \text{where} \quad \frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} = 1. \end{equation*} It's fairly trivial to extend this to \begin{equation*} x^ay^b \leq \frac{a}{a+b} x^{a+b} + \frac{b}{a+b} y^{a+b}. \end{equation*} It should be possible to find constants where the following holds: \begin{equation*} \prod_{i=1}^n x_i^{a_i} \leq \sum_{i=1}^n C_i x_i^m, \end{equation*} where ∑i=1nai=m. I'm really only interested in the case where n=3; however, a generalized result would be nice. Any help is much appreciated.
