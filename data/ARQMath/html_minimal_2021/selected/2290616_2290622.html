Let A=(|f(x)|>||f||∞, B=(|g(x)|>||g||∞). What can you say about μ⁢(A∪B)?

Let A:={x∈X:|f⁢(x)|≤∥f∥∞},B:={x∈X:|g⁢(x)|≤∥g∥∞}. Since μ⁢(X∖A)=μ⁢(X∖B)=0, you have that μ⁢(X∖(A∪B))=0. Hence |f⁢(x)⁢g⁢(x)|≤∥f∥∞⁢∥g∥∞  ∀x∈A∪B, i.e. μ-a.e.

By definition, this fact implies that ∥f⁢g∥∞≤∥f∥∞⁢∥g∥∞.
