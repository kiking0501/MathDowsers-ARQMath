that's a start. 122=22*22*32=24*32. And 15=3*5 so 122*15=24*32*3*5=24*33*5. Basically the first step is to rewrite *everything* as its *unique* prime factorization. 24*34*25*7=24*34*52*7 and 2*122*15=2*24*32*3*5=24*33*5. Then... you know what to do.

Yes, that is precisely what you should be doing.

In short, when you want to find the gcd of two numbers directly, you must first represent each one as a product of prime powers, before matching powers and primes.

So in our case, we can write: 24×34×\color⁢b⁢l⁢u⁢e⁢25×7=24×34×\color⁢b⁢l⁢u⁢e⁢52×7⁢2×\color⁢r⁢e⁢d⁢122×\color⁢g⁢r⁢e⁢e⁢n⁢15=2×\color⁢r⁢e⁢d⁢22×22×32×\color⁢g⁢r⁢e⁢e⁢n⁢3×5=25×33×5

where, I highlight by color the terms that are expanded on both the LHS and RHS.

Now, we are permitted to compare powers directly. This gives us the answer as 24×33×5.

Nicely explained and nice use of colors. The OP should note when you express a number and a product of prime powers, there will always be *exactly* one unique way to do it. So doing this will always be a failsafe that will work.
