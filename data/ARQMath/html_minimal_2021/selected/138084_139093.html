following Riemann's trick, here is what i did :

I⁢(s)=-s⁢∮cEs⁢((-x)s)-1x⁢ex⁢(ex-1)⁢𝑑x

I⁢(-s)=s⁢∮cE-s⁢((-x)-s)-1x⁢ex⁢(ex-1)⁢𝑑x=-s⁢∮cEs⁢((-x)s)x⁢ex⁢(ex-1)⁢𝑑x

I⁢(s)-I⁢(-s)=s⁢∮cd⁢xx⁢ex⁢(ex-1)=s⁢∮c(-x)-1⁢e-x⁢𝑑x-s⁢∮c(-x)-1⁢d⁢xex-1

∮c(-x)-1⁢d⁢xex-1=limz→0⁡∮c(-x)z-1⁢d⁢xex-1=-2⁢i⁢limz→0⁡sin⁡(π⁢z)⁢Γ⁢(z)⁢ζ⁢(z)=i⁢π

I⁢(s)=-s⁢∮cEs⁢((-x)s)-1x⁢ex⁢(ex-1)⁢𝑑x=-s⁢∑n=1∞1Γ⁢(1+n⁢s)⁢∮c(-x)s⁢k-1⁢d⁢xex⁢(ex-1) =s⁢∑n=1∞2⁢i⁢sin⁡(k⁢π⁢s)⁢Γ⁢(k⁢s)Γ⁢(1+n⁢s)⁢(ζ⁢(k⁢s)-1)=2⁢i⁢∑n=1∞sin⁡(k⁢π⁢s)⁢ζ⁢(k⁢s)-1k

A⁢(s)⁢k⁢(s)-A⁢(-s)⁢k⁢(-s)=-32⁢π⁢s and the problem becomes proving the existence of A⁢(s) for all s, and of course, finding it !!

Asymptotic Expansion (direct method)

We want to rewrite following variant of I⁢(s) as a Maclaurin series P⁢(s) : J⁢(s):=∑k=1∞ζ⁢(s⁢k)-1-1s⁢k-1k-ln⁡(s)2

Let's split the -1 at the numerator in two -12 terms to study the asymptotic behavior of this series (and obtain convergence of the individual 'power log integrals' we will see later...) : J⁢(s)=limN→∞⁡JN⁢(s) with JN⁢(s)=∑k=1Nζ⁢(s⁢k)-12-1s⁢k-1k-12⁢∑k=1N1k-ln⁡(s)2

Let's first show that limN→∞⁡JN⁢(s) exists for any s>0 such that 1s∉ℕ (so that all the terms of the sum are defined).

Since ∑k=1N1k=Hn=γ+ln⁡(N+12)+O⁡(1N) we have 12⁢∑k=1N1k+ln⁡(s)2=γ2+12⁢ln⁡(s⁢(N+12))+O⁡(1N)=γ2+ln⁡(s⁢N)2+O⁡(1N)

For s⁢N≫1 we have (setting x:=s⁢k) : s⁢∑k=NMζ⁢(s⁢k)-12-1s⁢k-1s⁢k∼∫s⁢Ns⁢Mζ⁢(x)-12-1x-1x⁢𝑑x
the error is of order 1N because of the HM-HN terms and because ζ⁢(x)-1∼2-x for x≫1.

A precise evaluation of the last integral is : [32⁢ln⁡(x)-ln⁡(x-1)+∑k>1Ei⁡(-x⁢ln⁡(k))]s⁢Ns⁢M (with Ei the exponential integral and Ei⁡(-u)∼e-u-u for u≫1) but this isn't needed since the integral is clearly equivalent to 12⁢[ln⁡(x)]s⁢Ns⁢M with again an error of order 1N for s⁢N≫1.

Both ln⁡(s⁢N)2 terms cancel in JN⁢(s) for large s⁢N so that the limit J⁢(s) exists for s>0 (for 1s∈ℕ J⁢(s) may be defined as a limit since we removed the poles).

Let's use the Euler Maclaurin asymptotic formula with f⁢(k)=ζ⁢(s⁢k)-12-1s⁢k-1k to expand directly JN⁢(s) : ∑k=0Nf⁢(k)∼∫0Nf⁢(x)⁢𝑑x+f⁢(0)+f⁢(N)2+∑k=1∞B2⁢k(2⁢k)!⁢(f(2⁢k-1)⁢(N)-f(2⁢k-1)⁢(0)) becomes : s⁢∑k=0Nζ⁢(s⁢k)-12-1s⁢k-1s⁢k∼∫0s⁢Nζ⁢(x)-12-1x-1x⁢𝑑x+f⁢(0)+f⁢(N)2+∑k=1∞B2⁢k(2⁢k)!⁢(f(2⁢k-1)⁢(N)-f(2⁢k-1)⁢(0))

Note that we started at k=0 with f⁢(0)=limk→0⁡s⁢ζ⁢(s⁢k)-12-1s⁢k-1s⁢k=(1+ζ′⁢(0))⁢s
and that, for any n∈ℕ*, we have :
f(n-1)⁢(0)=limN→0⁡s⁢(dd⁢N)n-1⁢(ζ⁢(s⁢N)-12-1s⁢N-1s⁢N)=limN→0⁡s⁢(s⋅dd⁢(s⁢N))n-1⁢(⋯)=limx→0⁡sn⁢(ζ⁢(x)-12-1x-1x)(n-1)=sn⁢limx→0⁡(ζ⁢(x)-1x-1)(n)n

or simply f(n-1)⁢(0)=n!+ζ(n)⁢(0)n⁢sn for n>0

For n≥0 and N≫1 we may show that f(n)⁢(N)∼(-1)n⁢n!2⁢Nn+1 so that the Euler Maclaurin asymptotic formula becomes (after removing HN+ln⁡(s)2 on both sides to get JN⁢(s) at the left) :

JN⁢(s)=s⁢∑k=0Nζ⁢(s⁢k)-1-1s⁢k-1s⁢k-ln⁡(s)∼∫0s⁢Nζ⁢(x)-12-1x-1x⁢𝑑u-γ2-ln⁡(s⁢N)2+O⁡(1N)+f⁢(0)2-∑k=1∞B2⁢k(2⁢k)!⁢f(2⁢k-1)⁢(0)

At this point let's rewrite ln⁡(s⁢N)=∫0s⁢N1x+1⁢𝑑u+O⁡(1N) and go at the limit N→∞ : J⁢(s)∼-γ2+∫0∞ζ⁢(x)-12-1x-1x-12⁢(x+1)⁢d⁢x+f⁢(0)2-∑k=1∞B2⁢k(2⁢k)!⁢f(2⁢k-1)⁢(0)

We recognize a Maclaurin series in s : J⁢(s)∼K0-K1⁢s2-∑k=1∞K2⁢k⁢B2⁢k(2⁢k)!⁢s2⁢k

with the constant term given by K0:=-γ2+∫0∞ζ⁢(x)-1x-1-12x-12⁢(1+x)⁢d⁢x

(the numerical value is K0=-0.95973512990⁢⋯ as wished ; I don't know a closed form...)
