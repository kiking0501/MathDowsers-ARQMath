This probably isn't a complete answer, because I'm no expert on ϑ functions, but from what I do know, they satisfy a couple of properties that are kind of reminiscent of the periodicity of exponential functions.

There seem to be a lot of ϑ functions on that page, but the definition I know is that ϑ⁢(z,τ)=∑n=-∞∞eπ⁢i⁢n2⁢τ+2⁢π⁢i⁢n⁢z

So these gadgets are defined in terms of ordinary exponential functions, and have some periodicity properties.

We have

ϑ⁢(z+1,τ)=ϑ⁢(z,τ) since increasing z by 1 just spits out a e2⁢π⁢i=1 in every summand, and we also have for integer α,β

ϑ⁢(z+α+β⁢τ,τ)=ϑ⁢(z,τ)⁢e-π⁢i⁢β2⁢τ-2⁢π⁢i⁢β⁢z

which is a sort-of quasi-periodicity.
