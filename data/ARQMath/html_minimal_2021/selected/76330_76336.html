Noticing that n1n>1 for all n, it all comes down to showing that for any ϵ>0, there is a n such that (1+ϵ)≥n1n, or by rearranging, that

(1+ϵ)n≥n

Now, let's first of all choose an m such that (1+ϵ)m is some number bigger than 2, let's say the smallest number greater than 3 that you can get. From here, swap m for 2⁢m. This will make the left side a little over 3 times larger, and the right side 2 times larger. The next doubling will still double the right side, but the left side will increase roughly 9-fold. Repeating, we can easily see that the left side will at some point overtake the right side, and we have our n
