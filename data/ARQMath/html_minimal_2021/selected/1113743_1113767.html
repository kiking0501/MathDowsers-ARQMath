Could you be more specific? What exactly confuses you? (If you look at CRT can see how the isomorphism is defined. In our case the invertibles are (±1,±1). Two of these, (1,1), resp. (-1,-1) correspond to 1 and -1 in the factor ring. Now let's consider (-1,1): this comes from a polynomial of degree one (the elements of ℤ⁢[x]/(x2-x) are residue classes of such polynomials) a⁢x+b such that for x=1 (or modulo x-1 if you prefer) we get -1 and for x=0 (or modulo x) we get 1, so b=1 and a=2, that is, 2⁢x+1.)
"why those equations apply, but how do they lead to the answer?": in the factor ring ℤ⁢[x]/(x2-x) we have x¯2-x¯=0 (here x¯ denotes the residue class of x modulo the ideal (x2-x)), so 4⁢x¯2-4⁢x¯=0. From this we get (2⁢x¯-1)2=1, so 2⁢x¯-1 is invertible.

ℂU=ℂ×≃(ℝ/ℤ,+)×(ℝ>0,⋅) by using the isomorphism you suggested. Moreover, (ℝ>0,⋅)≃(ℝ,+) by using logarithm.

ℤ⁢[x]/(x2-x)≃ℤ⁢[x]/(x-1)×ℤ⁢[x]/(x) by CRT. Moreover, ℤ⁢[x]/(x-1)≃ℤ⁢[x]/(x)≃ℤ, so (ℤ⁢[x]/(x2-x))U≃ℤU×ℤU.
