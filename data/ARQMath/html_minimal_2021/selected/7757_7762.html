Take the binomial expansion of (1+x)n. Differentiate both sides with respect to x, then substitute x=1 and that will give you the identity.

@user193203821309 The point is to note that n⁢2n-1 (the desired quantity) is equal to n⁢(1+x)n-1 when x=1, and that this is in turn equal to the derivative of (1+x)n evaluated at x=1. So you substitute x=1 because that's how you get the 2.
