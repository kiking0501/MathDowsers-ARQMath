This particular problem is discussed in detail in Chapter 7 of Rational Points on Elliptic Curves by Silverman and Tate.

Let p be a prime number. Let Mp denote the number of (projective) solutions to x3+y3≡z3⁢(mod ⁢p) in ℤ/p⁢ℤ. Gauss proved that if p≡1⁢(mod ⁢3), then there are integers A, and B such that 4⁢p=A2+27⁢B2. Up to sign, A and B are unique. Furthermore, if we fix the sign of A to be positive, then in fact Mp=p+1+A.

So indeed, the theorem tells us exactly the number of solutions in ℤ/p⁢ℤ for this elliptic curve! I find this result striking and beautiful.

The proof given in the book is somewhat long, and uses clever manipulation of Gauss sums.

Remark. Last line of Jyrki's comment relates to quite an interesting result called Hasse's bound. Note that in the above case, this bound is verified: indeed, 4⁢p=A2+27⁢B2 implies A2≤4⁢p, giving us A≤2⁢p. I highly recommend the above book by Silverman and Tate for accessible introduction to this topic and beyond.
