Now, given a specific x with x1=sin⁡x and xn+1=sin⁡xn it is a result of Jean Ecalle at Orsay that we may take α⁢(x)=limn→∞⁡3xn2+6⁢log⁡xn5+79⁢xn21050+29⁢xn42625-n.

Note that α actually is defined on 0<x<π with α⁢(π-x)=α⁢(x), but the symmetry also means that the inverse function returns to the interval 0<x≤π2. You can see how this cannot be extended around the origin as a meromorphic function because of the evident logarithm term.

Before going on, the limit technique in the previous paragraph is given in pages 346-353 of Iterative Functional Equations by Marek Kuczma, Bogdan Choczewski, and Roman Ger. The solution is specifically Theorem 8.5.8 of subsection 8.5D, bottom of page 351 to top of page 353. Subsection 8.5A, pages 346-347, about Julia's equation, is part of the development.

We define ( at least for 0<x≤π2) f⁢(x)=α-1⁢(12+α⁢(x)) That's the short version. In Milnor's book, α is called a Fatou coordinate.
