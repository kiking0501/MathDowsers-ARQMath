This is the Euclidean algorithm. The essential idea is that given n1>n2 then n2,modulo⁢(n1,n2) have the same cgd as n1,n2. Your code gives a sequence of pairs of natural numbers with the same gcd the larger decreasing, which eventually terminates ..

Let d=gcd⁡(m,n) with m>n. By the Euclidean division theorem, you can write m=q⁢n+r, with r<n. Now, since d|m and d|n then d|r=m-q⁢n, so d is a common divisor of n and r, but not necessarily the greatest one. Asume that there is D≥d dividing n and r. Then it would have to also divide m=q⁢n+r, and then it would divide gcd⁡(m⁢n)=d. So, D|d and since it was assumed that D≥d, then D=d. Therefore, gcd⁡(m,n)=gcd⁡(n,r) (in your notation, r = m % n).
