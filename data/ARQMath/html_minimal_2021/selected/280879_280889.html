Hypercube problem

B is an n-dimensional hypercube, considered as undirected graph. Let A be a subset of the vertices of B such that |A|⁢\gt⁢2n-1.

Let H is a subgraph of B induced by A. Prove that H has at least n edges.

Any help, would be greatly appreciated.

Basically, the original problem in Ramsey theory says that if you have a large enough complete graph and color its edges by n colors, you are destined to find a single-colored complete subgraph of a given size in the graph, for every such size. Namely, every edge-2-colored K6 contains a single-colored K3 (triangle).

Nor can I, but it does seem similar. Namely, see the [Hales-Jewett theorem](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hales-Jewett_theorem).

Temporarily consider one of the n coordinate axes. Your hypercube has 2n-1 edges parallel to that axis. Since B has more than 2n-1 vertices, it must have two on the same one of those 2n-1 edges. So H has an edge parallel to the coordinate axis under consideration. Apply this to all n of the axes.
