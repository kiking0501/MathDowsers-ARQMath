The prime ideals in a PID are precisely the principal ideals of the form (pi), where pi is an irreducible element. If an element x is contained inside infinitely many prime ideals, then it has infinitely many irreducible divisors. Since every PID is a UFD, this is impossible, unless x=0.

Take R=ℂ⁢[x,y], polynomial ring in two variables. Consider maximal ideals Pa=(x-a,y) where a∈ℂ. Then ∩aPa=(y)≠0.
