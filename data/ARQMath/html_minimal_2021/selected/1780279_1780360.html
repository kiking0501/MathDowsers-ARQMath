As you mentioned in your comment, and as others have pointed out, you know that the Taylor series about 0, or the Maclaurin series for a function f⁢(x), is defined:

f⁢(x)=∑n=0∞xn⋅f(n)⁢(0)n!

Where f(n)⁢(0) represents the nth derivative of f evaluated at 0.

You did right in noticing that the nth derivative of f⁢(x)=e2⁢x is:

f(n)⁢(x)=2n⁢e2⁢x

Now, notice, as you let x=0, the nth derivative evaluated at 0 is:

f(n)⁢(0)=2n

Now, using the definition you provided for the Maclaurin series, we have that:

f⁢(x)=e2⁢x=∑n=0∞2n⁢xnn!
