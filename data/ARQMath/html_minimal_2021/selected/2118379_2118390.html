Given that the sum is 7, the possibilities are:

(1,6),(2,5),(3,4),(4,3),(5,2),(6,1).

Out of theses, only (3,4) and (4,3) satisfy the condition.

Hence the desired answer is 13.

For this kind of simple case, enumerating the possibilities is often the fastest solution.
This works well since we can think about it as shrinking the sample space to the set of events whose sum equals 7 & then picking two events that contain 4 i.e. (4,3) and (3,4)
