(a,b)∈R does *not* mean that a and b are in R, but that the *ordered pair* of a and b is in R. To say that a and b are in R, you'd omit the parentheses.

A relation R on a set S is just a subset of S×S. and we say a R b in other words a and b are R related. for example I give a relation R on ℤ such that a⁢R⁢b⇔5|(a-b)

That relation partitions integers into classes modulo 5, though it is an example of a particularly *nice-behaving* relation, i.e. it's an equivalence relation.
