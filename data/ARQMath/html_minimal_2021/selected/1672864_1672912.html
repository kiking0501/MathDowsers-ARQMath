Graphs of functions with arcsin and arccos

I know that to graph arcsin⁡x, I need to only take into account its principal values, that is its image is restricted to [-π2,π2]. Same with arccos⁡x, its image is restricted to [0,π].

Now, say I want to graph a function like f⁢(x)=12⁢arcsin⁡(4⁢(x-1))+π2, what is the correct graph? Can I choose where it is restricted? When I use a graphing software it's restricted to [π4,3⁢π4], but can I restrict the image to [-π4,π4] or any other interval that fulfills the definition of function?

trigonometry graphing-functions

No. The arccos and arcsin functions are defined by convention, just as you describe them in the first paragraph. If you plug the arcsin function with that definition into your f⁢(x), the image lies in [π4,3⁢π4]. That's not an arbitrary feature of your graphics software; it's a necessarily result of the conventional definition. It wouldn't make much sense to define arcsin differently in different contexts.
