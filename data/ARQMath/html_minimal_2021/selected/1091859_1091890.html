Orientation of multiplying integrals

Consider,

I=∫-∞∞e-x2⁢𝑑x

The trick is to multiply by I again to get I2 But they often write:

I2=∫-∞∞∫-∞∞e-x2-y2⁢𝑑y⁢𝑑x

The issue is orientation.

I is an integral, which is orientated in the x⁢y plane, and x is the independent variable, y is the dependent variable.

When you consider the other one with e-y2, then y is the independent variable and x is the dependent variable.
