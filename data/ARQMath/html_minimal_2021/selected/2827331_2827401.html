Let Δ⁢f⁢(x)=f⁢(x+1)-f⁢(x), D⁢f⁢(x)=f′⁢(x). Since f⁢(x+1)-f⁢(x)=f′⁢(x)+f′′⁢(x)2!+f′′′⁢(x)3!+⋯, we have Δ=D+D22!+D33!+⋯.

Hence Δn⁢f⁢(x)=(D+D22!+D33!+⋯)n⁢f⁢(x).

Applying successively the finite difference operator, we obtain Δn⁢f⁢(x)=∑r=0n(-1)r⁢(nr)⁢f⁢(x+n-r).

If f⁢(x)=xn then Dn⁢f⁢(x)=n!, and Dm⁢f⁢(x)=0 if m>n. Thus combining \eqref{1} and \eqref{2} we get n!=∑r=0n(-1)r⁢(nr)⁢(x+n-r)n. The desired result follows by setting x=0. Note that this works in greater generality.
