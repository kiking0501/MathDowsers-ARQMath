23 does not equal 0.66, although they are approximate. You've been simplifying the wrong number! You need to convert the "mixed fraction" 99⁤23 to the "improper fraction" pq for some whole numbers p and q.

@njzk2 bad notation: "mixed fractions" are evil.
In general, x⁢pq is equals to (x*q)+pq. Therefore, x⁢pq% equals (x*q)+pq*100.

@haccks If x, p, and q are literal numbers, then x⁢pq is usually interpreted to mean x+pq. But if all of them are variables, then x⁢pq is usually interpreted to mean x⋅pq. For example, 3⁤12=72, but if x=3, p=1, and q=2, then I would interpret x⁢pq=32.
