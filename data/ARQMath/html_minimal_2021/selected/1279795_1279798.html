A field E over F is a splitting field if it is the smallest field extension of F containing all the roots of some polynomial p∈F⁢[X] (defined regardless of the characteristic of F). Let α1,…,αn be the roots of p in an algebraic closure. Then the splitting field of p over F is E=F⁢(α1,…,αn) (smallest field containing αi). This is a finite extension of F since each αi is algebraic over F (and any extension of F). You can form a (finite) tower of finite extensions: F⊂F⁢(α1)⊂F⁢(α1,α2)⊂…⊂E.

Compare this with a normal extension, which may be infinite. One characterization of a normal extension E over F is that E is the splitting field for a (possibly infinite) family of polynomials in F⁢[X].
