Condition on p for convergence of ∑1n⁢(log⁡(n))p

For what values of p is the series ∑1n⁢(log⁡(n))p divergent and for what values it is convergent?

Divergent for p≤1, convergent for p⁢\gt⁢1. The Integral Test will work nicely. So will the likely less familiar Cauchy Condensation Test.

This is a Bertrand's series. It converges for any real exponent p>1, and diverges for p≤1. It trivially diverges if p≤0. If 0<p≤1, it is enough to prove it diverges for p=1. As the function 1x⁢ln⁡x is continuous, decreasing for x>1e, we may apply Cauchy's integral test: ∫2+∞d⁢xx⁢ln⁡x=lnlnx|2+∞diverges, hence the series ∑n=2∞1n⁢ln⁡n diverges.
