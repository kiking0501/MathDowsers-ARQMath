Your series can be rewritten as \begin{align} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n! 2^n} &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{ \left(\frac{1}{2} \right)^n }{n!} \\ &= \left[ \frac{ \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^0 }{0!} \color{red}{- \frac{ \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^0 }{0!}}\right] + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{ \left(\frac{1}{2} \right)^n }{n!} && (\text{add zero}) \\ &= \color{red}{-1} + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{ \left(\frac{1}{2} \right)^n }{n!} \\ &= -1 + \mathrm{e}^{\frac{1}{2}} && (\text{use the well known result}) \\ &= \sqrt{\mathrm{e}} -1. \end{align}
