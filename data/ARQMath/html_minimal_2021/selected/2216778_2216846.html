sum of squares of diagonal binomial coefficients.

My question is if there exists a way to evaluate the sum

(ss)2+(s+1s)2+…⁢(s+rs)2.

In other words, it's the sum of the squares of the first r binomial coefficients on the s-th right-to-left diagonal of Pascal's triangle. Moreover, is it true that the previous sum is Os⁢(rs)?

I don't think there's a nice closed form.

For the second question, perhaps you mean O⁢(r2⁢s+1), not O⁢(rs). In fact, for each fixed positive integer s, (s+xs) is a polynomial in x of degree s, so (s+xs)2 is a polynomial in x of degree 2⁢s, and the partial sum (s+0s)2+(s+1s)2+…⁢(s+rs)2 is a polynomial in r of degree 2⁢s+1.
