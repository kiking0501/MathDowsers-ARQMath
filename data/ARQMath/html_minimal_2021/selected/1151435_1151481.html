Here's an idea that doesn't "use" differential equations:

Assume f≥0 for x≥0 and consider the interval (0,ϵ), ϵ<1 where 0≤f<1/2, which exists by continuity of f.

First, pick an x0∈(0,ϵ). By the mean value theorem, f⁢(x0)=f⁢(x0)-f⁢(0)=x0⁢f′⁢(x1)≤f⁢(x1)2 for some 0<x1<x0. Define x2 and in general xn in a similar manner so that f⁢(xn)≤f⁢(xn+1)2. Then f⁢(x0)≤f⁢(xn)2n≤(1/2)2n. Taking the limit we get f⁢(x0)=0.

Now fill in the gaps and deal with the other cases (or combine them) and figure out a way to show f=0 beyond (0,ϵ). If you use this idea in your homework just write down you got the idea here, no biggie.
