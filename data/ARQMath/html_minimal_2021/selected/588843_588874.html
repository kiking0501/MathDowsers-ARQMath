You need to be more careful in your use of notation. An open cover of a set E is a collection of open sets; On=(0,1+1n) is just one open set, not a collection of sets, so it cannot possibly be a cover.

Here is what I think you were trying to say:

Let E=(0,1]. A family {Oa:a∈A} is an open cover of E if each of the sets Oa is open, and E⊆⋃a∈AOa. For n≥1 let On=(0,1+1n); is {On:n≥1} an open cover of E? Is E compact?

The answer to the first question is yes: O1=(0,2), and E=(0,1]⊆(0,2), so E⊆O1⊆⋃n≥1On.

In fact, E⊆On for every n≥1.

The answer to the second question is no; see Luis’s answer for a good hint.
