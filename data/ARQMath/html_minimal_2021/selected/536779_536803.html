Yes, you can just treat everything in the expression asacomplex number. The result is that you will compute what is called the "principal value" of the roots in question. For real square roots, the principal square root is the positive one. For complex square roots, the issue is more complicated. The key issue in the question in your comment is that you can just treat everything in the expression as if it is a complex number - you never need to "convert back" at the end.
