Be careful, the inequality inf⁡{d⁢(α,a):a∈A}-inf⁡{d⁢(x,a):a∈A}≤inf⁡{d⁢(α,a)-d⁢(x,a):a∈A} need not hold. Let for example α=0,x=2,A={1,2.5}. Then the left side of the equation is 0.5 while the right side is 0.

We have d⁢(x,A)≤d⁢(x,y)+d⁢(y,A). So |d⁢(x,A)-d⁢(y,A)|≤d⁢(x,y). It is nonexpansive and therefore Lipschitz continuous.
