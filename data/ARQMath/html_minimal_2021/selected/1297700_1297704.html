Measure Theory (Defining Measureability)

just a very basic measure theory question from the book by Bartle.

Let (a) be the statement "For every α∈ℝ, the set Aα={x∈X:f⁢(x)>α} belongs to X." (X is a measurable space)

It is claimed that if (a) holds, then Aα-1/n belongs to X for each n.

Why is this so?

measure-theory
Because α-1/n∈ℝ...

As Did remarks, α-1/n∈ℝ, so Aα-1/n={x∈X:f⁢(x)>α-1/n}∈X. Everything is well-defined. Kind of strange that they make this into an explicit claim.
