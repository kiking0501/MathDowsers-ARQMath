R is a relation on X, i.e. R⊆X×X.

Also ∼ is a relation on X, i.e. ∼⊆X×X, defined by :

x∼y iff (x,y)∈R and (y,x)∈R.

We have to show that ∼ is :

reflexive, symmetric and transitive.

1) Reflexive :

Consider x∈X. We have that (x,x)∈R, because R is reflexive; thus, trivially : (x,x)∈R and (x,x)∈R, i.e. x∼x.

2) Symmetric :

Consider x,y∈X such that x∼y. This means, by definition of ∼ : (x,y)∈R and (y,x)∈R.

But trivially, also : (y,x)∈R and (x,y)∈R, i.e. y∼x, and thus we have that : if x∼y, then y∼x, that is the symmetry of ∼ .
