How to Use

This demo facillitates users to view the result of the MathCQA task, in particular, the result from the MathDowsers' team. The included features are:

  • [Filter & Select Question]
  • [View MathDowsers' Answers]
  • [View Human Judgement of the Answers]
  • [Filter Relevant Answers]
  • [Input Custom Runs]

  • [Filter & Select Question]

    At first, select a question from the . The available questions are query topics from the ARQMath Lab 2020 and 2021, selected from real-life questions in the Math StackExchange forum in 2019 and 2020. After selecting a question, one might then click on the top-right button "Refresh & View" button to navigate to the panel (note that the panel is only accessible afterwards).

    • Back to Top
  • [View MathDowsers' Runs]

    From the panel, users might then explore different lists of potential answers and their accessed relevance with respect to the selected question. By default, the displayed lists of answers (or runs) are retrieved and ranked by MathDowsers using forum posts in 2010 to 2018 from the Math Stack Exchange corpus. The ARQMath Working Paper 2021 has a full documentation on the runs.

    • Back to Top
  • [View Human Judgement of the Answers]

    The displayed lists of answers were collected automatically by the MathDowsers' systems, that is, without human intervention. To view the relevance judgement of those answers by the Lab assessors, turn on the "Show Human Relevance Judgement" button.

    • Back to Top
  • [Filter Relevant Answers]

    To filter answers with a specific relevance judgement, click on the corresponding label on the top. It might be used together with the "View in Separate Windows" button to view target answers at once.

    • Back to Top
  • [Input Custom Runs]

    Interested users might input custom runs by first selecting "custom" from the dropdown menu, then following the instructions to input a custom run into the provided box. Corresponding answers and their details will show up after clicking on the "Show Answer Ranking" button.
